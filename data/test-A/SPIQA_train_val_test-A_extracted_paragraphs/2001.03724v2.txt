In this paper, we propose a novel algorithm called Stochastic Recursive gradiEnt Descent Ascent (SREDA) for stochastic nonconvex-strongly-concave minimax problems.Unlike SGDmax and SGDA, which only iterate with current stochastic gradients, our SREDA updates the estimator recursively and reduces its variance.

The variance reduction techniques have been widely used in convex and nonconvex minimizationproblems [52, 20, 30, 39, 11, 1, 3, 31, 38, 2, 22, 4, 15, 36, 23, 54, 45] and convex-concave saddle point problems [35, 13, 12, 28, 9].However, the nonconvex-strongly-concave minimax problems have two variables 𝐱𝐱{\bf{x}} and 𝐲𝐲{\bf{y}} and their roles in the objective function are quite different. In this paper, we studied stochastic nonconvex-strongly-concave minimax problems.We proposed a novel algorithm called Stochastic Recursive gradiEnt Descent Ascent (SREDA).The algorithm employs variance reduction to solve minimax problems.Based on the appropriate choice of the parameters,we prove SREDA finds an 𝒪​(ε)𝒪𝜀{\mathcal{O}}({\varepsilon})-stationary point of ΦΦ\Phi with astochastic gradient complexity of 𝒪​(κ3​ε−3)𝒪superscript𝜅3superscript𝜀3{\mathcal{O}}(\kappa^{3}\varepsilon^{-3}). This result is better thanstate-of-the-art algorithms and optimal in its dependency on ε𝜀{\varepsilon}.We can also apply SREDA to the finite-sum case, and show that it performs well when n𝑛n is larger than κ2superscript𝜅2\kappa^{2}.

There are still some open problems left.The complexity of SREDA is optimal with respect to ε𝜀{\varepsilon}, but weatherit is optimal with respect to κ𝜅\kappa is unknown.It is also possible to employ SREDA to reduce the complexity ofstochastic nonconvex-concave minimax problems without the strongly-concave assumption.
