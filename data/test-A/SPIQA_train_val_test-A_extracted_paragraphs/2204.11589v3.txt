Ads and organic items are mixed together and displayed to users in e-commerce feed nowadays (Yanet al., 2020; Ghose and Yang, 2009; Liet al., 2020). How to allocate limited ads slots reasonably and effectively to maximize platform revenue has attracted growing attention (Wanget al., 2011; Mehta, 2013; Zhanget al., 2018). Several recent strategies for ads allocation model the problem as Markov Decision Process (MDP) (Suttonet al., 1998) and solve it using reinforcement learning (RL) (Zhanget al., 2018; Liao et al., 2021; Zhao et al., 2021; Fenget al., 2018; Zhaoet al., 2020). For instance,Xieet al. (2021) propose a hierarchical RL-based framework to first decide the type of the item to present and then determine the specific item for each slot.Liao et al. (2021) propose CrossDQN which takes the crossed items according to the action as input and allocates the slots in one screen at a time.

However, these excellent RL-based algorithms face one major challenge when applied in platforms (e.g., e-commerce platforms, video platforms, food delivery platforms and so on). As shown in Figure 1, the Meituan food delivery platform has multiple entrances for different categories (e.g., Homepage, Food, Desert and so on), facilitating users to access different categories of content. Some entrances have few visits, resulting in a low data coverage. This further makes it difficult for the agent to learn.Therefore, it is desirable for a learning algorithm to transfer samples and leverage knowledge acquired in data-rich entrance to improve performance of the agent for ads allocation in data-poor entrance.Motivated by this phenomenon, we incorporate Transfer learning (TL) technology into RL to solve this problem.

TL in RL has been rarely used for ads allocation. Meanwhile, TL in RL for other scenarios (Zhu et al., 2020; Giannopoulos et al., 2021; Taoet al., 2021; Liuet al., 2019; Tirinzoni et al., 2018). For instance, Tirinzoni et al. (2018) present a algorithm called IWFQI for transferring samples in batch RL that uses importance weighting to automatically account for the difference in source and target distributions. But IWFQI does not fully exploit possible similarities between tasks. Liuet al. (2019) quantify the environmental dynamics of an MDP by the N-step return (NSR) values and present a knowledge transfer method called NSR-based value function transfer. However, they ignore the uncertainty of the NSR model itself when transferring, since the rewards can induce randomness in observed long-term return (Dabneyet al., 2018).

To this end, we present Similarity-based Hybrid Transfer for Ads Allocation (SHTAA)111The code and data example are publicly accessible at https://github.com/princewen/SHTAA, which effectively transfers samples and knowledge from data-rich entrance to agent trained from other data-poor entrance. Specifically, we quantify the environmental dynamics of an MDP using distributional N-step return (NSR) value, which is predicted by pre-trained NSR model. And we define a concept of uncertainty-aware similarity of MDP based on it.Then we design a hybrid transfer framework, which consists of instance transfer and strategy transfer, based on the similarity value. The framework aims to effectively transfer the samples and knowledge from data-rich source task to data-poor target task.We conduct extensive offline experiments and evaluate our approach on Meituan food delivery platform. The experimental results show that SHTAA can effectively improve the performance of the agent for ads allocation in data-poor entrance, and obtain significant improvements in platform revenue.

In this section, we will introduce SHTAA in detail.Two main ideas are: i) using predicted uncertain-aware NSR to measure MDP similarity, ii) proposing a hybrid transfer approach (consisting of instance transfer and strategy transfer) for selective transfer and avoidance of negative transfer.

We analyze the sensitivity of these two hyperparameters: N𝑁N and τ𝜏\tau. The optimal hyperparameter values are obtained by grid search and we have the following findings:i) N𝑁N is the step number of NSR. Due to the discount rate γ𝛾\gamma, the rewards sampled at larger time steps must contribute less to the NSR. Therefore, if an appropriate step number N𝑁N is chosen, the corresponding NSR may contain the most information about the local environmental dynamics. ii) τ𝜏\tau is the weight threshold for instance transfer. A smaller τ𝜏\tau would lead to more negative transfer while a larger τ𝜏\tau would lead to less efficient transfer learning.

We deploy the agent trained by SHTAA on Meituan food delivery platform’s data-poor entrance through online A/B test. The ads revenue and service fees increase by 3.72% and 3.31%, which demonstrates that our method greatly increases the platform revenue.

In this paper, we propose Similarity-based Hybrid Transfer for Ads Allocation (SHTAA) to effectively transfer the samples and knowledge from data-rich source entrance to other data-poor entrance.Both offline experiments and online A/B test have demonstrated the superior performance and efficiency of our method.
