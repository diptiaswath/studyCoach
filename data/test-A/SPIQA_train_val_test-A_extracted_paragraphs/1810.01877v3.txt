During the past decade, deep neural networks (DNNs) have demonstrated an amazing performance insolving many complex artificial intelligence tasks such as object recognition andidentification, text understanding and translation, question answering, and more [11]. The capacity of unregularized fully connected DNNs, as a function of the network size and depth, is fairly well understood [1, 4, 23].By bounding the L2subscript𝐿2L_{2} norm of the incoming weights of each unit, [22] is able to accelerate the convergence of stochastic gradient descent optimization across applications in supervised image recognition, generative modeling, and deep reinforcement learning. However, theoretical investigations on such networks are less explored in the literature, and a few exceptions are [4, 5, 10, 18, 19, 25]. There is a central question waiting for an answer: Can we bound the capacity of fully connected DNNs with bias neurons by weight normalization alone, which has the least dependence on the architecture?

In this paper, we focus on networks with rectified linear units (ReLU) and study a more general weight normalized deep neural network (WN-DNN),which includes all layer-wise Lp,qsubscript𝐿𝑝𝑞L_{p,q} weight normalizations. In addition, these networks have a bias neuron per hidden layer, while prior studies [4, 5, 10, 18, 19, 25] either exclude the bias neuron, or only include the bias neuron in the input layer, which differs from the practical application. We establish the upper bound on the Rademacher complexities of this family and study the theoretical properties of WN-DNNs in terms of the approximation error.

We first examine how the Lp,qsubscript𝐿𝑝𝑞L_{p,q} WN-DNN architecture influences their generalization properties. Specifically, for Lp,qsubscript𝐿𝑝𝑞L_{p,q} normalization where q≤p∗𝑞superscript𝑝q\leq p^{*} and 1/p+1/p∗=11𝑝1superscript𝑝11/p+1/p^{*}=1, we obtain a complexity bound that is independent of width and only has a square root dependence on the depth. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first theoretical result for the fully connected DNNs including a bias neuron for each hidden layer in terms of generalization. We will demonstrate later that it is nontrivial to extend the existing results to the DNNs with bias neurons. We also examine the approximation error of WN-DNNs. It is shown that the L1,∞subscript𝐿1L_{1,\infty} WN-DNN is able to approximate any Lipschitz continuous function arbitrarily well by increasing the norm of its output layer and growing its size. Early work on neural network approximation theory includes the universal approximation theorem [8, 13, 20], indicating that a fully connected network with a single hidden layer can approximate any continuous functions.More recent work expands the result of shallow networks to deep networks with an increased interest in the expressive power of deep networks especially for some families of "hard" functions [2, 9, 16, 21, 26, 27]. For instance, [26] shows that for any positive integer l𝑙l, there exist neural networks with Θ​(l3)Θsuperscript𝑙3\Theta(l^{3}) layers and Θ​(1)Θ1\Theta(1) nodes per layer, which can not be approximated by networks with Θ​(l)Θ𝑙\Theta(l) layers unless they possess Ω​(2l)Ωsuperscript2𝑙\Omega(2^{l}) nodes. These results on the other hand request for an artificial neural network of which the generalization bounds grow slowly with depth and even avoid explicit dependence on depth.
