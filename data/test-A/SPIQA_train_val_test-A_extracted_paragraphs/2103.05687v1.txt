Convolutional Networks (ConvNets) reach striking performance on semantic segmentationВ [1, 34], a dense visual recognition task that aims at transforming an image into its underlying semantic regions.Particularly, segmenting images of road scenes automatically is of interest as it lies vital groundwork for scene understanding in an autonomous driving environmentВ [26].Most segmentation algorithmsВ [8, 40, 73] are designed to work on pinhole-camera images whose Field of View (FoV) is rather narrow, capturing only a fraction of what is occurring on and aside the road.For a more holistic view on street-scenes, omnidirectional cameras are becoming ubiquitous in autonomous driving systemsВ [71], as their capability of all-around sensing, gives rise to comprehensive 360вҲҳ scene understanding.

Yet, rather than designing models based on the new imaging modality, predominantly, due to lack of sufficient labeled data, narrow FoV-trained models are applied to yield a segmentationВ [7, 15, 59].Brought about by the large mismatch in FoV and structural distribution between imaging domains, this practice causes significant performance degradation, even to a point of rendering the perception of surroundings completely unreliableВ [70].

Fig.В 1 shows the distribution of semantic classes along the viewing angle, where green-tinted regions indicate the section visible to a front-facing narrow FoV camera.It becomes apparent, that the positional priors in narrow- and omnidirectional images diverge severely, \eg, the road class occupies much of the center portion in narrow FoV images, which, in 360вҲҳ images, is far smaller at viewing angles perpendicular to the front-facing direction (180вҲҳ).These observations suggest distinct contextual priors between the imaging techniques, which provide critical cues for semantic segmentationВ [78, 87].Here, we address the challenging problem of leveraging inherent long-range contextual priors unique to omnidirectional images, that previous systems fail to harvestВ [69].We lift the widely used, but computationally expensive non-local operations based on pixel-to-pixel correlationsВ [17, 64] to the 360вҲҳ FoV scenario in a computationally efficient fashion, \ie, modeling correlations of each position to omni-range regions.

By design, non-local attentionВ [64] is meant to aggregate associations from highly correlated positions like regions of homogeneous classes, which often lie in the horizontal direction at a similar height, \eg, the sidewalks can be distributed across 360вҲҳ but principally in the lower part of street-scenes. Aside from considering the inherent properties of 360вҲҳ data in architecture design, training such models is not straightforward, as a lack of sufficient annotated panoramic images impedes optimization.The labeling process is extremely time- and labor-intensive, thus, only few surround-view datasetsВ [52, 63, 76] are publicly accessible and none capture as diverse scenes as contemporary pinhole datasetsВ [38, 58] do.This is why, to unlock the full potential of our novel ECANet architecture, we propose a multi-source omni-supervised learning regimen that intertwines training on unlabeled, full-sized panoramic images and densely labeled pinhole images.By means of data distillationВ [46] and our novel multi-source prediction fusion, we increase prediction certainty, ingrain long-range contextual priors while still benefiting from large-scale, narrow FoV segmentation datasets.

To facilitate progress in omnidirectional semantic image segmentation, we put forward the Wild PAnoramic Semantic Segmentation (WildPASS) dataset for evaluating models in unconstrained environments, featuring scenes collected from all around the globe, reflecting the perception challenge of autonomous driving in the real world.An exhaustive number of diverse experiments demonstrate the proposed ECANet model, learning- and fusion strategy make it possible to deploy highly efficient ConvNets for panoramic image segmentation, surpassing the state-of-the-art on the public PASSВ [69] and novel WildPASS datasets.On a glance, we deliver the following contributions:вҖўRethink omnidirectional semantic segmentation from the context-aware perspective and propose Efficient Concurrent Attention Networks, capturing inherent long-range dependencies across the 360вҲҳ.вҖўIntroduce multi-source omni-supervised learning, integrating unlabeled panoramic images into training, empowering models to learn rich contextual priors.вҖўPresent the diverse WildPASS dataset: Collected from 6 continents and 65 cities, enabling evaluation of panoramic semantic segmentation in the wild.вҖўOur methods surpass previous and set new state-of-the-art results on the PASS and WildPASS datasets.

Context-aware semantic segmentation.Semantic segmentation has progressed exponentiallysince the conception of fully convolutional networksВ [34]and the groundwork laid by early encoder-decoder architecturesВ [1, 39, 49].Building atop classification networksВ [18, 22], DeepLabВ [4, 5], PSPNetВ [91], RefineNetВ [31] and DenseASPPВ [73]leverage pre-trained models and achieve notable performance improvements.Yet, a lot of progress is driven by sub-modules like dilated convolutionsВ [81],large kernelsВ [43],pyramid-, strip- and atrous spatial pyramid poolingВ [4, 19, 91]for capturing structured context.

A second branch of work takes advantage of recent channel/spatial attention mechanismsВ [20, 21, 62, 65] to exploit global contextВ [29, 86], dimension-wise priorsВ [8, 72] and cross-modal featuresВ [55, 67, 88].More recently, inspired by non-local blocks in recognition tasksВ [64], DANetВ [17] and OCNetВ [83] adopt self-attentionВ [60] to capture either associations between any pair of pixels/channels or dense object context, respectively.Further, point-wise attention is adaptively learned inВ [92]. This trend prompts a variety of attention modulesВ [6, 24, 30, 32],as well asgraph-based modelsВ [77, 89]and factorized variantsВ [23, 51, 75].With the nature of dense prediction in segmentation, the computational cost induced by combinatorics of pixel-pairs becomes infeasible very fast, which is amplified when working on pixel-rich, extra-wide panoramic images.In order to reduce these pair-wise computations, CCNetВ [25] operates in criss-cross paths, Axial-DeepLabВ [61] propagates along height- and width-axis sequentially,whileВ [16, 53, 77, 94] act on key responding positions.Addressing the nature of omni-range contextual priors in panoramic images, we propose an efficient concurrent attention module.Unlike context-aware networksВ [19, 25] that only aggregate spatial dependency or refine pixel-level context, our attention concurrently highlights horizontally-driven dependencies and collects global contextual information for wide-FoV segmentation.While accuracy-oriented networks reach high performance,efficient networks like Fast-SCNNВ [45], CGNetВ [66] and ERFNetВ [48] aim to perform both swift and accurate segmentation.Further, a subset of compact ConvNetsВ [66, 70, 79] utilize attention mechanisms to efficiently aggregate context.However, these methods do not consider inherent properties of omnidirectional imagery, and thus suffer from large accuracy degradation on such data.Our solution to solve this issue extends the line of efficient ConvNets and consistently elevates their performance and reliability when operating on panoramic images.

Omnidirectional semantic segmentation.As systems progressed towards 360вҲҳ perception, early omnidirectional segmentation works were based on fisheye imagesВ [12, 74] or focused on indoor scenesВ [54, 56] with surround-view systems in street-scenes being largely based on a multitude of camerasВ [11, 37, 41, 47].Motivated by the prospect of attaining 360вҲҳ semantic perception based on a singular panoramic camera, recent works build semantic segmentation systems directly on top of this sensor-modalityВ [2, 68, 85].However, these methods rely on either expensive manually labeled or synthesized omnidirectional data, that does not capture the diversity nor the realism as seen in large-scale pinhole image collections with rich ontologiesВ [38, 58].With this sentiment in mind, we introduce the WildPASS dataset, comprising 360вҲҳ images from over 60 cities and multiple continents, encouraging a more realistic assessment of panoramic segmentation performance.Yang et al.presented a frameworkВ [69, 70] for re-using models trained on pinhole images via style transferВ [93] and separating the panorama into multiple partitions for predictions, each resembling a narrow FoV image.While quite accurate, running multiple forward passes suffers from high latencyВ [69] and disregards global context information within the panorama, as it is divided into patches.With our holistic view on panoramic image segmentation, we cut this computational burden by nearly a factor of four, extending the framework inВ [71] with a multi-source omni-supervised learning regimen that covers 360вҲҳ imagery via data distillationВ [46] as well as enjoying the advantages of large, readily available, labeled pinhole image datasets.Further, making best use of the multi-source setting, we integrate a prediction fusion scheme, improving semantic certainty and generalization, as indicated by performance measures on both: PASS and WildPASS datasets.

In the following, we consider the training of panoramic segmentation networks and propose a better suited regimen.

Multi-source omni-supervision.We extend the design ofВ [71] based on data distillationВ [46] and present a multi-source omni-supervised learning regimen.As shown in Fig.В 4, we train efficient networks on both labeled pinhole- and unlabeled panoramic images in a multi-source setup.This adds diversity of FoV while simultaneously preventing the model from overfitting to pinhole images.While different datasets have heterogeneous class definitions, therefore hindering direct training by merging sources, we argue that the relationships encoded in their similar label hierarchies positively affect the generalization of feature representations.For example, street-scene datasetsВ [9, 38, 58] have different definitions of road surfaces, but share similar class hierarchies that both comprise flat region and traffic object categories.As shown in Fig.В 4, our approach is designed to enable learning with unequal sources in spite of their class-label conflicts.Precisely, to address the heterogeneity in semantic spaces, we append multiple output-heads to the efficient learner, each of which is a fully convolutional module with an upsampling layer for prediction in different label spaces (semantic spaces 111 and 222 for two sources, as illustrated in Fig.В 4).

For unlabeled panoramas, we create annotations using a sophisticated architecture pre-trained on pinhole sets by means of the PASS pipelineВ [69], as such we leverage the correspondence of FoV between pinhole images and partitioned panoramas for accurate segmentation.The panorama is rotated along the horizontal direction based on its 360вҲҳ wrap-around structure, and the predictions on differently rotated transformations are ensembled, producing the annotation.While time-consuming due to the complex PASS pipeline and ensembling, the resulting fine-grained, high quality annotations are computed in advance and can be leveraged for data distillation (Fig.В 4).UnlikeВ [71] that uses a battery of single-city unlabeled images, we distill knowledge via diverse panoramas from all around the globe, which will unfold as crucial detail for attaining generalization to open omnidirectional domains.

To foster research andto facilitate credible numerical evaluation for panoramic scene segmentation, we introduce the Wild PAnoramic Semantic Segmentation (WildPASS) dataset.Unlike mainstream large-scale datasets like CityscapesВ [9] and BDDВ [80] that focus on urban scene understanding in Europe or North America, WildPASS embraces segmentation in the wild with images from all around the world based on Google Street View.As shown in Fig.В 5, it includes unstructured and adverse road scenes like nighttime, as well as imagestaken from different viewpoints to paint a comprehensive picture of real-world challenges for navigational perception systems.Overall, we gather 2500 panoramas in 65 cities from all continents Asia, Europe, Africa, Oceania, North and South America (excluding only Antarctica).Following PASSВ [69] and RainyNightВ [13], 500 panoramas from 25 cities are precisely annotated for the most critical navigation-related classes.We extend the semantics defined for panoramic segmentationВ [69] to 8 classes: Car, Road, Sidewalk, Crosswalk, Curb, Person, Truck and Bus.The remaining 2000 panoramas from 40 different cities are collected for omni-supervised learning.All images have a 360Г—вҲҳ{}^{\circ}\times70вҲҳ FoV at 2048Г—\times400.WildPASS thereby is the largest panoramic street-scene segmentation benchmark, exceeding other evaluation-oriented WildDash (211 annotations)В [84], ISSAFE (313 annotations)В [88] and PASS (400 annotations)В [69] datasets.

Our experiments are built on the Mapillary VistasВ [38] and IDD20KВ [58] pinhole datasets for a multi-source setting.These datasets are appealing as they feature diverse viewpoints and unstructured scenes inherent in real-world unconstrained surroundings.Both datasets contain 18000/2000 and 14027/2036 images for training/validation, respectively. FollowingВ [71], we use 25 classes from Vistas and the level-3 labels (26 classes) of IDD20K, 15 of which overlap.We use the 2000 unlabeled panoramas of WildPASS to create omnidirectional annotations with PSPNet50В [91] for data distillation.We test on public PASS (400 annular images) and WildPASS (500 panoramas) datasets and evaluate with the mean Intersection over Union (mIoU) metric.Facilitating fair comparisons with omnidirectional segmentation frameworksВ [69, 71], ECANet is implemented using the ERFNetВ [48] backbone pre-trained on ImageNetВ [50].We use N=4рқ‘Ғ4N=4 segments and a strip pooling of (H^,W^)=(2,16)^рқҗ»^рқ‘Ҡ216(\widehat{H},\widehat{W})=(2,16) in HSA and spatial pooling of (H^,W^)={(3,3),(4,4),(6,6)}^рқҗ»^рқ‘Ҡ334466(\widehat{H},\widehat{W})=\{(3,3),(4,4),(6,6)\} in PSA.ECANet is trained under Adam optimizationВ [28] with a Weight Decay of 2Г—\times10-4 and a Learning Rate of 1Г—\times10-4 that decreases exponentially over 200 epochs.
