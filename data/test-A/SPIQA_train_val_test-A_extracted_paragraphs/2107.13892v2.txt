Related work. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work in personalized federated learning where the aim is to learn quantized and personalized models potentially having different dimensions/structures for inference. Our work can be seen in the intersection of personalized federated learning and learning quantized models; we also employ knowledge distillation for collaboration. 

Personalized federated learning:Recent works adopted different approaches for learning personalized models:(i) Combine global and local models throughout the training [7, 26, 14];(ii) first learn a global model and then personalize it locally [9, 1];(iii) consider multiple global models to collaborate among only those clients that share similar personalized models [35, 26, 10, 32];(iv) augment the traditional FL objective via a penalty term that enables collaboration between global and personalized models [14, 13, 8].

Learning quantized models:There are two kinds of approaches for training quantized networks that are of our interest. The first one approximates the hard quantization function by using a soft surrogate [33, 11, 25, 6], while the other one iteratively projects the model parameters onto the fixed set of centers [2, 34, 20, 17]. Each approach has its own limitation; see Section 2.1 for a discussion. While the initial focus in learning quantized networks was on achieving good empirical performance, there are some works that analyzed convergence properties [22, 34, 2], but only in the centralized case.Among these, [2] analyzed convergence for a relaxed/regularized loss function using proximal updates.

Knowledge distillation (KD): KD [16] is a framework for transfer learning that is generally used to train a small student network using the soft labels generated by a deep teacher network. It can also be used to train two or more networks mutually by switching teacher and students in each iteration [36]. KD has been employed in FL settings as an alternative to simple aggregation which is not feasible when clients have models with different dimensions [24]. [21] used KD in personalized FL by assuming existence of a public dataset.[29] used KD in combination with quantization in a centralized case for model compression; in contrast, we do not use KD for model compression but for collaboration between personalized and global model. Unlike the above works which are empirical, our paper is the first to formalize an optimization problem for personalized FL training with KD and analyze its convergence properties. Our proposed scheme yields personalized client models with different precision/dimension through local alternating proximal updates; see Section 2.2 for details.

In this part, different than Section 3, we have an additional update due to local iterations. The key is to integrate the local iterations into our alternating update scheme. First, we show the sufficient decrease property by sequentially using and combining Lipschitz properties for each update step in Algorithm 2.
