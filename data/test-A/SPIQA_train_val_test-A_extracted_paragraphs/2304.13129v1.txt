The main goal of our analysis is to identify users’ intent when they reformulate their queries for particular gender and how gender distribution in SERP affect the reformulation. To better understand user demand for gender-based query reformulation first we need to identify what kind of queries they reformulate for particular gender. We want to find if there exists any pattern in their gender-based reformulation and analyzing the queries may elicit their intention or demand that cause gender-based reformulation.Then, we want to observe the impact of SERP in their gender-based reformulation and if the representation and distribution of gender in SERP have any influence on users’ gender-based query reformulation.We want to answer these following research questions:•RQ1 Can we observe a pattern or tendency in users’ gender-based query reformulations?•RQ2 What are the potential reasons of users’ group-based query reformulation?•RQ3 How are demographic groups (gender) represented in SERP in response to queries?•RQ4 How does gender representation in SERP affect users’ group-based query reformulation?

From our analysis on gender-based query reformulation, we identified some common topics of query that user frequently reformulate with gender.

Then we observed the gender distribution in query reformulation for these topics. We identified a separation between ’sports and outdoor’ and ’shopping and fashion’ when user reformulate query for gender. Queries about ’sports and outdoor’ are more likely to reformulated for women whereas queries regarding ’shopping and outdoor’ often get reformulated for men.

From the above observation, we identified some potential demands that may lead users to reformulate their query for specific gender-•Not satisfied- not find what they were looking for due to insufficient resource for specific gender; for example, ”hairstyles for men”- Users do not want mixed information•Satisfied- Users want to learn more- Users don’t have particular need in mind; clicked on the suggestion•Neutral- Users want products for specific gender; for example, ”bike for women”- Users want web page section for specific gender; for example, ”macys’ online shopping women”

To connect user demands with gender distribution in SERP, we need to observe in which ways genders are represented and distributed in SERP.Gender distribution in SERP can be-•Skewed towards a particular gender- Resources are mostly about a particular gender- No explicit mention of gender but the related images portrays a particular gender relevance•Mixed gender information- Results can carry social stereotypes in gender representation.- Position of gendered resource is different•No gender information is shown but matches with particular gendered results.

The potential reasons of the discrepancy of gender distribution in SERP can be-•SERP show trending results which can cause gender skew. For example: World cup 2022•The product or brand is particularly for a gender. For example, Athleta is shopping site for women’s sportswear.•Search engine is showing personalized SERP based on users’ gender.•For a product or topic, there are more resource available for a particular gender•A query term can have multiple meanings. For example: what is period?•Search engine may replicate social stereotypes in representing and distributing gender in SERP. For example, SERP regarding sports and games queries are often male dominant.
