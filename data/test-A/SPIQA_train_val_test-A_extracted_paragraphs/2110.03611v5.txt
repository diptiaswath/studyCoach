Dense text retrievalВ (Lee etВ al., 2019; Karpukhin etВ al., 2020) has achieved great successes in a wide variety of both research and industrial areas, such as search enginesВ (Brickley etВ al., 2019; Shen etВ al., 2014), recommendation systemВ (Hu etВ al., 2020), open-domain question answering В (Guo etВ al., 2018; Liu etВ al., 2020), etc. A typical dense retrieval model adopts a dual-encoderВ (Huang etВ al., 2013) architecture to encode queries and documents into low-dimensional embedding vectors, with the relevance between query and document being measured by the similarity between embeddings. In real-world dense text retrieval applications, it pre-computes all the embedding vectors of documents in the corpus, and leverages the approximate nearest neighbor (ANN)В (Johnson etВ al., 2019) technique for efficiency. To train a dense retriever, contrastive loss with negative samples is widely applied in the literatureВ (Xiong etВ al., 2021; Karpukhin etВ al., 2020).During training, the model utilizes a negative sampling method to obtain negative documents for a given query-document pair, and then minimizes the contrastive loss which relies on both the positive document and the sampled negative ones (Shen etВ al., 2014; Chen etВ al., 2017; Radford etВ al., 2021).

Recent studies on contrastive learning (Xiong etВ al., 2021; Karpukhin etВ al., 2020) show that the iterative вҖңhard-negativeвҖқ sampling technique can significantly improve the performance compared with вҖңrandom-negativeвҖқ sampling approach, as it can pick more representative negative samples to learn a more discriminative retriever. In the work В (Qu etВ al., 2021), it suggests leveraging cross-encoder model to heuristically filter вҖңhard-negativeвҖқ samples to further improve performance and shows the importance of sampling technique in the contrastive learning.

On the other hand, the model architecture of dual-encoders enables the encoding of queries and documents independently which is essential for document indexing and fast retrieval. However, this ignores the modeling of finer-grained interactions between queries and documents which could be a sub-optimal solution in terms of retrieval accuracy.

Motivated by these phenomena, we propose an Adversarial Retriever-Ranker (AR2) framework. The intuitive idea of AR2 is inspired by the вҖңretriever-rankerвҖқ architecture in the classical information retrieval systems. AR2 consists of two modules: a dual-encoder model served as the retrieval module in Figure 1(a) and a cross-encoder model served as the ranker module in Figure 1(b). The cross-encoder model takes the concatenation of a query and document as input text, and can generate more accurate relevance scores compared with the dual-encoder model, since it can fully explore the interactions between the query and document through a self-attention mechanism using a conventional transformer modelВ (Vaswani etВ al., 2017; Guo etВ al., 2020).Instead of training вҖңretriever-rankerвҖқ modules independently in some IR systems (Manning etВ al., 2008; Mitra & Craswell, 2017), AR2 constructs a unified minimax game for training the retriever and ranker models interactively, as shown in Figure 2.

In particular, AR2 adopts a minimax objective from the adversarial game (Goodfellow etВ al., 2014) where the retrieval model is optimized to produce relevant documents to fool the ranker model, whereas the ranker model learns to distinguish the ground-truth relevant document and retrieved ones by its opponent retrieval model. Within the adversarial вҖңretriever-rankerвҖқ training framework, the retrieval model receives the smooth training signals from the ranker model which helps alleviate the harmful effects of вҖңfalse-negativeвҖқ issues. For example, a вҖңfalse-negativeвҖқ example which is rated as high-relevance by the ranker model, will also be granted with high probability by retrieval model in order to fool the ranker, meanwhile the ranker model with better generalization capability is more resistant to label noises compared to the retrieval model.

In the empirical studies of AR2, we further introduce a distillation regularization approach to help stabilize/improve the training of the retriever. Intuitively, the retriever would converge to sharp conditional-probabilities over documents given a query within the adversarial training framework, i.e., high retrieval probabilities for the top relevant documents and near-zero retrieval ones for the rest. However, it is not a desirable property as it might impede exploring different documents during training. Thus, we incorporate the distillation loss between the retriever and ranker models as a smooth term for further improvement.

In experiments, we evaluate AR2 on three widely used benchmarks for dense text retrieval: Natural Questions, Trivia QA and MS-MARCO. Experimental results show that AR2 achieves state-of-the-art performance on all these datasets. Meanwhile, we provide a comprehensive ablation study to demonstrate the advantage of different AR2 components.

Third step, we iteratively train the ranker (AR2-рқҗғрқҗғ\mathbf{D}) model initialized with ERNIE-2.0-large and the retriever (AR2-рқҗҶрқҗҶ\mathbf{G}) initialized with рқҗҶ0superscriptрқҗҶ0\mathbf{G}^{0} according to Algorithm 1. Text Retrieval: Text retrieval aims to find related documents from a large corpus given a query. Retrieval-then-rank is the widely used pipelineВ (Huang etВ al., 2020; Zou etВ al., 2021).

For the first stage retrieval, early researchers used sparse vector space models, e.g., BM25В (Yang etВ al., 2017).Recently, some works improve the traditional sparse retriever with neural network, e.g.,В Dai & Callan (2019) use BERT to dynamically generate term weights, doc2QueryВ (Nogueira etВ al., 2019b), docTTTTTQueryВ (Nogueira etВ al., 2019a), and GARВ (Mao etВ al., 2021a) use text generation to expand queries or documents to make better use of BM25.

Recently, dense retrieval methods have become a new paradigm for the first stage of retrieval.Various methods have been proposed to enhance dense retrieval, e.g., DPRВ (Karpukhin etВ al., 2020) and ME-BERTВ (Luan etВ al., 2021) use in-batch negatives and construct hard negatives by BM25; ANCEВ (Xiong etВ al., 2021), RocketQAВ (Qu etВ al., 2021), and ADOREВ (Zhan etВ al., 2021) improve the hard negative sampling by iterative replacement, denoising, and dynamic sampling, respectively; PAIRВ (Ren etВ al., 2021) leverages passage-centric similarity relation into training object; FID-KDВ (Izacard & Grave, 2020) and RDRВ (Yang & Seo, 2020) distill knowledge from reader to retriever; В Guu etВ al. (2020) and В Sachan etВ al. (2021b) enhance retriever by jointly training with downstream tasks. Some researchers focus on the pre-training of dense retrieval, such as ICTВ (Lee etВ al., 2019), CondenserВ (Gao & Callan, 2021b) and CocondenserВ (Gao & Callan, 2021a).

For the second stage ranking, previous works typically use cross-encoder based methods. The cross-encoder models which capture the token-level interactions between the query and the documentВ (Guo etВ al., 2016; Xiong etВ al., 2017), have shown to be more effective. Various methods are proposed to enhance ranker, e.g., В Nogueira & Cho (2019) use BERT to make a binary relevance decision for each query-passage pair; В Nogueira etВ al. (2020) adopt BART to generate relevance labels as target tokens in a seq2seq manner;В Khattab & Zaharia (2020) andВ Gao etВ al. (2020) adopt the lightweight interaction based on the representations of dense retrievers to reduce computation. However, negative samples are statically sampled in these works. In AR2, negative samples for training the ranker will be dynamically adjusted with the progressive retriever.

Generative Adversarial Nets:Generative Adversarial NetsВ (Goodfellow etВ al., 2014) have been widely studied in the generation field, i.e., image generationВ (Brock etВ al., 2018) and text generationВ (Yu etВ al., 2017). With a minimax game, GAN aims to train a generative model to fit the real data distribution under the guidance of a discriminative model. Few works study GAN to text retrieval. A related work is IRGANВ (Wang etВ al., 2017). It proposes a minimax retrieval framework that aims to unify the generative and discriminative retrieval models.

In this paper, we introduce AR2, an adversarial retriever-ranker framework to jointly train the end-to-end retrieve-then-rank pipeline. In AR2, the retriever retrieves hard negatives to cheat the ranker, and the ranker learns to rank the collection of positives and hard negatives while providing progressive rewards to the retriever. AR2 can iteratively improve the performance of both the retriever and the ranker because (1) the retriever is guided by the progressive ranker; (2) the ranker learns better through the harder negatives sampled by the progressive retriever. AR2 achieves new state-of-the-art performance on all three competitive benchmarks.
