Large-scale Vision-Language (V-L) modelsВ [60] trained with contrastive learning currently represent the de-facto approach for few-shot visual adaptation. Their unprecedented success lies in part in the strength of the joint V-L embedding space learned by aligning the image and text modalities. However, when a V-L model is applied to a new domain, the domain shift exacerbates the V-L modality gapВ [48], and some sort of adaptation is required to obtain high accuracy (see Fig.В 1(a)). The question that we want to address in this paper is: вҖңcan we effectively adapt a V-L model to a new domain by having access to pre-computed features only?вҖқ We call this black-box adaptation.

Similar to their NLP counterpartsВ [60, 44], soft prompt learninghas emerged as the preferred technique for adapting a V&L to new tasks. Specifically, a number ofworksВ [86, 85, 11, 87, 15, 67, 51, 36] have proposed to replace the manuallydesigned prompts ofВ [60] (e.g., a photo of a {cls_name}), with a sequenceof learnable vectors, coined soft prompts. These are passed as input to the text encoder jointlywith the class name cls_name to create the new prototypes effectively reducing the modality gap.The prompts are learned in a supervised manner using a standard cross-entropy loss given a set of labeled images.

While soft-prompt learning approaches demonstrate promising results on various downstreamtasksВ [86, 85, 11], they suffer from twolimitations: (1) They require access to the modelвҖҷs weights, and (2) the training cost canbe prohibitive, especially on commodity hardware and low-power devices, as computing the gradientsand updating the prompts for thousands of iterationsВ [86] is required.As the modelвҖҷs size continues to grow (e.g., billion-parameter models such asCoCa [80]), and the industry transitions to models as a service (e.g., via API),the existing methods can be rendered either inapplicable or impractical.

In this work, we seek to address these limitations by bridging the modality gap directly in the feature spacewithout prompting or access to the modelвҖҷs weights. We first empirically show that a simple lineartransformation can approximate the alignment effect of promptlearning (e.g., see Fig.В 1 and Sec.В 3).Importantly, this shows that it is possible to derive a black-box method that manipulates the CLIP features directly fordownstream adaptation. Then, motivated by this observation, we propose Linear Feature Alignment (LFA), a black-box methodthat learns a linear mapping рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W}, obtained by solving a simple optimization problem, which effectively aligns theimage features рқҗ—рқҗ—\mathbf{X} with their text class prototypes рқҗҳрқҗҳ\mathbf{Y},i.e., рқҗ—вҶ’рқҗ–рқҗҳрқҗ–вҶ’рқҗ—рқҗҳ\mathbf{X}\xrightarrow{\mathbf{W}}\mathbf{Y}. Specifically, our contributions are:

вҖўWe propose the very first black-box method for the few-shot adaptation of V-L models.вҖўTo this end, and motivated by the observation that prompting can be successfully approximated by alinear transformation, we propose Linear Feature Alignment (LFA), an efficient and effective adaptation method for reducing the modality gapbetween the image and text modalities of a V-L model. LFA is initialized by ОІрқӣҪ\beta-Procrustes,a regularized version of orthogonal Procrustes, and then minimizes a simple adaptive reranking loss adapted for V-L models.вҖўWe propose both supervised and unsupervised formulations for LFA and moreover, a variant thatworks for the case of base-to-new (i.e., zero-shot) generalization.вҖўWe demonstrate that LFA can achieve better alignment (e.g., see Fig.В 1 (1d) and (2)) and improvedaccuracy compared to prompt learning methods while being more efficient (i.e., training takes few minutes)and practical (i.e., not requiring access to the modelвҖҷs weights). Finally, we show that it can even align image andtext features computed from uni-modal models.

Vision-Language (V-L) Models: Recently, we have witnessedan explosion of research in V-L foundation models, including CLIP [60],ALIGN [38], Florence [81],LiT [83], BASIC [59], ALBEF [46] and CoCa [80].Such models are pre-trained on large amounts of image and text datato learn a joint multi-modal embedding space.After pre-training, they can be used on various downstream tasks in a few- or zero-shot setting.For our work, we used CLIPВ [60] to extract the frozen image and text features.

Learnable Prompts for V-L Models:Despite pre-training, V-L models still suffer from a modality gapВ [48]which is further exacerbated during downstream adaptation. To address this issue, recently,soft prompt learning methods [86, 85, 11, 87, 15, 67, 51, 36]optimize a new set of learnable (soft) prompts to reduce the gap and align the visual andtext modalities. CoOpВ [86] was the first method to apply promptlearning methods [47, 44, 30, 66]popularized in NLP to V-L models. Subsequent works have improved upon this by incorporatingimage conditioning for better generalization [85],test-time adaptation [67], gradient matching [67], or by usingan additional text-to-text loss [11]. In contrast to all the aforementionedmethods, we propose to bridge the domain gap for a given downstream task directly in the feature space,without requiring access to the modelвҖҷs weights nor expensive training procedures.

Linear Alignment:The problem of linearly aligning two sets of embeddings or high-dimensional realvectors is a well-studied problem in machine learning, with various applicationsin computer vision and NLP. Classical applications range from sentenceclassification [26, 62],to shape and motion extraction [72], registration [19]and geometrical alignment [24, 43, 49].In vision, linear mappings are widely used for zero-shot learning[25, 1, 2, 64]for aligning the image features and their class attributes.In NLP, and after the introduction of word embeddings [53, 9],this linear alignment problem was revisited and extensively studied, and improved upon for the taskof word translation [3, 27, 35, 18, 79, 54, 13, 39, 84, 68, 7, 6, 5, 4].In this paper, we take strong inspiration from this line of work and set toadapt them for the case of V-L models.

Thusthe assignment matrix рқҗҸрқҗҸ\mathbf{P} must be estimated jointly with learning the mapping рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W}.By keeping the orthogonality constraint over рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W} and solving forboth рқҗҸрқҗҸ\mathbf{P} and рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W}, the resulting optimization problem,often referred to as the Wasserstein-Procrustes[84, 27] problem takes the following form:рқҗ–вӢҶ,рқҗҸвӢҶ=argminрқҗ–вҲҲрқ’Әd,рқҗҸвҲҲрқ’«NГ—CвҖӢвҖ–рқҗ—рқҗ–вҲ’рқҗҸрқҗҳвҖ–F2.superscriptрқҗ–вӢҶsuperscriptрқҗҸвӢҶformulae-sequenceрқҗ–subscriptрқ’Әрқ‘‘рқҗҸsubscriptрқ’«рқ‘Ғрқҗ¶argminsuperscriptsubscriptnormрқҗ—рқҗ–рқҗҸрқҗҳF2\mathbf{W}^{\star},\mathbf{P}^{\star}=\underset{\mathbf{W}\in\mathcal{O}_{d},\mathbf{P}\in\mathcal{P}_{N\times C}}{\operatorname{argmin}}\|\mathbf{X}\mathbf{W}-\mathbf{P}\mathbf{Y}\|_{\mathrm{F}}^{2}.(7)As neither of the two sets рқ’Әdsubscriptрқ’Әрқ‘‘\mathcal{O}_{d}and рқ’«NГ—Csubscriptрқ’«рқ‘Ғрқҗ¶\mathcal{P}_{N\times C} are convex, this optimization problem is notconvex either. To solve it, in practice, we follow a simple heuristic by alternating betweenfinding the assignments рқҗҸрқҗҸ\mathbf{P} using the efficient Sinkhorn algorithmВ [20] andrefining the mapping following Eq.В 6. Given this,Unsupervised LFA (U-LFA) takes the following form:

1.рқҗҸвҶҗвҶҗрқҗҸabsent\mathbf{P}\leftarrow Sinkhorn [рқҗ—,рқҗҳрқҗ—рқҗҳ\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}].2.рқҗ–opвҶҗвҶҗsubscriptрқҗ–opabsent\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{op}}\leftarrow Orthogonal Procrustes [рқҗ—,рқҗҸрқҗҳрқҗ—рқҗҸрқҗҳ\mathbf{X},\mathbf{P}\mathbf{Y}].3.рқҗ–вҶҗвҶҗрқҗ–absent\mathbf{W}\leftarrow ОІрқӣҪ\beta-Procrustes[рқҗ–opsubscriptрқҗ–op\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{op}}].4.Repeat for nрқ‘ӣn iterations:(a)рқҗҸвҶҗвҶҗрқҗҸabsent\mathbf{P}\leftarrow Sinkhorn [рқҗ—рқҗ–,рқҗҳрқҗ—рқҗ–рқҗҳ\mathbf{X}\mathbf{W},\mathbf{Y}].(b)рқҗ–вҶҗвҶҗрқҗ–absent\mathbf{W}\leftarrow Refine [рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W}, рқҗ—,рқҗҸрқҗҳрқҗ—рқҗҸрқҗҳ\mathbf{X},\mathbf{P}\mathbf{Y}].

An important property of large-scale V-L models that recent few-shot adaptation methods seek to preserve istheir zero-shot generalization ability, i.e., generalization from seen (base) classes to unseen (new) classes.Training LFA in this setting, i.e., on the base set, may result in a mapping that fails to generalise to the newset due to the distribution shift in between the two.

To address this, starting from a task-specific mapping рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W}, during the iterative refinement step,we initialize a second рқҗ–ttsubscriptрқҗ–tt\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}} as an identity map рқҗҲdsubscriptрқҗҲрқ‘‘\mathbf{I}_{d}. At each optimization step tрқ‘Ўt ofthe refinement procedure, we then update рқҗ–ttsubscriptрқҗ–tt\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}}using рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W} with an exponential moving average as follows:рқҗ–ttвҖӢ(t)вҶҗОұвҖӢ(t)вҖӢрқҗ–ttвҖӢ(t)+(1вҲ’ОұвҖӢ(t))вҖӢрқҗ–вҖӢ(t)вҶҗsubscriptрқҗ–ttрқ‘Ўрқӣјрқ‘Ўsubscriptрқҗ–ttрқ‘Ў1рқӣјрқ‘Ўрқҗ–рқ‘Ў\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}}(t)\leftarrow\alpha(t)\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}}(t)+(1-\alpha(t))\mathbf{W}(t)(8)with ОұвҖӢ(t)вҲҲ[0,1]рқӣјрқ‘Ў01\alpha(t)\in[0,1] as the momentum parameter, which is initialized as 0.9,and is increased to 1.0 following a log schedule during the first half of the optimization.This way, we only incorporate the first refinement updates into рқҗ–ttsubscriptрқҗ–tt\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}},while the later ones, which tend to be more task-specific and may hinder generalization are largely ignored.At test time, рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W} can be used on the base classes, while рқҗ–ttsubscriptрқҗ–tt\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}}for the new classes. As shown in Tab.В 6, this maintains the good accuracy on the base training domain,while demonstrating good generalization when a distribution shift occurs (i.e., on novel classes).Additionally, using a single mapping, obtained by taking the averageof рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W} and рқҗ–ttsubscriptрқҗ–tt\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{tt}} also achieves good results (see Tab.В 6).

In this work we proposed LFA, the first black box few-shot adaptation method for V-L models,that uses pre-computed image and text features without accessing the modelвҖҷs weights. Other advantages ofLFA include fast training, applicability to both supervised and unsupervised setting, and even application foraligning image and text features computed from uni-modal models. Thanks to the use of precomputed features, wehope that LFA will enable few-shot adaptation of very large V-L foundation models that would otherwise beimpossible to adapt or even deploy.
