A central question in information theory and statistics is to establish the fundamental limits for recovering a planted signal in high-dimensional models.A common theme in these works is the presence of phase transitions, where the behavior of optimal estimators changes dramatically at critical values.An infamous example is the PCA transition, where one observes a matrix рқҗҳвҲҲв„қpГ—pрқҗҳsuperscriptв„қрқ‘қрқ‘қ\mathbf{Y}\in\mathbb{R}^{p\times p} given byрқҗҳ=ОІвҖӢpвҖӢрқҗұрқҗұвҠӨ+рқҗ–,рқҗҳрқӣҪрқ‘қsuperscriptрқҗұрқҗұtopрқҗ–\mathbf{Y}=\sqrt{\beta p}\,\mathbf{x}\mathbf{x}^{\top}+\mathbf{W}\,,where рқҗұрқҗұ\mathbf{x} is drawn uniformly from the unit sphere in в„қpsuperscriptв„қрқ‘қ\mathbb{R}^{p} and рқҗ–рқҗ–\mathbf{W} is an independent Gaussian Wigner matrix.When ОІ<1рқӣҪ1\beta<1, then as pвҶ’вҲһвҶ’рқ‘қp\to\infty the leading eigenvector of рқҗҳрқҗҳ\mathbf{Y} is asymptotically uncorrelated with рқҗұрқҗұ\mathbf{x}; on the other hand, when ОІ>1рқӣҪ1\beta>1, the correlation between the hidden signal рқҗұрқҗұ\mathbf{x} and the leading eigenvector of рқҗҳрқҗҳ\mathbf{Y} remains positive as pвҶ’вҲһвҶ’рқ‘қp\to\infty [BBP05, FP07, BGN11].

A number of recent works establish that several sparse estimation tasks in high dimensions evince the following even more striking phase transition, called the вҖңall-or-nothingвҖқ phenomenon [GZ17, RXZ19b, Zad19]: below a critical signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), it is impossible to achieve any correlation with the hidden signal, but above this critical SNR, it is possible to achieve almost perfect correlation with the hidden signal.In other words, at any fixed SNR, one can either recover the signal perfectly, or nothing at all.

In prior work, the all-or-nothing phenomenon has been established in a smattering of different models and sparsity regimes. Understanding the extent to which this phenomenon holds more generally, beyond the models and sparsity conditions previously considered, is the main motivation of the present work. The all-or-nothing phenomenon for sparse linear regression was initially conjectured byВ [GZ17]. That work established that a version of this phenomenon holds for the maximum likelihood estimator (MLE): below a given threshold, the MLE does not achieve any constant correlation with the hidden signal, while above the same threshold the MLE achieves almost perfect correlation. However, this result does not rule out the existence of other estimators with better performance. Subsequently,В [RXZ19b] proved that the all-or-nothing phenomenon indeed holds for sparse linear regression, when the sparsity level kрқ‘ҳk satisfies kвүӨp12вҲ’Пөрқ‘ҳsuperscriptрқ‘қ12italic-Пөk\leq p^{\frac{1}{2}-\epsilon} for some Пө>0italic-Пө0\epsilon>0, where pрқ‘қp is the dimension of the model. Furthermore,В [RXZ19a] provided generic conditions under which the phenomenon holds for sparse linear regression when k/p=Оҙ>0рқ‘ҳрқ‘қрқӣҝ0k/p=\delta>0 where Оҙ>0рқӣҝ0\delta>0 is a constant which shrinks to zero. Following these works, [BM19, BMR20] showed that the sparse PCA model with binary and Rademacher non-zero entries also exhibits the all-or-nothing phenomenon when the sparsity level kрқ‘ҳk satisfies p1213+ПөвүӨkвүӘpsuperscriptрқ‘қ1213italic-Пөрқ‘ҳmuch-less-thanрқ‘қp^{\frac{12}{13}+\epsilon}\leq k\ll p. The вҖңall-or-nothingвҖқ phenomenon has also recently been established in the context of the Bernoulli group testing model [TAS20]. That work proves the existence of this phenomenon in an extremely sparse setting, where kрқ‘ҳk scales polylogarithmically with the dimension of the model. Finally, it was recently shown in [LBM20] using analytical non-rigorous methods, that the all-or-nothing phenomenon also holds for various generalized linear models with a kрқ‘ҳk-sparse signal, under the assumption p89+ПөвүӨkвүӘp.superscriptрқ‘қ89italic-Пөрқ‘ҳmuch-less-thanрқ‘қp^{\frac{8}{9}+\epsilon}\leq k\ll p.

In this work, in an attempt to shed some light on the fundamental reason for the existence of the all-or-nothing phenomenon, we focus on a simple Gaussian model, which we refer to as the Gaussian additive model, in which one observes a hidden signal drawn from some known prior, corrupted with additive Gaussian noise. For example, all PCA models, and in particular the sparse PCA model considered by [BM19, BMR20], are special cases of Gaussian additive models. We focus on the case where the prior is an arbitrary uniform distribution over some discrete subset of the Euclidean sphere.

We make the following contributions.вҖўWe show that for this additive Gaussian model, the all-or-nothing phenomenon is equivalent to a simple criterion on the Kullback-Lieber divergence between the model and a null distribution with i.i.d.В Gaussian entries.вҖўWe show that, under an appropriate вҖңnear-orthogonalityвҖқ condition on the prior, the all-or-nothing phenomenon always holds.вҖўAs an application, we study sparse tensor PCA, in which the hidden signal is a rank-one tensor рқҗұвҠ—dвҲҲ(в„қp)вҠ—dsuperscriptрқҗұtensor-productabsentрқ‘‘superscriptsuperscriptв„қрқ‘қtensor-productabsentрқ‘‘\mathbf{x}^{\otimes d}\in(\mathbb{R}^{p})^{\otimes d}, where the entries of xрқ‘Ҙx are kрқ‘ҳk-sparse. We show that for both the Bernoulli and Bernoulli-Rademacher prior, all sparsity levels k=oвҖӢ(p)рқ‘ҳрқ‘ңрқ‘қk=o(p), and all dвүҘ2рқ‘‘2d\geq 2, this model satisfies the aforementioned near-orthogonality condition, and therefore evinces the all-or-nothing phenomenon. To the best of our knowledge this is the first result that proves the all-or-nothing phenomenon for all sparsity levels which are sublinear in the dimension of the model.

Our results for sparse tensor PCA are closely connected to several prior works.

These papers study the sparse tensor PCA problem with a Bernoulli-Rademacher prior. Their focus is on optimal recovery of the hidden signal in the regime where the sparsity satisfies k=ОівҖӢpрқ‘ҳрқӣҫрқ‘қk=\gamma p for some constant Оі>0рқӣҫ0\gamma>0. Both papers use a trick known as the conditional second moment method, and our argument in SectionВ 4 is closely inspired by their techniques.

Our results differ from theirs in two important respects. First, though taking the sparse limit ОівҶ’0вҶ’рқӣҫ0\gamma\to 0 is suggestive, these works do not offer a rigorous way to establish the presence of a threshold when k=oвҖӢ(p)рқ‘ҳрқ‘ңрқ‘қk=o(p).More importantly, the results of [BMV+18] and [PWB20] elucidate the threshold between вҖңno recoveryвҖқ (zero correlation with hidden signal) and вҖңpartial recoveryвҖқ (constant correlation with hidden signal) for sparse tensor PCA. This work shows that the all-or-nothing phenomenon in Gaussian additive models is equivalent to a condition on the Kullback-Leibler divergence between the model at a particular SNR and a standard Gaussian vector.Using this equivalence, we derive a simple condition on the overlaps which guarantees the existence of the all-or-nothing phenomenon, and as a corollary show that this phenomenon indeed arises in sparse tensor PCA for all sublinear sparsity levels.

While this paper gives a characterization of the all-or-nothing phenomenon for Gaussian models, we leave open the question of whether our framework can be extended to a more general setting.Neither the results ofВ [RXZ19b] for sparse linear regression, nor ofВ [TAS20] for Bernoulli group testing, nor ofВ [LBM20] for sparse generalized linear models are immediately implied by our main theorems.It may yet be possible to obtain more general results which encompass all of these settings.
