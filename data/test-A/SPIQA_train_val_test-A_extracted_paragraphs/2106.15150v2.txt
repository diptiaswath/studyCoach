Formal ontologies are of significant importance in artificial intelligence, playing a central role in the Semantic Web, ontology-based information integration, or peer-to-peer data management.In such scenarios, an especially prominent role is played by description logics (DLs) [BHLS17] – a robust family of logical formalisms used to describe ontologies and serving as the logical underpinning of contemporary standardised ontology languages.To put knowledge bases to full use as core part of intelligent information systems, much attention is being devoted to the area of ontology-based data-access, with conjunctive queries (CQs) being employed as a fundamental querying formalism [OS12].

In recent years, it has become apparent that various modelling features of DLs affect the complexity of CQ answering in a rather strong sense.Let us focus on the most popular DL, 𝒜​ℒ​𝒞𝒜ℒ𝒞\mathcal{ALC}.It was first shown in [Lut08, Thm. 2] that CQ entailment is exponentially harder than the consistency problem for 𝒜​ℒ​𝒞𝒜ℒ𝒞\mathcal{ALC} extended with inverse roles (ℐ)ℐ(\mathcal{I}).Shortly after, a combination of transitivity and role-hierarchies (𝒮​ℋ)𝒮ℋ(\mathcal{S}\mathcal{H}) was shown as a culprit of higher worst-case complexity of reasoning [ELOS09, Thm. 1].Finally, also nominals (𝒪)𝒪(\mathcal{O}) turned out to be problematic [NOS16, Thm. 9].Nevertheless, there are also more benign DL constructs regarding the complexity of CQ entailment.Examples are counting (𝒬𝒬\mathcal{Q}) [Lut08, Thm. 4] (the complexity stays the same even for expressive arithmetical constraints [BBR20, Thm. 21]), role-hierarchies alone (ℋ)ℋ(\mathcal{H}) [EOS12, Cor. 3], or even a tamed use of higher-arity relations [Bed21a, Thm. 20].

We study CQ entailment in 𝒜​ℒ​𝒞𝖲𝖾𝗅𝖿𝒜ℒsubscript𝒞𝖲𝖾𝗅𝖿\mathcal{ALC}_{\mathsf{Self}}, an extension of 𝒜​ℒ​𝒞𝒜ℒ𝒞\mathcal{ALC} with the 𝖲𝖾𝗅𝖿𝖲𝖾𝗅𝖿\mathsf{Self} operator, \iea modelling feature that allows us to specify the situation when an element is related to itself by a binary relationship.
