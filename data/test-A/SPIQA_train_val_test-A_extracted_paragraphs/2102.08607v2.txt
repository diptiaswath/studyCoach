In this paper, we propose a simple gradient truncation mechanism to fix this issue.

Next, let us go beyond cumulative return and consider policy optimization for a general utility where F𝐹F may be nonlinear. However, much less is known in this setting. The nonlinearity of F𝐹F invalidates the concept of Q-function and value function, leading to the failure of policy gradient theorem [43]. To overcome such difficulty,[50] showed that the policy gradient for the general utilities is the solution to a min-max problem. However, estimating a single PG is highly nontrivial in this case. It is still unclear how to make PG methods to use samples in a most efficient way.

In this paper, we aim to investigate the convergence and sample efficiency of the PG method, using episodic sampling, for both linear F𝐹F (i.e., cumulative rewards) and nonlinear F𝐹F (i.e., general utility). Policy gradient gives rises to a rich family of RL algorithms, such as REINFORCE and many of its variants [46, 5, 57], as well as extensions such as the natural policy gradient methods [20, 31], the actor-critic methods [22, 21, 27], the trust-region policy optimization [36, 56, 38], and the proximal policy optimization method [37, 24], etc. In this paper we mainly focus on REINFORCE-type methods, where many of them need 𝒪~​(ϵ−4)~𝒪superscriptitalic-ϵ4\tilde{\mathcal{O}}(\epsilon^{-4}) samples to find an ϵitalic-ϵ\epsilon-stationary solution, including the vanilla REINFORCE [46], as well as its variants with baseline [57, 42] and GPOMDP [5], etc. By incorporating the stochastic variance reduction techniques, the sample efficiency of PG methods can be further improved. In [30], the SVRG [19] variance reduction scheme is adopted and an 𝒪​(ϵ−4)𝒪superscriptitalic-ϵ4\mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{-4}) sample complexity is achieved, which is later improved to 𝒪​(ϵ−10/3)𝒪superscriptitalic-ϵ103\mathcal{O}(\epsilon^{-10/3}) by [47]. We would like to comment that these results are only for finding ϵitalic-ϵ\epsilon-stationary (rather than near-optimal) solutions, and all of them requires an uncheckable condition on the importance weights in every iteration.

Recently, for cumulative reward, a series of works have started to study the convergence of policy gradient method to global optimal solutions [1, 14, 55, 6, 25, 7, 9, 54].In particular, [50] exploited the hidden convexity property of the MDP problem and established the convergence to global optimality for general utility RL problem, as long as the policy gradient can be computed exactly.

Our approach is related to the stochastic composite optimization (SCO) [44, 45]. For the general composition problem, there have been numerous developments, including momentum-based and multi-time-scale algorithms [44, 45, 15], and various composite stochastic variance-reduced algorithms [23, 17, 51, 53]. Our approach is also inspired by variance reduction techniques that were initially used for stochastic convex optimization, see [19, 35, 11, 28]; and were later on extended to the stochastic nonconvex optimization problems [2, 33, 18, 34, 13, 29]. In particular, we will utilize the SARAH/Spider scheme [13, 29].
