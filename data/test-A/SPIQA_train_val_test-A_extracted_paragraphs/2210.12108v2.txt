Audio source separation consists of separating the sources present in an audio mix, as in music source separation (separating vocals, bass, and drums from a music mixВ [1, 2, 3]) or speech source separation (separating various speakers talking simultaneouslyВ [4, 5, 6]).Recently, universal sound separation was proposedВ [7]. It consists of building source agnostic models that are not constrained to a specific domain (like music or speech) and can separate any source given an arbitrary mix.Permutation invariant trainingВ (PIT)В [8] is used for training universal source separation models based on deep learningВ [7, 9, 10].

In this work, we complement PIT with adversarial losses for universal sound separation.A number of speech source separation works also complemented PIT with adversarial lossesВ [11, 12, 13, 14]. Yet, we find that the adversarial PIT formulation used in speech separation does not perform well for universal source separation (sectionsВ 3 andВ 4). To improve upon that, in sectionВ 2 we extend speech separation works with: a novel IрқҗјI-replacement context-based adversarial loss, by combining multiple discriminators, and generalize adversarial PIT such that it works for universal sound separation (with source agnostic discriminators dealing with more than two sources). Adversarial training, in the context of source separation, consists of simultaneously training two models: fОёsubscriptрқ‘“рқңғf_{\theta} producing plausible separations рқҗ¬^^рқҗ¬\mathbf{\hat{s}}, and one (or multiple) discriminator(s) Dрқҗ·D assessing if separations рқҗ¬^^рқҗ¬\mathbf{\hat{s}} are produced by fОёsubscriptрқ‘“рқңғf_{\theta} (fake) or are ground-truth separations рқҗ¬рқҗ¬\mathbf{{s}} (real).Under this setup, the goal of fОёsubscriptрқ‘“рқңғf_{\theta} is to estimate (fake) separations that are as close as possible to the (real) ones from the dataset, such that Dрқҗ·D misclassifies рқҗ¬^^рқҗ¬\mathbf{\hat{s}} as рқҗ¬рқҗ¬\mathbf{{s}}В [15, 16].We propose combining variations of an instance-based discriminatorВ Dinstsubscriptрқҗ·instD_{\text{inst}} with a novel IрқҗјI-replacement context-based discriminatorВ Dctx,Isubscriptрқҗ·ctxрқҗјD_{\text{ctx},I}.Each Dрқҗ·D has a different role and is applicable to various domains: waveforms, magnitude STFTs, or masks.Without loss of generality, we present Dinstsubscriptрқҗ·instD_{\text{inst}} and Dctx,Isubscriptрқҗ·ctxрқҗјD_{\text{ctx},I} in the waveform domain and then show how to combine multiple discriminators operating at various domains to train fОёsubscriptрқ‘“рқңғf_{\theta}.
