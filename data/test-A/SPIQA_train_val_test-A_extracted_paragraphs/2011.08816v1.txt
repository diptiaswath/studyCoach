Traditionally, most of the research in OBDA has focused on answeringconjunctive queries. However, navigational queries haverecently gained a lot of attentionВ (?;В ?;В ?)since they are key in various applications. For instance, inbiomedicine they are used to retrieve specific paths from protein,cellular and disease networksВ (?;В ?). Aprominent class of navigational queries is that of regular pathqueriesВ (?), where paths are specified by aregular expression. Indeed, motivated by applications in the semanticweb, the latest W3C standard SPARQL 1.1 includes property paths, relatedto regular expressions.

The objective of this paper is to start the research on queryanswering in DLs supporting qualified number restrictions overtransitive roles. We study the entailment problem of positiveexistential two-way regular path queriesВ (?) overрқ’®вҖӢрқ’¬рқ’®рқ’¬\mathcal{SQ} ontologies, thus generalizing both conjunctive and regular pathqueries. To this end, we pursue an automata-based approach forquery answering using two-way alternating tree automata(2ATA)В (?). This roughly consists of threestepsВ (?): (iрқ‘–i) show that, if a query ПҶрқң‘\varphi isnot entailed by the knowledge base рқ’Ұрқ’Ұ\mathcal{K}, there is a tree-likeinterpretation witnessing this, (iвҖӢiрқ‘–рқ‘–ii) devise an automaton рқ”„рқ’Ұsubscriptрқ”„рқ’Ұ\mathfrak{A}_{\mathcal{K}}which accepts precisely the tree-like interpretations of рқ’Ұрқ’Ұ\mathcal{K},(iвҖӢiвҖӢiрқ‘–рқ‘–рқ‘–iii) devise an automaton рқ”„ПҶsubscriptрқ”„рқң‘\mathfrak{A}_{\varphi} which accepts a tree-likeinterpretation iff it satisfies ПҶрқң‘\varphi.
