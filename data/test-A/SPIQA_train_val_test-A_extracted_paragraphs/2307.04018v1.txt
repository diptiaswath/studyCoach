With the advance in deep learning and pre-trained language models (PLMs)В Devlin etВ al. (2019); Lewis etВ al. (2020); Raffel etВ al. (2020), much recent progress has been made in text summarization Liu and Lapata (2019); Zhong etВ al. (2022a); Chen etВ al. (2022a).However, most work focuses on English (En) dataВ Zhong etВ al. (2021); Gliwa etВ al. (2019); Chen etВ al. (2021), which does not consider cross-lingual sources for summarizationВ Wang etВ al. (2022b). To address this limitation, cross-lingual summarization (CLS) aims to generate summaries in a target language given texts from a source languageВ Zhu etВ al. (2019), which has shown values to both academic and industrial communitiesВ Bai etВ al. (2021); Perez-Beltrachini andLapata (2021).

Most existing workВ Zhu etВ al. (2019); Bai etВ al. (2021); Feng etВ al. (2022) constructs CLS corpora by translating summaries from existing mono-lingual summarization datasets into other languages, which is de facto a вҖңpipelineвҖқ annotation protocol (first summarize, then translate) as shown in Figure 1.However, such an annotation method can suffer from two major problems:First, summaries from mono-lingual summarization corpora (summarization process) can contain errorsВ Liu etВ al. (2022), which are likely to be preserved in translated summaries.For example, the English summary in Figure 1-(a) contains unmentioned content/hallucination (red text), which leads to the same discrepancy as in the translated summary (Figure 1-(b), red text).Second, the translation process can further introduce errors, in particular for polysemous words.For example, in Figure 1-(b), the term, вҖңEx-VikingвҖқ (which refers to previous members of the Minnesota Vikings team), is mistakenly translated into вҖңеүҚжө·зӣ—вҖқ (which means вҖңex-pirate/buccaneerвҖқ).To determine proper translation, it require more information beyond the scope of short summaries.

To qualitatively understand the above problems, we conduct human evaluation and error analysis on existing popular CLS corpora. Empirical results show that existing corpora suffer from the two aforementioned problems, containing significantly many hallucinations and factual errors.111The term error later in this paper refers to errors that are hallucinations or can cause factual misunderstandings, except when otherwise specified.In particular, we find that overall 20вҲј67%similar-to20percent6720\sim 67\% of summaries in CLS datasets contain errors, where 7вҲј46%similar-to7percent467\sim 46\% and 13вҲј47%similar-to13percent4713\sim 47\% of summaries suffer from summarization and translation processes, respectively.This suggests that the pipeline protocol, which is widely used in CLS research, can result in low-quality data and negatively impact the validity of modeling research. Motivated by the above findings and to address this issue, we propose the protocol that cross-lingual summaries should be sourced from original input text where mono-lingual summaries can serve as a quick review for salient information.With this concept, we annotate cross-lingual summaries (StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt}) by relying on source text (DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}) and source-language summaries (SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}) as shown in Figure 1-(c).Such an annotation protocol brings three advantages:First, compared with translation only given SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}, rich context information from DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src} helps annotators to disambiguate word senses and comprehend SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src} accurately, e.g., вҖңеүҚз»ҙдә¬дәәйҳҹвҖқ (which means вҖңex-viking team playerвҖқ) in Figure 1-(c);Second, DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src} is more reliable and can provide ground-truth information to correct potential errors in SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}, e.g., red text in Figure 1-(a);Third, compared with writing StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} only given DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}, SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src} can serve as supplement guidance to help annotators be aware of what should be involved in the summaries, ensuring that salient information in SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src} and StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} is aligned.

Using CLS protocol, we build ConvSumX, a new benchmark to facilitate future CLS research.ConvSumX focuses on conversational text in a few-shot setting.Compared with monologue (e.g., news), conversational text is less explored yet is also practically useful in real-world scenariosВ Chen etВ al. Based on the same intuition that DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src} and SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src} can serve as a critical complement to each other, we propose a 2-Step framework for CLS, which fine-tunes a multi-lingual PLM using concatenated SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src} and DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src} as input, and StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} as output.Experimental results show that our conceptual framework yields surprisingly better performance over strong baselines on ConvSumX.Analysis and human evaluation show that our method can effectively generate more faithful cross-lingual summaries in a low-resource setting, and verify that source input text and summaries are supplementary to each other in modeling cross-lingual summaries.

To address the aforementioned issues in pipeline annotation, we propose ConvSumX with a new annotation protocol, focusing on few-shot CLS.ConvSumX contains two cross-lingual summarization scenarios, namely daily dialogue summarization, and query-based summarization, covering 3 language directions: En2Zh, En2Fr and En2Ukr.

We choose DialogSumВ (Chen etВ al., 2021) and QMSumВ (Zhong etВ al., 2021) for ConvSumX by considering their potential to build real-world applications, and annotating their test and dev sets.

DialogSumВ Chen etВ al. (2021) is a real-life scenario dialogue summarization dataset, including various types of task-oriented dialogues.

QMSumВ Zhong etВ al. (2021) is a query-based meeting summarization dataset, covering the academic, product and committee domains.We select data from academic and product for annotation.

As discussed in В§В 2, the final quality of CLS corpora can be influenced by both summarization process and translation process, most of which can be resolved with the information from input documents.Therefore, instead of merely focusing on summaries in source languages, we ask annotators to write summaries in target languages (StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt}) directly by considering both input documents (DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}) and pre-annotated summaries (SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}).We refer to our protocol as CLS protocol.

We take English as the source language and choose Mandarin, French and Ukrainian as target languages because they are from different language families, and have different morphological variations and syntactic structures, with the potential to benefit other languages in their families.We invite expert translators, who are native in target languages and professional in English, as our annotators (AppendixВ A).We ask annotators to first comprehend DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}, and then write StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} with the help of SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}.In addition to the standard annotation criteria of DialogSum and QMSum, we ask our annotators specifically pay attention to the following aspects featuring the CLS:

вҖўCross-lingual Consistency: Although being in different languages, the core semantic information of StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} should be consistent with DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}, in particular for polysemous words or phrases.вҖўLanguage Style and Terminology: Annotators should write StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} in the same language style of SsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җS^{src}, and use proper terminologies in some certain domains, such as academic meetings.вҖўTranslationese: The annotated summaries should be natural in the target languages.

For QMSum, annotators are additionally asked to write a query in target languages (QtвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘„рқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎQ^{tgt}) with the help of the query in source language (QsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқ‘„рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җQ^{src}), where QtвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘„рқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎQ^{tgt} and StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} form a QA pair.

Generally, the task of few-shot CLS is defined as:given a source input text DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src}, few-shot CLS is to generate a summary in a target language StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} by learning a limited number of gold-annotated вҹЁDsвҖӢrвҖӢc,StвҖӢgвҖӢtвҹ©superscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘Ў\langle D^{src},S^{tgt}\rangle data, with the help of external knowledge, which can be from mono-lingual summarization data, machine translation data and PLMs.

Specifically, for query-focused CLS, the system is asked to generate StвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘Ҷрқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎS^{tgt} given DsвҖӢrвҖӢcsuperscriptрқҗ·рқ‘ рқ‘ҹрқ‘җD^{src} with a query in the target language QtвҖӢgвҖӢtsuperscriptрқ‘„рқ‘Ўрқ‘”рқ‘ЎQ^{tgt}.

Existing CLS corpora construction can be categorized into two main protocols.1) Pipeline annotation: translating summaries from MLS corpora into other languages and;2) Automatic alignment: aligning summaries and input texts of different language versions.

Zhu etВ al. (2019) construct the first large-scale CLS dataset by automatically translating mono-lingual summaries using MT systems with a round-trip strategy and manual post-editing on test sets.Bai etВ al. (2021) construct an En2De dataset using the same method.Feng etВ al. (2022) automatically translate summaries from SAMSumВ Gliwa etВ al. (2019) into Russian, De and Zh. Wang etВ al. (2022a) manually translate summaries from SAMSumВ Gliwa etВ al. (2019) and MediaSumВ Zhu etВ al. (2021) into De and Zh.Different from them, we propose a new annotation protocol, which helps annotators to comprehend documents quickly and accurately.To our knowledge, we are the first to address such human annotation issues for CLS research and present a new benchmark, ConvSumX.

A different line of work constructs CLS datasets by linking different language versions of online articles, such as WikipediaВ Perez-Beltrachini andLapata (2021) and WikiHowВ Ladhak etВ al. (2020).Despite the cheap cost and large scale, there can be misalignment and hallucination problems.For example, Wikipedia articles and their leading paragraphs (pseudo summaries) of the same person in different languages can contain different contents.Also, such a method is limited to resources that contain multi-lingual data, which may not be available for all domains of interest, for example, the conversational text.

Early work on CLS focuses on a pipeline paradigm by first summarizing, then translating, or vice versa.However, due to the poor performance of early MT and summarization systems, such methods can often suffer from error propagation.With the advance of deep learning and PLM technologies, recent work deploys end-to-end methods.Zhu etВ al. (2019), Xu etВ al. (2020), Bai etВ al. (2021) and Wang etВ al. (2022a) propose multi-task learning or pre-training on large in-domain CLS, mono-lingual summarization and translation data.Different from them, we propose a 2-Step method under the same concept of sourcing from source input text with the guidance of source summary, which is free of pre-training on large and thus can be easily adapted to other tasks and languages.

We conducted data analysis on 3 typical corpora and showed that the pipeline annotation protocol suffers from errors from both the summarization and translation processes.To address these issues, we proposed that cross-lingual summaries should be sourced from source input text.Based on this principle, we annotated a more faithful CLS benchmark, ConvSumX by relying on both source-language texts and summaries.Based on the same intuition, we proposed a 2-Step method that takes both source text and source summaries as input.Experimental results showed that 2-Step method outperforms strong baselines on ConvSumX, demonstrating that both source-language texts and summaries are crucial in modeling cross-lingual summaries and are complementary to each other.To our knowledge, we are the first to show that summary translation has limitations for CLS, giving a more faithful solution.
