Given an arbitrary vertex uрқ‘ўu of a graph G=(V,E)рқҗәрқ‘үрқҗёG=(V,E) as input, a local graph clustering algorithm finds some low-conductance set SвҠӮVрқ‘Ҷрқ‘үS\subset V containing uрқ‘ўu, while the algorithm runs in time proportional to the size of the target cluster and independent of the size of the graph GрқҗәG. Because of the increasing size of available datasets, whichmakes centralised computation too expensive, local graph clustering has become an important learning technique foranalysing a number of large-scale graphs and has been applied to solve many other learning and combinatorial optimisation problemsВ [1, 5, 11, 16, 25, 29, 30, 32].

We study local graph clustering for learning the structure of clusters that are defined by their inter-connections,and present two local algorithms to achieve this objective in both undirected graphs and directed ones.

Our first result is a local algorithm for finding densely connected clustersin an undirected graph G=(V,E)рқҗәрқ‘үрқҗёG=(V,E): given any seed vertex uрқ‘ўu, our algorithm is designed to find two clusters L,Rрқҗҝрқ‘…L,R around uрқ‘ўu, which are densely connected to each other and are loosely connected to VвҲ–(LвҲӘR)рқ‘үрқҗҝрқ‘…V\setminus(L\cup R). The design of our algorithm is based on a new reduction that allows us to relate the connections between L,Rрқҗҝрқ‘…L,R and VвҲ–(LвҲӘR)рқ‘үрқҗҝрқ‘…V\setminus(L\cup R) to a single cluster in the resulting graph Hрқҗ»H, and a generalised analysis of Pagerank-based algorithms for local graph clustering. The significance of our designed algorithm is demonstrated by our experimental results on the Interstate Dispute Network from 1816 to 2010. By connecting two verticesВ (countries) with an undirected edgeweighted according to the severity of their military disputesand using the USA as the seed vertex,our algorithm recovers two groups of countries that tend to have conflicts with each other, and shows how the two groups evolve over time. In particular, as shown in FiguresВ 1(a)-(d), our algorithm not only identifies the changing roles of Russia, Japan, and eastern Europe in line with 20th century geopolitics, but also the reunification of east and west Germany around 1990.

We further study densely connected clustersin a directed graphВ (digraph). Specifically, given any vertex uрқ‘ўu in a digraph G=(V,E)рқҗәрқ‘үрқҗёG=(V,E) as input, our second local algorithm outputs two disjoint vertex sets LрқҗҝL and Rрқ‘…R, such that (i) there are many edges from LрқҗҝL to Rрқ‘…R, and (ii) LвҲӘRрқҗҝрқ‘…L\cup R is loosely connected to VвҲ–(LвҲӘR)рқ‘үрқҗҝрқ‘…V\setminus(L\cup R). The design of our algorithm is based on the following two techniques: (1) anew reduction that allows us to relate the edge weight from LрқҗҝL to Rрқ‘…R, as well as the edge connections between LвҲӘRрқҗҝрқ‘…L\cup R and VвҲ–(LвҲӘR)рқ‘үрқҗҝрқ‘…V\setminus(L\cup R), to a single vertex set in the resulting undirected graph Hрқҗ»H; (2) a refined analysis of the ESP-based algorithm for local graph clustering.We show that our algorithm is able to recover local densely connected clusters in the US migration dataset, in which two vertex sets L,Rрқҗҝрқ‘…L,R defined as above could represent a higher-order migration trend. In particular, as shown in FiguresВ 1(e)вҖ“(h), by using different counties as starting vertices, our algorithm uncovers refined and more localised migration patterns than the previous work on the same datasetВ [10]. To the best of our knowledge, our work represents the first local clustering algorithm that achieves a similar goal.

Li and PengВ [15] study the same problem for undirected graphs,and present a random walk based algorithm. AndersenВ [1] studies a similar problem for undirected graphs under a different objective function, and our algorithmвҖҷs runtime significantly improves the one in [1].There is some recent work on local clustering for hypergraphsВ [25],and algorithms for finding higher-order structures of graphs based on network motifs both centrallyВ [7, 8] and locallyВ [30].These algorithms are designed to find different types of clusters, and cannot be directly compared with ours.Our problem is related to the problem of finding clusters in disassortative networksВ [22, 23, 31], although the existing techniques are based on semi-supervised, global methods while ours is unsupervised and local.There are also recent studies which find clusters with a specific structurein the centralised settingВ [10, 14].Our current work shows that such clusters can be learned locally via our presented new techniques.

In addition,we underline that one cannot apply the tighter analysis of the ESP process given by Andersen et al.В [5] to our algorithm.The key to their analysis is animproved bound on the probability that a random walk escapes from the target cluster.In order to take advantage of this, they use a larger value of Tрқ‘ҮT in the algorithm which relaxes the guarantee on the volume of the output set.Since our analysis relies on a very tight guarantee on the overlap of the output set with the target set, we cannot use their improvement in our setting.
