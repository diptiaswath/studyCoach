The flexibility of Transformer Vaswani et al. (2017) facilitates the development of multitask models that use one model to handle multiple tasks Liu et al. (2019); Raffel et al. (2019); Lewis et al. (2020).These models are typically “dense” —  all the model parameters are activated for all the tasks.However, it is unclear what skills are learned in which parts of the parameters.Even though tackling different tasks requires different skills Dean (2021); Tang et al. (2022), dense models do not allow us to carry out subtle operations to choose different skills for different tasks.Moreover, when adapting a well-trained dense model to learn new tasks, all the encoded “vague” skills are transferred blindly, regardless of their relevance to the tasks.

In this work, we propose a general-purpose natural language generation model called SkillNet-NLG.The basic idea is that the model includes multiple skill modules, each of which stands for a particular skill defined in Table 1.Instead of activating all the parameters in traditional dense models, we only activate relevant skills for a downstream task.As depicted in Figure 1, for the task of dialogue generation, SkillNet-NLG requires the ability to generate open-ended language (So​p​e​n−e​n​dsubscript𝑆𝑜𝑝𝑒𝑛𝑒𝑛𝑑S_{open-end}), understand the conversational context (Sc​o​n​v​e​r​s​a​t​i​o​nsubscript𝑆𝑐𝑜𝑛𝑣𝑒𝑟𝑠𝑎𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛S_{conversation}) and understand natural language questions (Sq​u​e​s​t​i​o​nsubscript𝑆𝑞𝑢𝑒𝑠𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛S_{question}).Therefore, the skill modules related to So​p​e​n−e​n​dsubscript𝑆𝑜𝑝𝑒𝑛𝑒𝑛𝑑S_{open-end}, Sc​o​n​v​e​r​s​a​t​i​o​nsubscript𝑆𝑐𝑜𝑛𝑣𝑒𝑟𝑠𝑎𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛S_{conversation}, Sq​u​e​s​t​i​o​nsubscript𝑆𝑞𝑢𝑒𝑠𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛S_{question} and Sg​e​n​e​r​a​lsubscript𝑆𝑔𝑒𝑛𝑒𝑟𝑎𝑙S_{general}111We define a general skill Sg​e​n​e​r​a​lsubscript𝑆𝑔𝑒𝑛𝑒𝑟𝑎𝑙S_{general}, which works as a default skill and is always activated. are activated.The remaining modules (Sn​o​n−o​p​e​n−e​n​dsubscript𝑆𝑛𝑜𝑛𝑜𝑝𝑒𝑛𝑒𝑛𝑑S_{non-open-end} and Sd​a​t​a−t​o−t​e​x​tsubscript𝑆𝑑𝑎𝑡𝑎𝑡𝑜𝑡𝑒𝑥𝑡S_{data-to-text}) are not activated.We develop SkillNet-NLG based on Transformer Vaswani et al. We conduct extensive experiments on Chinese natural language generation tasks222Our approach is language agnostic. We leave the extension of SkillNet-NLG to more languages in the future..We consider five tasks (text summarization, advertisement generation, question answering, dialogue generation and grammatical error correction) for multi-task training.With one model, SkillNet-NLG outperforms previous best performance methods on four of five tasks and performs better than both dense and MoE multitask baselines.We further adapt well-trained models to three new tasks (topic-to-essay generation, paraphrase generation and story generation), and find that SkillNet-NLG performs better than all the aforementioned baseline systems.

In this work, we present a general-purpose model called SkillNet-NLG.It deals with multiple natural language generation tasks with one model.The key feature of our approach is that it is sparsely activated guided by a set of predefined skills. Only the parameters of relevant skills are activated. The advantage of such model design is that it enables us to only transfer relevant skills to learn new tasks.Experimental results on Chinese NLG tasks verify the effectiveness of our approach.In the future, we plan to adapt the model to more languages and even more modalities.
