We test the effectiveness of ImpFlow on several classification and generative modeling tasks. ImpFlow outperforms ResFlow on all the benchmarks, with comparable model sizes and computational cost.

Expressive Normalizing Flows There are many works focusing on improving the capacity of NFs. For example, Dinh etВ al. (2014; 2017); Kingma & Dhariwal (2018); Ho etВ al. (2019); Song etВ al. (2019); Hoogeboom etВ al. (2019); DeВ Cao etВ al. (2020); Durkan etВ al. (2019) design dedicated model architectures with tractable Jacobian. More recently, Grathwohl etВ al. (2019); Behrmann etВ al. (2019); Chen etВ al. (2019) propose NFs with free-form Jacobian, which approximate the determinant with stochastic estimators. In parallel with architecture design, Chen etВ al. (2020); Huang etВ al. (2020); Cornish etВ al. (2020); Nielsen etВ al. (2020) improve the capacity of NFs by operating in a higher-dimensional space. As mentioned in the introduction, all these existing works adopt explicit forward mappings, which is only a subset of the broad class of invertible functions. In contrast, the implicit function family we consider is richer. While we primarily discuss the implicit generalization of ResFlowsВ (Chen etВ al., 2019) in this paper, the general idea of utilizing implicit invertible functions could be potentially applied to other models as well. Finally,В Zhang etВ al. (2020) formally prove that the model capacity of ResFlows is restricted by the dimension of the residual blocks.In comparison,we study another limitation of ResFlows in terms of the bounded Lipschitz constant, and compare the function family of ResFlows and ImpFlows with a comparable depth.

Continuous Time Flows (CTFs)В (Chen etВ al., 2018b; Grathwohl etВ al., 2019; Chen etВ al., 2018a) are flexible alternative to discrete time flows for generative modeling. They typically treat the invertible transformation as a dynamical system, which is approximately simulated by ordinary differential equation (ODE) solvers. In contrast, the implicit function family considered in this paper does not contain differential equations, and only requires fixed point solvers. Moreover, the theoretical guarantee is different. While CTFs typically study the universal approximation capacity under the continuous time case (i.e., вҖңinfinite depthвҖқ limit), we consider the model capacity of ImpFlows and ResFlows under a finite number of transformation steps. Finally, while CTFs are flexible, their learning is challenging due to instabilityВ (Liu etВ al., 2020; Massaroli etВ al., 2020) and exceedingly many ODE solver stepsВ (Finlay etВ al., 2020), making their large-scale application still an open problem.

Implicit Deep LearningUtilizing implicit functions enhances the flexibility of neural networks, enabling the design of network layers in a problem-specific way. For instance, Bai etВ al. (2019) propose a deep equilibrium model as a compact replacement of recurrent networks; Amos & Kolter (2017) generalize each layer to solve an optimization problem; Wang etВ al. (2019) integrate logical reasoning into neural networks; Reshniak & Webster (2019) utilize the implicit Euler method to improve the stability of both forward and backward processes for residual blocks; and Sitzmann etВ al. (2020) incorporate periodic functions for representation learning. Different from these works, which consider implicit functions as a replacement to feed-forward networks, we develop invertible implicit functions for normalizing flows, discuss the conditions of the existence of such functions, and theoretically study the model capacity of our proposed ImpFlow in the function space.

We propose implicit normalizing flows (ImpFlows), which generalize normalizing flows via utilizing an implicit invertible mapping defined by the roots of the equation FвҖӢ(рқҗі,рқҗұ)=0рқҗ№рқҗірқҗұ0F(\boldsymbol{\mathbf{z}},\boldsymbol{\mathbf{x}})=0. ImpFlows build on Residual Flows (ResFlows) with a good balance between tractability and expressiveness. We show that the functional family of ImpFlows is richer than that of ResFlows, particularly for modeling functions with large Lipschitz constants.Based on the implicit differentiation formula, we present a scalable algorithm to train and evaluate ImpFlows. Empirically, ImpFlows outperform ResFlows on several classification and density modeling benchmarks. Finally, while this paper mostly focuses on the implicit generalization of ResFlows, the general idea of utilizing implicit functions for NFs could be extended to a wider scope. We leave it as a future work.
