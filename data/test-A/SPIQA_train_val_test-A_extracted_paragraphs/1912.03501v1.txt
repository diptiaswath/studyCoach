Integer linear programming is a fundamental hard problem, which motivates the search for tractable special cases.In the ’80s, Lenstra and Kannan [23, 30] and Papadimitriou [32] have shown that the classes of ILPs with few variables or few constraints and small coefficients, respectively, are polynomially solvable.A line of research going back almost 20 years [17, 3, 8, 1, 16, 11, 10, 6] has recently culminated with the discovery of another tractable class of ILPs [9, 29], namely ILPs with small treedepth and coefficients.The obtained results already found various algorithmic applications in areas such asscheduling [25, 4, 22],stringology and socialchoice [26, 27], and the traveling salesman problem [3].
