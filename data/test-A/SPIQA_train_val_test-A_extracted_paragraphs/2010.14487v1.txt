kрқ‘ҳk-means clustering is one of the most commonly encountered unsupervised learning problems. Given a set of nрқ‘ӣn data points in Euclidean space, our goal is to partition them into kрқ‘ҳk clusters (each characterized by a center), such that the sum of squares of distances of data points to their nearest centers is minimized.The most popular heuristic for solving this problem is LloydвҖҷs algorithm Lloyd (1982), often referred to simply as вҖңthe kрқ‘ҳk-means algorithm".

LloydвҖҷs algorithm uses iterative improvements to find a locally optimal kрқ‘ҳk-means clustering. The performance of LloydвҖҷs algorithm crucially depends on the quality of the initial clustering, which is defined by the initial set of centers, called a seed. Arthur and Vassilvitskii (2007) and Ostrovsky, Rabani, Schulman, andSwamy (2006) developed an elegant randomized seeding algorithm, known as the kрқ‘ҳk-means++ algorithm. It works by choosing the first center uniformly at random from the data set and then choosing the subsequent kвҲ’1рқ‘ҳ1k-1 centers by randomly sampling a single point in each round with the sampling probability of every point proportional to its current cost. That is, the probability of choosing any data point xрқ‘Ҙx is proportional to the squared distance to its closest already chosen center. This squared distance is often denoted by D2вҖӢ(x)superscriptрқҗ·2рқ‘ҘD^{2}(x). Arthur and Vassilvitskii (2007) proved that the expected cost of the initial clustering obtained by kрқ‘ҳk-means++ is at most 8вҖӢ(lnвҒЎk+2)8рқ‘ҳ28\left(\ln k+2\right) times the cost of the optimal clustering i.e., kрқ‘ҳk-means++ gives an 8вҖӢ(lnвҒЎk+2)8рқ‘ҳ28\left(\ln k+2\right)-approximation for the kрқ‘ҳk-means problem. They also provided a family of kрқ‘ҳk-means instances for which the approximation factor of kрқ‘ҳk-means++ is 2вҖӢlnвҒЎk2рқ‘ҳ2\ln k and thus showed thattheir analysis of kрқ‘ҳk-means++ is almost tight.

Due to its speed, simplicity, and good empirical performance, kрқ‘ҳk-means++ is the most widely used algorithm for kрқ‘ҳk-means clustering. It is employed by such machine learning libraries as Apache Spark MLlib, Google BigQuery, IBM SPSS, Intel DAAL, and Microsoft ML.NET. In addition to kрқ‘ҳk-means++, these libraries implement a scalable variant of kрқ‘ҳk-means++ called kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel (read вҖңkрқ‘ҳk-means parallelвҖқ) designed by Bahmani, Moseley, Vattani, Kumar, andVassilvitskii (2012). Somewhat surprisingly, kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel not only works better in parallel than kрқ‘ҳk-means++ but also slightly outperforms kрқ‘ҳk-means++ in practice in the single machine setting (seeВ Bahmani etВ al. (2012) and Figure 1 below).However, theoretical guarantees for kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel are substantially weaker than for kрқ‘ҳk-means++.

The kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel algorithm makes Tрқ‘ҮT passes over the data set (usually T=5рқ‘Ү5T=5). In every round, it independently draws approximately в„“=ОҳвҖӢ(k)в„“Оҳрқ‘ҳ\ell=\Theta(k) random centers according to the D2superscriptрқҗ·2D^{2} distribution. After each round it recomputes the distances to the closest chosen centers and updates D2вҖӢ(x)superscriptрқҗ·2рқ‘ҘD^{2}(x) for all xрқ‘Ҙx in the data set. Thus, after Tрқ‘ҮT rounds, kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel chooses approximately TвҖӢв„“рқ‘Үв„“T\ell centers. It then selects kрқ‘ҳk centers among TвҖӢв„“рқ‘Үв„“T\ell centers using kрқ‘ҳk-means++.

Our contributions.  In this paper, we improve the theoretical guarantees for kрқ‘ҳk-means++, kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel, and Bi-Criteria kрқ‘ҳk-means++ (which we define below).

First, we show that the expected cost of the solution output by kрқ‘ҳk-means++ is at most 5вҖӢ(lnвҒЎk+2)5рқ‘ҳ25(\ln k+2) times the optimal solutionвҖҷs cost. This improves upon the bound of 8вҖӢ(lnвҒЎk+2)8рқ‘ҳ28(\ln k+2) shown by Arthur and Vassilvitskii (2007) and directly improves the approximation factors for several algorithms which use kрқ‘ҳk-means++ as a subroutine like Local Search k-means++ (Lattanzi and Sohler, 2019). To obtain this result, we give a refined analysis of the expected cost of covered clusters (see Lemma 3.2 in Arthur and Vassilvitskii (2007) and LemmaВ 4.1 in this paper). We also show that our new bound on the expected cost of covered clusters is tight (see LemmaВ C.1).

Then, we address the question of why the observed performance of kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel is better than the performance of kрқ‘ҳk-means++. There are two possible explanations for this fact. (1) This may be the case because kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel picks kрқ‘ҳk centers in two stages. At the first stage, it samples в„“вҖӢTвүҘkв„“рқ‘Үрқ‘ҳ\ell T\geq k centers. At the second stage, it prunes centers and chooses kрқ‘ҳk centers among в„“вҖӢTв„“рқ‘Ү\ell T centers using kрқ‘ҳk-means++. (2) This may also be the case because kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel updates the distribution function D2вҖӢ(x)superscriptрқҗ·2рқ‘ҘD^{2}(x) once in every round. That is, it recomputes D2вҖӢ(x)superscriptрқҗ·2рқ‘ҘD^{2}(x) once for every в„“в„“\ell chosen centers, while kрқ‘ҳk-means++ recomputes D2вҖӢ(x)superscriptрқҗ·2рқ‘ҘD^{2}(x) every time it chooses a center. In this paper, we empirically demonstrate that the first explanation is correct.First, we noticed that kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel for в„“вӢ…T=kвӢ…в„“рқ‘Үрқ‘ҳ\ell\cdot T=k is almost identical with kрқ‘ҳk-means++ (see AppendixВ A).Second, we compare kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel with another algorithm which we call Bi-Criteria kрқ‘ҳk-means++ with Pruning. This algorithm also works in two stages: At the Bi-Criteria kрқ‘ҳk-means++ stage, it chooses k+О”рқ‘ҳО”k+\Delta centers in the data set using kрқ‘ҳk-means++. Then, at the Pruning stage, it picks kрқ‘ҳk centers among the k+О”рқ‘ҳО”k+\Delta centers selected at the first stage again using kрқ‘ҳk-means++. Our experiments on the standard data sets BioTest from KDD-Cup 2004 Elber (2004) and COVTYPE from the UCI ML repository Dua and Graff (2017) show that the performance of kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel and Bi-Criteria kрқ‘ҳk-means++ with Pruning are essentially identical (see FiguresВ 1 and AppendixВ A).

These results lead to another interesting question: How good are kрқ‘ҳk-means++ and kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel algorithms that sample k+О”рқ‘ҳО”k+\Delta instead of kрқ‘ҳk centers? The idea of oversampling using kрқ‘ҳk-means++ was studied earlier in the literature under the name of bi-criteria approximation. В Aggarwal, Deshpande, andKannan (2009) showed that with constant probability, sampling k+О”рқ‘ҳО”k+\Delta centers by kрқ‘ҳk-means++ provides a constant-factor approximation if О”вүҘОІвҖӢkО”рқӣҪрқ‘ҳ\Delta\geq\beta k for some constant ОІ>0рқӣҪ0\beta>0. Other related work. Dasgupta (2008) and Aloise, Deshpande, Hansen, and Popat (2009) showed that kрқ‘ҳk-means problem is NP-hard. Awasthi, Charikar, Krishnaswamy, andSinop (2015) proved that it is also NP-hard to approximate kрқ‘ҳk-means objective within a factor of (1+Оө)1рқңҖ(1+\varepsilon) for some constant Оө>0рқңҖ0\varepsilon>0 (see also Lee, Schmidt, and Wright (2017)). We also mention that kрқ‘ҳk-means was studied not only for Euclidean spaces but also for arbitrary metric spaces.

There are several known constant factor approximation algorithms for the kрқ‘ҳk-means problem. Kanungo, Mount, Netanyahu, Piatko, Silverman, andWu (2004) gave a 9+Оө9рқңҖ9+\varepsilon approximation local search algorithm. Ahmadian, Norouzi-Fard, Svensson, andWard (2019) proposed a primal-dual algorithm with an approximation factor of 6.3576.3576.357. This is the best known approximation for kрқ‘ҳk-means. Makarychev, Makarychev, Sviridenko, andWard (2016) gave constant-factor bi-criteria approximation algorithms based on linear programming and local search. Note that although these algorithms run in polynomial time, they do not scale well to massive data sets. Lattanzi and Sohler (2019) provided a constant factor approximation by combining the local search idea with the kрқ‘ҳk-means++ algorithm. Choo, Grunau, Portmann, and RozhoЕҲ (2020) further improved upon this result by reducing the number of local search steps needed from OвҖӢ(kвҖӢlogвҒЎlogвҒЎk)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ҳрқ‘ҳO(k\log\log k) to OвҖӢ(k)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ҳO(k).

Independently and concurrently to our work, RozhoЕҲ (2020) gave an interesting analysis for kрқ‘ҳk-meansвҲҘparallel-to\parallel by viewing it as a balls into bins problem and showed that OвҖӢ(logвҒЎn/logвҒЎlogвҒЎn)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ӣрқ‘ӣO(\log n/\log\log n) rounds suffice to give a constant approximation with high probability.
