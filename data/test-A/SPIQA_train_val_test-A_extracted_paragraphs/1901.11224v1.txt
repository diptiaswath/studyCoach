In this section, we review additional related work that is not discussed in the introduction section.

Existing lower bounds for nonconvex optimization: To the best of our knowledge, the only existing lower bounds for nonconvex optimization are proved in Carmon et al. (2017a, b); Fang et al. (2018). Carmon et al. (2017a, b) proved the lower bounds for both deterministic and randomized algorithms on nonconvex optimization with high-order smoothness assumption. However, they did not consider the finite-sum structure which will bring additional dependency on the lower-smoothness parameter l𝑙l and the number of component functions n𝑛n. Fang et al. (2018) proved a lower bound for nonconvex finite-sum optimization under conventional smoothness assumption, i.e., l=−L𝑙𝐿l=-L. Our work extends this line of research, and proves matching lower bounds for nonconvex finite-sum optimization (and sum-of-nonconvex optimization) under the refined (l,L)𝑙𝐿(l,L)-smooth assumption.

Existing upper bounds for first-order convex optimization:There existing a bunch of work focusing on establishing upper complexity bounds to find ϵitalic-ϵ\epsilon-suboptimal solution for convex finite-sum optimization problems. It is well known that by treating F​(𝐱)𝐹𝐱F(\mathbf{x}) as a whole part, gradient descent can achieve O​(n​L/ϵ)𝑂𝑛𝐿italic-ϵO(nL/\epsilon) IFO complexity for convex functions and O​(n​L/σ​log⁡(1/ϵ))𝑂𝑛𝐿𝜎1italic-ϵO(nL/\sigma\log(1/\epsilon)) for σ𝜎\sigma-strongly convex functions, and accelerated gradient descent (AGD) (Nesterov, 1983) can achieve O​(n​L/ϵ)𝑂𝑛𝐿italic-ϵO(n\sqrt{L/\epsilon}) IFO complexity for convex functions and O​(n​L/σ​log⁡(1/ϵ))𝑂𝑛𝐿𝜎1italic-ϵO(n\sqrt{L/\sigma}\log(1/\epsilon)) for σ𝜎\sigma-strongly convex functions. Both IFO complexities achieved by AGD are optimal when n=1𝑛1n=1 (Nesterov, 1983). By using variance reduction technique (Roux et al., 2012; Johnson and Zhang, 2013; Xiao and Zhang, 2014; Defazio et al., 2014; Mairal, 2015; Bietti and Mairal, 2017), the IFO complexity can be improved to be O​((n+L/σ)​log⁡(1/ϵ))𝑂𝑛𝐿𝜎1italic-ϵO((n+L/\sigma)\log(1/\epsilon)) for strongly convex functions.
