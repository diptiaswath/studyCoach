Our contributionsВ В In this work, we offer four improvements over the original KT algorithm.First, we show in Sec.В 2.1 that a generalization of KT that uses only the target kernel рқҗӨрқҗӨ\mathbf{k} provides a tighter рқ’ӘвҖӢ(nвҲ’12вҖӢlogвҒЎn)рқ’Әsuperscriptрқ‘ӣ12рқ‘ӣ\mathcal{O}(n^{-\frac{1}{2}}\sqrt{\log n}) integration error guarantee for each function fрқ‘“f in the RKHS.This target KT guarantee (a) applies to any kernel рқҗӨрқҗӨ\mathbf{k} on any domain (even kernels that do not admit a square-root and kernels defined on non-Euclidean spaces), (b) applies to any target distribution в„ҷв„ҷ\mathbb{P} (even heavy-tailed в„ҷв„ҷ\mathbb{P} not covered by root KT guarantees), and (c) is dimension-free, eliminating the exponential dimension dependence and (logвҒЎn)d/2superscriptрқ‘ӣрқ‘‘2(\log n)^{d/2} factors of prior root KT guarantees.

Second, we prove in Sec.В 2.2 that, for analytic kernels, like Gaussian, inverse multiquadric (IMQ), and sinc, target KT admits MMD guarantees comparable to or better than those of Dwivedi & Mackey (2021) without making explicit use of a square-root kernel.Third, we establish in Sec.В 3 that generalized KT with a fractional Оұрқӣј\alpha-power kernel рқҗӨОұsubscriptрқҗӨрқӣј\mathbf{k}_{\alpha} yields improved MMD guarantees for kernels that do not admit a square-root, like Laplace and non-smooth MatГ©rn.Fourth, we show in Sec.В 3 that, remarkably, applying generalized KT to a sum of рқҗӨрқҗӨ\mathbf{k} and рқҗӨОұsubscriptрқҗӨрқӣј\mathbf{k}_{\alpha}вҖ”a procedure we call kernel thinning+ (KT+)вҖ”simultaneously inherits the improved MMD of power KT and the dimension-free individual function guarantees of target KT.

In Sec.В 4, we use our new tools to generate substantially compressed representations of both i.i.d. samples in dimensions d=2рқ‘‘2d=2 through 100100100 and Markov chain Monte Carlo samples targeting challenging differential equation posteriors.
