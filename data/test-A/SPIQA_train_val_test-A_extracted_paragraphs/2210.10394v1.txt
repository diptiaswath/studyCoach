We give near-optimal ϵitalic-ϵ{\epsilon}-coresets for k𝑘k-Median and k𝑘k-Means (and more generally, (k,z)𝑘𝑧(k,z)-Clustering)with outliers in Euclidean spaces.Clustering is a central task in data analysis,and popular center-based clustering methods, such as k𝑘k-Median and k𝑘k-Means, have been widely applied.In the vanilla version of these clustering problems,given a center set of k𝑘k points C𝐶C,the objective is usually defined by the sum of (squared) distances from each data point to C𝐶C.

This formulation, while quite intuitive and simple to use,has severe robustness issues when dealing with noisy/adversarial data;for instance, an adversary may add few noisy outlier points that are far from the centerto “fool” the clustering algorithm to wrongly put centers towards those points in order to minimize the cost.Indeed, such robustness issue introduced by outliers has becomea major challenge in data science and machine learning,and it attracted extensive algorithmic research on the topic [CKMN01, Che08, CLMW11, CG13, MNP+14, GKL+17, SRF20, DW20].Moreover, similar issues have also been studied from the angle of statistics [HR09].

We consider robust versions of these clustering problems,particularly a natural and popular variant, called clustering with outliers [CKMN01].Specifically, given a dataset X⊂ℝd𝑋superscriptℝ𝑑X\subset\mathbb{R}^{d}, the (k,z,m)𝑘𝑧𝑚(k,z,m)-Robust Clustering problem is to find a center set C⊂ℝd𝐶superscriptℝ𝑑C\subset\mathbb{R}^{d} of k𝑘k points (repetitions allowed), that minimizes the objective functioncostz(m)⁡(X,C):=minL⊆X:|L|=m​∑x∈X∖L(dist⁡(x,C))z.assignsubscriptsuperscriptcost𝑚𝑧𝑋𝐶subscript:𝐿𝑋𝐿𝑚subscript𝑥𝑋𝐿superscriptdist𝑥𝐶𝑧\operatorname{cost}^{(m)}_{z}(X,C):=\min_{L\subseteq X:|L|=m}\sum_{x\in X\setminus L}(\operatorname{dist}(x,C))^{z}.(1)Here, L𝐿L denotes the set of outliers, distdist\operatorname{dist} denotes the Euclidean distance, and dist⁡(x,C):=minc∈C⁡dist⁡(x,c)assigndist𝑥𝐶subscript𝑐𝐶dist𝑥𝑐\operatorname{dist}(x,C):=\min_{c\in C}\operatorname{dist}(x,c).Intuitively, the outliers capture the furthest points in a cluster which are “not well-clustered” and are most likely to be the noise.Notice that the parameter z𝑧z captures various (robust) clustering problems, including (k,m)𝑘𝑚(k,m)-Robust Median (where z=1𝑧1z=1), (k,m)𝑘𝑚(k,m)-Robust Means (where z=2𝑧2z=2).On the other hand, if the number of outliers m=0𝑚0m=0 then the robust clustering problem falls back to the non-robust version.The (k,z,m)𝑘𝑧𝑚(k,z,m)-Robust Clustering problem has been widely studied in the literature [Che08, GKL+17, KLS18, FKRS19, SRF20].Moreover, the idea of removing outliers has been also considered in other machine learning tasks, e.g., robust PCA [BK18] and robust regression [RL87, MNP+14].

However, the presence of outliers introduces significant computational challenges,and it inspires a series of research to design efficient algorithms for robust clustering.On one hand, approximation algorithms with strict accuracy guarantee has been obtained [CKMN01, Che08, GKL+17, KLS18, FZH+19, FKRS19, ZFH+21]but their running time is a high-degree polynomial which is impractical.On the other hand, more scalable algorithms were also proposed [BVX19, DKP20],however, the approximation ratio is worse, and a more severe limitationis that their guarantee usually violates the required number of outliers.Moreover, to the best of our knowledge, we are not aware of worksthat design algorithms in sublinear models, such as streaming and distributed computing.

In order to tackle the computational challenges,we consider coresets for robust clustering.Roughly, an ϵitalic-ϵ{\epsilon}-coreset is a tiny proxy of the massive input dataset,on which the clustering objective is preserved within ϵitalic-ϵ{\epsilon}-error for every potential center set.Existing algorithms may benefit a significant speedup if running on top of a coreset,and more importantly, coresets can be used to derive sublinear algorithms,including streaming algorithms [HM04],distributed algorithms [BEL13] and dynamic algorithms [HK20],which are highly useful to deal with massive datasets.

Stemming from [HM04], the study of coresets for the non-robust version of clustering, i.e., (k,z)𝑘𝑧(k,z)-Clustering,has been very fruitful [FL11, FSS20, SW18, HV20, BJKW21, CSS21b, BCAJ+22], and the state-of-the-art coreset achievesa size poly⁡(k​ϵ−1)poly𝑘superscriptitalic-ϵ1\operatorname{poly}(k{\epsilon}^{-1}), independent of d𝑑d and n𝑛n.However, coresets for robust clustering were much less understood.Existing results either suffers an exponential (k+m)k+msuperscript𝑘𝑚𝑘𝑚(k+m)^{k+m} factor in the coreset size [FS12],or needs to violate the required number of outliers [HJLW18].This gap leads to the following question: can we efficiently construct an ϵitalic-ϵ{\epsilon}-coreset of size poly⁡(m,k,ϵ−1)poly𝑚𝑘superscriptitalic-ϵ1\operatorname{poly}(m,k,{\epsilon}^{-1}) for (k,z,m)𝑘𝑧𝑚(k,z,m)-Robust Clustering (without violating the number of outliers)?

Our main contribution, stated in Theorem 1.1,is a near-optimal coreset for robust clustering,affirmatively answering the above question.In fact, we not only achieve poly⁡(m)poly𝑚\operatorname{poly}(m),but also linear in m𝑚m and is isolated from other factors.This can be very useful when the number of outliers m𝑚m is large.

Our coreset improves over previous results in several aspects.Notably, compared with [FS12],our result avoids the exponential (k+m)k+msuperscript𝑘𝑚𝑘𝑚(k+m)^{k+m} factor in the coreset size,and our coreset has a strict guarantee for m𝑚m outliers instead ofa bi-criteria guarantee as in [HJLW18] that needs to allow more or fewer outliers in the objective for the coreset.We also note that our coreset is composable (Remark 3.2).

Furthermore, we show that the linear dependence in m𝑚m is not avoidable in coreset size (Theorem 1.2).Hence, combining this with a recent size lower bound of Ω​(k​ϵ−2)Ω𝑘superscriptitalic-ϵ2\Omega(k{\epsilon}^{-2}) [CLSS22]for vanilla clustering (i.e., m=0𝑚0m=0),we conclude that the dependence of every parameter (i.e., m,k,ϵ𝑚𝑘italic-ϵm,k,{\epsilon}) in our coreset is nearly tight.

We evaluate the empirical performance of our coreset on various datasets (in Section 5).We validate the size-accuracy tradeoff of our coreset compared with popular coreset construction methods,particularly uniform sampling (which is a natural heuristic)and sensitivity sampling [FS12],and we observe that our coreset consistently outperforms these baselines in accuracyby a significant margin for every experimented coreset size ranging from 500500500 to 500050005000.We also run existing approximation algorithms on top of our coreset,and we achieve about 100x speedup for both a) a Lloyd heuristic adopted to the outlier setting [CG13] that is seeded by an outlier-version of k𝑘k-Means++ [BVX19],and b) a natural local search algorithm [FKRS19].These numbers show that our coreset is not only near-optimal in theory,but also demonstrates the potential to be used in practice.

We implement our coreset construction algorithm and evaluate its empirical performance on various real datasets.We compare it with several baselines and demonstrate the superior performance of our coreset.In addition, we show that our coresets can significantly speed up approximation algorithms for both (k,m)𝑘𝑚(k,m)-Robust Median and (k,m)𝑘𝑚(k,m)-Robust Means problems.
