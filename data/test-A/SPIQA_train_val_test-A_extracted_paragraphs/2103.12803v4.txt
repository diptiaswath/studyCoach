We also mention a couple limitations of implicit networks.

As discussed above, in theory given any feedforward network one may write down an implicit network yielding the same output (for all inputs). In practice, evaluating the implicit network requires finding a fixed point of RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta}. The fixed point finding algorithm then places constraints on RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta} (e.g.В AssumptionВ 2.1). Guaranteeing the existence and computability of dвҖӢрқ’©Оҳ/dвҖӢОҳрқ‘‘subscriptрқ’©Оҳрқ‘‘Оҳd{\cal N}_{\Theta}\big{/}d\Theta places further constraints on RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta}. For example, if Jacobian-based backpropagation is used, RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta} cannot contain batch normalization (Bai, Kolter, and Koltun 2019).

Once trained, inference with an implicit network requires solving for a fixed point of RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta}. Finding this fixed point using an iterative algorithm requires evaluating RОҳsubscriptрқ‘…ОҳR_{\Theta} repeatedly and, thus, is often slower than inference with a feedforward network.

A related implicit learning formulation is the well-known neural ODE modelВ (Chen etВ al. 2018; Dupont, Doucet, and Teh 2019; Ruthotto and Haber 2021).Neural ODEs leverage known connections between deep residual models and discretizations of differential equationsВ (Haber and Ruthotto 2017; Weinan 2017; Ruthotto and Haber 2019; Chang etВ al. 2018; Finlay etВ al. 2020; Lu etВ al. 2018), and replace these discretizations by black-box ODE solvers in forward and backward passes.The implicit property of these models arise from their method for computing gradients.Rather than backpropagate through each layer, backpropagation is instead done by solving the adjoint equationВ (Jameson 1988) using a blackbox ODE solver as well. This is analogous to solving the Jacobian-based equation when performing backpropagation for implicit networks (see (13)) and allows the user to alleviate the memory costs of backpropagation through deep neural models by solving the adjoint equation at additional computational costs. A drawback is that the adjoint equation must be solved to high-accuracy; otherwise, a descent direction is not necessarily guaranteedВ (Gholami, Keutzer, and Biros 2019; Onken and Ruthotto 2020; Onken etВ al. 2021).

This work presents a new and simple Jacobian-free backpropagation (JFB) scheme.JFB enables training of implicit networks with fixed memory costs (regardless of depth), is easy to code (see Figure 3), and yields efficient backpropagation (by removing computations to do linear solves at each step).Use of JFB is theoretically justified (even when fixed points are approximately computed).Our experiments show JFB yields competitive results for implicit networks.Extensions will enable satisfaction of additional constraints for imaging and phase retrievalВ (Klibanov 1986; Fienup 1982; Heaton etВ al. 2020; Fung and Wendy 2020; Kan, Fung, and Ruthotto 2020), geophysicsВ (Haber 2014; Fung and Ruthotto 2019a, b), and gamesВ (VonВ Neumann 1959; Lin etВ al. 2020; Li etВ al. ; Ruthotto etВ al. 2020).Future work will analyze our proposed JFB in stochastic settings.
