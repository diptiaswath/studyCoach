This work gives nearly tight upper and lower bounds for the problemof α𝛼\alpha-agnostic proper learning of halfspaces with a margin, for α=O​(1)𝛼𝑂1\alpha=O(1).Our upper and lower bounds for α=ω​(1)𝛼𝜔1\alpha=\omega(1) are far from tight.Closing this gap is an interesting open problem. More broadly, an interesting direction for future work would be to generalizeour agnostic learning results to broader classes of geometric functions.Finally, we believe that finding further connections between the problem of agnostic learning with a marginand adversarially robust learning is an intriguing direction to be explored.
