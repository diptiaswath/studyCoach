\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We have proposed Dynamics of Attention for Focus Transition (DAFT), which embeds the human prior of continuous focus transition.
In contrast to previous approaches, DAFT learns the dynamics in-between reasoning steps, yielding more interpretable attention maps.
When applied to MAC, the state-of-the-art among models that only use natural supervision, DAFT achieves the same performance while using $\nicefrac{1}{3}$ the number of reasoning steps.
In addition, we proposed a novel metric called Total Length Transition (TLT).
Following the minimum description length principle, TLT measures how good the model is on planning effective, short reasoning path (latent program), which is directly related to the interpretability of the model.

Next on our agenda includes
(1) extending DAFT to other tasks where performance and interpretability are both important to develop a method to balance between the two criteria, and 
(2) investigating what other values TLT can serve as a proxy for.
