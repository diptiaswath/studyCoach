In this paper, we studied the problem of game redesign where the players apply no-regret algorithms to play the game. We show that a designer can force all players to play a target action profile in T−o​(T)𝑇𝑜𝑇T-o(T) rounds while incurring only o​(T)𝑜𝑇o(T) cumulative design cost. We develop redesign algorithms for both the interior and the boundary target loss scenarios. Experiments on four game examples demonstrate the performance of our redesign algorithms.Future work could studydefense mechanismsto mitigate the effect of game redesign when the designer is malicious; or when the designer is one of the playerswith more knowledge than other players and willing to intentionally lose.
