When the set of points Xрқ‘ӢX is very large, we seek anefficient method that computes a small summary structure that canact as a surrogate to the full data sets and allow us to efficientlyapproximate clustering costs. These structures arecommonly in the form of subsets SвҠӮXрқ‘Ҷрқ‘ӢS\subset Xwith weights рқ’ҳвҖІsuperscriptрқ’ҳвҖІ\boldsymbol{w}^{\prime} so thatVвҖӢ(QвҲЈS,рқ’ҳвҖІ)рқ‘үconditionalрқ‘„рқ‘Ҷsuperscriptрқ’ҳвҖІV(Q\mid S,\boldsymbol{w}^{\prime}) approximates VвҖӢ(QвҲЈX,рқ’ҳ)рқ‘үconditionalрқ‘„рқ‘Ӣрқ’ҳV(Q\mid X,\boldsymbol{w}) for each Qрқ‘„Q ofsize kрқ‘ҳk.Random samples are a natural form of such structures. The challengeis, however, that we need to choose the weights carefully: A uniformsample of Xрқ‘ӢX always provide us with unbiased estimates ofclustering costs but can miss critical points and will not provide quality guarantees.

When designing summary structures, we seek to optimize the tradeoffbetween the structure size and the quality guarantees it provides.The term coresets for such summary structures was coined in the computational geometryliterature [1, 19], building on the theory ofПөitalic-Пө\epsilon-nets. Some notable coreset constructions include [25, 10, 16, 17].Early coresets constructions had bounds with high(exponential or high polynomial) dependence on someparameters (dimension, Пөitalic-Пө\epsilon, kрқ‘ҳk) and poly logarithmic dependenceon nрқ‘ӣn. The state-of-the-art asymptotic bound of OвҖӢ(kвҖӢПөвҲ’2вҖӢlogвҒЎkвҖӢlogвҒЎn)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ҳsuperscriptitalic-Пө2рқ‘ҳрқ‘ӣO(k\epsilon^{-2}\log k\log n) is claimed in [6].

A much smallersummary structure might suffice when there is structure typical in datasuch as natural clusterability (which is what weseek) and lower dimensionality than the ambient space.

It seems on the surface, however, that in order to achievestatistical guarantees on quality of the resultsone must either make explicitassumptions on the data or use the worst-case size.We show here how to avoid both these pitfalls via elegantadaptive designs.

Our main building block are novel summary structures for clustering costs based onmulti-objective probability-proportional-to-size (pps) samples[14, 12], which build on theclassic notion of sample coordination [22, 7, 26, 11].

For the task of approximate clustering, we adapt anoptimization framework over multi-objectivesamplesВ [12]. The meta algorithm is a wrapperthat inputs multi-objective pps probabilities, specified errorguarantee Пөitalic-Пө\epsilon, anda black-box (exact, approximate, bicriteria, heuristic) base clustering algorithmрқ’ңрқ’ң\mathcal{A}. Whenthe sample is much smaller than the full data set, we can expectbetter clustering quality using less computation.Our initial multi-objective pps sample providesForEachВ guarantees that apply to each estimate in isolation but not to the sample optimum. Our adaptiveapproach exploits a critical benefit of ForEach: That is,we are able to test the quality of the sampleapproximate optimizer Qрқ‘„Q returned by рқ’ңрқ’ң\mathcal{A}: If the clustering cost of VвҖӢ(QвҲЈX,рқ’ҳ)рқ‘үconditionalрқ‘„рқ‘Ӣрқ’ҳV(Q\mid X,\boldsymbol{w}) agrees withthe estimate VвҖӢ(QвҲЈS,рқ’ҳвҖІ)рқ‘үconditionalрқ‘„рқ‘Ҷsuperscriptрқ’ҳвҖІV(Q\mid S,\boldsymbol{w}^{\prime}) then we can certify that Qрқ‘„Q has similar (within (1+Пө)1italic-Пө(1+\epsilon) quality over Xрқ‘ӢX as it has over the sample Sрқ‘ҶS.Otherwise,the wrapper doubles the sample size Sрқ‘ҶS and repeats until the test is satisfied.Since the base algorithm is always at leastlinear, the total computation is dominated by that last largest samplesize we use.

Note that the only computation performed over the full data setare the OвҖӢ(k)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ҳO(k) iterations of kmeans++ that produce (M,Оұ)рқ‘Җрқӣј(M,\alpha) to whichwe apply one2all. Each such iteration performs OвҖӢ(|X|)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ӢO(|X|) distancecomputations. This is a significant gain, as even with LloydвҖҷsalgorithm (EM heuristic), each iteration is OвҖӢ(kвҖӢ|X|)рқ‘Ӯрқ‘ҳрқ‘ӢO(k|X|). A further adaptive optimization targets this initial cost:On real-world data it is often the case thatmuch fewer iterations of kmeans++ bring us to within somereasonable factor Оұрқӣј\alpha of the optimal kрқ‘ҳk-clustering. We thuspropose to adaptively perform additional kmeans++ iterations asto balance their cost with the size of the sample that we need to work with.
