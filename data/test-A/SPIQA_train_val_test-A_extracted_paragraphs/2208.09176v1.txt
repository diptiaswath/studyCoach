Consider a social network рқ’ў=(рқ’ұ,в„°)рқ’ўрқ’ұв„°\mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{V},\mathcal{E}), where each node vsвҲҲрқ’ұsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ рқ’ұv_{s}\in\mathcal{V} represents the user vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and each edge (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E} represents the friendship of two users vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}.Measuring the closeness of the friendship (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}) is an important problem and finds numerous applications in real-world scenarios, where a user vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} (called the source) only interacts with the friend vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} (called the target).For example, online gaming platforms often host friendship-enhancing events, which encourage the source vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} to invite the target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} to return to the game or play togetherВ (Luo etВ al., 2019, 2020). In this scenario, the friendship closeness measure is the backbone for understanding the invitation (resp. adoption) behavior of vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} (resp. vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}). Furthermore, this measure can be applied in recommending promising targets for the sources because the number of targets of a source could be considerable.Analogously, in the instant messaging platform WeChat, a sourceвҖҷs post can only be liked and commented on by the target in contactsВ (Zhang etВ al., 2018), thus the friendship closeness measure can also be leveraged to predict corresponding activitiesВ (Zhang etВ al., 2021; Sankar etВ al., 2020; Gomez-Rodriguez etВ al., 2011).

In the present work, we focus on the topological friendship closeness (рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}}) measure that reflects the closeness of a source-target pair (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}) on рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} rather than labels associated with them. In particular, most existing рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measuresВ (Adamic and Adar, 2003; Page etВ al., 1999; Jeh and Widom, 2002; Granovetter, 1978; Fang etВ al., 2014; Perozzi etВ al., 2014; Grover and Leskovec, 2016; Yang etВ al., 2020; Roweis and Saul, 2000; Tang etВ al., 2015; Wang etВ al., 2016) adopt the structural information between two users vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}. For example, (Fang etВ al., 2014) directly employs the tie strength of (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}), which is measured by the times of historical interactions; (Adamic and Adar, 2003) proposes several measures in terms of the common neighborhood of (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}). Furthermore, some measures preserve long and intricate paths between (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}) based on random walks, e.g., personalized PageRankВ (Page etВ al., 1999).Unfortunately, the above-said measures may render compromised results, as they ignore the group information and the potential group effect related to (vs,vt)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v_{s},v_{t}), which says a userвҖҷs belonging group can influence his/her decision.

Even though some рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measuresВ (Soundarajan and Hopcroft, 2012; Epasto etВ al., 2015; Ugander etВ al., 2012; Song etВ al., 2020; Barbieri etВ al., 2014; De etВ al., 2016) attempt to take the group effect into consideration, the involved information is still inadequate.To explain, we find that these measures typically require (i) first representing the affiliation relationship between users and groups as a bipartite graph, where users and groups are two disjoint sets of nodes and affiliation relationships are a set of edges, and (ii) then exploiting information on this bipartite graph. For example, (Ugander etВ al., 2012) proposes structural diversity to measure the number of adjacent groups for a given user; (Song etВ al., 2020; Barbieri etВ al., 2014; De etВ al., 2016) utilize edge weights between users and groups; (Soundarajan and Hopcroft, 2012; Epasto etВ al., 2015) measures the commonality of groups between two users on this bipartite graph. However, in these works, contracting a group into a node makes more detailed group information imperceptible, e.g., connectivity.

To mitigate the deficiencies of existing measures, we propose to explore the group effect by leveraging the social identity theory (рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}), which is a fundamental concept in social psychology and is widely applied in domains of human healthВ (Scheepers and Ellemers, 2019), team sportsВ (Rees etВ al., 2015), computer-supported cooperative worksВ (Seering etВ al., 2018) and fake news detectionВ (Shu etВ al., 2017).рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} describes that a target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} tends to endorse attitudes and behaviors of groups of users, who are assessed as us in vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}вҖҷs cognition.In other words, given a target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} and an identified group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C} of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}, vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} is more likely to adopt the invitation from the source vsвҲҲрқ’һsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ рқ’һv_{s}\in\mathcal{C}. Unfortunately, the identification process is difficult to be quantified due to perceptual factors.To this end, we seamlessly reify the factors of рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} into рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures, which consider a groupвҖҷs local and global information. For example, by modeling the social network as a physical system, we propose to measure the attractive spring-like force related to a group, in which the information of group connectivity and in-group user homogeneity are naturally incorporated.In contrast, previous рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures only consider similarities of group membershipsВ (Soundarajan and Hopcroft, 2012; Epasto etВ al., 2015).

We experimentally evaluate the proposed рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based solution against 8 representative competitors on 3 real-world online game datasets.In particular, we demonstrate that the proposed solution outperforms all competitors in terms of AUC, accuracy, and F1 score while predicting target adoption and source invitation behaviors on the tested dataset. Besides, we detailedly analyze the relations between user behaviors and рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} factors, and the importance of these factors. At last, we deploy our solution to the online target recommendation, which achieves up to 34.2% improvement over the best treatment in the corresponding evaluation metric.

To summarize, we make the following contributions in this work:вҖўWe propose new рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures based on рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} in social psychology, which preserve both local and global information of groups.вҖўWe conduct extensive experiments and analysis to demonstrate the superiority of proposed рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures over state-of-the-art competitors while predicting user behaviors.вҖўWe deploy the presented solution to the online target recommendation, which achieves significant improvement.

In this section, we first elaborate on the problem of measuring the topological friendship closeness (рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}}), followed by the illustration of main competitors and downstream tasks that рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures are to solve in this work. At last, we introduce the background of the social identity theory.

Let рқ’ў=(рқ’ұ,в„°)рқ’ўрқ’ұв„°\mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{V},\mathcal{E}) be a social network, where рқ’ұрқ’ұ\mathcal{V} is a set of users (called nodes) and в„°в„°\mathcal{E} is a set of friendships (called edges).We assume that the friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E} is directed and is associated with an edge weight ws,tвҲҲ(0,1]subscriptрқ‘Өрқ‘ рқ‘Ў01w_{s,t}\in(0,1].Given a directed edge (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, we call vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} (resp. vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}) the source (resp. target) neighbor of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} (resp. vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s}).Given a node vtвҲҲрқ’ұsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўрқ’ұv_{t}\in\mathcal{V}, the source neighborhood of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} consists of: (i) vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} itself; (ii) the node set рқ’©tsubscriptрқ’©рқ‘Ў\mathcal{N}_{t}, which contains sources of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}; (iii) the edge set в„°tsubscriptв„°рқ‘Ў\mathcal{E}_{t}, which contains the edge (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}; (iv) the edge set в„ӣtsubscriptв„ӣрқ‘Ў\mathcal{R}_{t}, which contains the edge (vs,vk)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ҳв„°(v_{s},v_{k})\in\mathcal{E} between sources vs,vkвҲҲрқ’©tsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ҳsubscriptрқ’©рқ‘Ўv_{s},v_{k}\in\mathcal{N}_{t}.

Recall in SectionВ 1 that, given an input social network рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G}, the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure reflects the topological closeness for each (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}.Given a source-target pair (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, the goal of our present work is to design the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure, which leverages the structural information of the group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C}, where vs,vtвҲҲрқ’һsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўрқ’һv_{s},v_{t}\in\mathcal{C}. For simplicity, we assume that vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} are only co-located in one group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C}.As for the partitioning of groups, it relies on the social identity theory and will be illustrated in SectionВ 3.

In what follows, we briefly elaborate on the main ideas of competing рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures in the present work. For better clarity, we call a measure as the group-level рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure if considering the group information, and the individual-level рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure otherwise.Specifically, we select 5 representative individual-level measures: tie strengthВ (Tang etВ al., 2015; Fang etВ al., 2014), number of common neighborsВ (Tang etВ al., 2015; Adamic and Adar, 2003), personalized PageRankВ (Page etВ al., 1999), cosine and euclidean similarity of Node2Vec vectorsВ (Grover and Leskovec, 2016); 3 commonly-used group-level measures: structural diversityВ (Ugander etВ al., 2012), user-group tie strengthВ (Song etВ al., 2020) and group edge densityВ (Purohit etВ al., 2014).It is worth noting that the selected individual-level measures are also known as proximitiesВ (Cai etВ al., 2018; Tang etВ al., 2015), which can reflect the topological closeness for all node pairs. Furthermore, these рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures are widely applied in Tencent gaming platformsВ (Luo etВ al., 2019, 2020).

Tie strength. Given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, the tie strength equals to the edge weight ws,tsubscriptрқ‘Өрқ‘ рқ‘Ўw_{s,t}В (Tang etВ al., 2015; Fang etВ al., 2014).

Number of common neighbors. Given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, this measure counts the number of common neighborsВ (Tang etВ al., 2015; Adamic and Adar, 2003) of source vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}, which can reflect the similarity of two nodeвҖҷs local structure.

Personalized PageRank. Given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, this measure aims to preserve intricate topological relations between nodes by performing random walks. In particular, the personalized PageRankВ (Page etВ al., 1999) from vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} to vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} is defined as the probability that a random walk with restart (RWR)В (Tong etВ al., 2006) originating from vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} stops at vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}. The corresponding RWR starts from the source vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and, in each step, chooses to (i) either terminate at the current node with probability Оұрқӣј\alpha, (ii) or navigate to a random target neighbor of the current node with the remaining 1вҲ’Оұ1рқӣј1-\alpha probability.

Similarity of Node2Vec vectors. As a node embedding methodВ (Cai etВ al., 2018), Node2VecВ (Grover and Leskovec, 2016) proposes to represent each node vsвҲҲрқ’ўsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ рқ’ўv_{s}\in\mathcal{G} as a compact vector, which preserves the structural information in the vicinity of vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s}. By this method, given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} can be measured by the cosine similarity or euclidean distance between the Node2Vec vectors of vssubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ v_{s} and vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}. Compared with the prior work DeepWalkВ (Perozzi etВ al., 2014), the core contribution of Node2Vec is that it takes two-order random walks as inputs for training. Compared with the RWR, the two-order random walk will not terminate until a pre-defined length limit. Moreover, given the current node vjsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—v_{j}, the target neighbors of vjsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—v_{j} are first divided into the following three categories: (i) the source neighbor visubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–v_{i} that was visited in the previous step, (ii) vksubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ҳv_{k} that is the common target neighbor of visubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–v_{i} or (iii) otherwise. Then different (resp. same) transition probabilities are assigned to nodes across (resp. inside) each category. At last, the next node of this walk is selected in terms of the biased probability.

Structural diversity.Ugander et al. are the first to propose the concept of structural diversityВ (Ugander etВ al., 2012), which is further applied for influence analysisВ (Fang etВ al., 2014; Zhang etВ al., 2021; Su etВ al., 2020; Qiu etВ al., 2016). Specifically, given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E}, the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure based on the structural diversity equals to the number of weakly connected components (see DefinitionВ 2.1) in a subgraph, which is derived from vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}вҖҷs source neighbors affected by a specific event and their relationships. This work (Ugander etВ al., 2012) points out that a more extensive structural diversity of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} indicates a higher probability that vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} will be influenced.

User-group tie strength.Given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E} and a group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C} where vs,vtвҲҲрқ’һsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўрқ’һv_{s},v_{t}\in\mathcal{C}, the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure based on the user-group tie strengthВ (Song etВ al., 2020; Barbieri etВ al., 2014; De etВ al., 2016) equals to the summation of tie strength between vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} and other members in рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C}.

Group edge density.Given a friendship (vs,vt)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўв„°(v_{s},v_{t})\in\mathcal{E} and a group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C} where vs,vtвҲҲрқ’һsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘ subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўрқ’һv_{s},v_{t}\in\mathcal{C}, the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure based on the group edge density is defined as the fraction of the summation of edge weights in рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C} over the number of all possible edges in рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C}, which is also applied to analyze group dynamicsВ (Purohit etВ al., 2014; Qiu etВ al., 2016).

Formulation.Turner and Tajfel define social identity as вҖҳthe individualвҖҷs knowledge that he/she belongs to certain social groups together with some emotional and value significance to him of this group membershipвҖҷВ (Tajfel and Turner, 2004). In other words, social identity theory (рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}) emphasizes how the target userвҖҷs belonging group affects his/her behavior.Given a social network GрқҗәG and a target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} is formulated by three cognitive processes: categorization, identification and comparisonВ (Tajfel and Turner, 2004). Specifically, the categorization is a preprocessing step, which partitions the users of рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} into several candidate social groups w.r.t vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}. In the identification stage, vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} will identify him/herself as a member of a certain candidate group. Finally, the comparison stage says that, for the sake of self-esteem, vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} tends to shift the sense of belonging based on group social standing.

Besides, Leach et al.В (Leach etВ al., 2008) extensively summarize existing social perceptual factors as: satisfactionВ (Tajfel and Turner, 2004; Cameron, 2004), solidarityВ (Cameron, 2004; Doosje etВ al., 1998), centralityВ (Cameron, 2004; Doosje etВ al., 1998; Brewer and Gardner, 1996), self-stereotypingВ (Oakes etВ al., 1994; Simon, 1992) and in-group homogeneityВ (Oakes etВ al., 1994; Simon, 1992).Regarding the comparison stage, by definition, the social standing of a group is also recognized as a pivotal factorВ (Tajfel and Turner, 2004).

In this part, we devise the formulation for рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} factors and illustrate their rationales compared with existing group-level measures.

Inspired from the structural diversityВ (Ugander etВ al., 2012), we utilize the number of candidate groups and the size of each group of a target to represent the statistical factors of multi-membership and inclusiveness, respectively. In particular, given a graph рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} and a target user vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}, we define the multi-membership of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} as the number of candidate groups (#вҖӢрқ–ўрқ–ў#рқ–ўрқ–ў\#\mathsf{CC}) consisting of all source neighbors of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}, and the inclusiveness of a given group рқ’һрқ’һ\mathcal{C} as the group size (рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}}), i.e., the cardinality |рқ’һ|рқ’һ|\mathcal{C}|.In contrast, the prior work of structural diversityВ (Ugander etВ al., 2012) focuses on the groups with source neighbors who have sent the invitation only.

The rationales are as follows. First, before the release time of a given event, invitation behaviors of source users are unknown, and hence corresponding ego networks fail to be extracted. Second, even though the ego networks may be constructed by exploiting source behaviors from historical events, however, the invitation behaviors are only triggered by a small number of source users, which renders a much sparser ego network with many singleton рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ—Ңрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ—Ң{\mathsf{CCs}} (i.e., one inside member)В (Ugander etВ al., 2012) and makes less group information revealed.For example, Figure 2 shows the distributions of averaged рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}} size of invited target users on FPS dataset, in which averaged рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}} size of a target vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t} equals the fraction of the number of all (or inviting) source neighbors over ##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}} of vtsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘Ўv_{t}. This part proposes user-group tightness (рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}) to seamlessly encapsulate solidarity and self-stereotyping factors, and proposes intra-group tightness (рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}) to quantify the in-group homogeneity factor.More concrete, given a graph рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} and a node pair (vi,vj)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—(v_{i},v_{j}), we use the tie strength wi,jsubscriptрқ‘Өрқ‘–рқ‘—w_{i,j} (resp. the similarity Оҙi,jsubscriptрқӣҝрқ‘–рқ‘—\delta_{i,j}) as the backbone for the solidarity factor (resp. self-stereotyping and homogeneity factors). W.l.o.g, the similarity score Оҙi,jвҲҲ[0,1]subscriptрқӣҝрқ‘–рқ‘—01\delta_{i,j}\in[0,1] is defined as the cosine similarity of the Node2Vec representationsВ (Grover and Leskovec, 2016) of (vi,vj)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—(v_{i},v_{j}), which is further normalized to the range of [0,1]01[0,1] by the min-max scaler.In what follows, we formally define рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}} and рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}} as follows.

The intuition behind рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}} (resp. рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}) can be interpreted as the averaged attractive spring-like forces between the target user and other group members (resp. among other group members), if the source neighborhood of the target user is abstracted as a force system. This abstraction is widely adopted in graph drawingВ (Fruchterman and Reingold, 1991; Gansner etВ al., 2004).Take Figure 3(a) as an example. The source neighborhood of v1subscriptрқ‘Ј1v_{1} is represented as a spring network where each edge is assumed as a spring, then the tie strength wi,jsubscriptрқ‘Өрқ‘–рқ‘—w_{i,j} can be regarded as the stiffness constant (i.e., physical strength) of the spring on (vi,vj)вҲҲв„°subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—в„°(v_{i},v_{j})\in\mathcal{E}.Let li,jвҲҲ[0,1]subscriptрқ‘ҷрқ‘–рқ‘—01l_{i,j}\in[0,1] be the natural length (when no force is exerted) of a given spring on (vi,vj)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—(v_{i},v_{j}), which is equivalent to the theoretical distance between visubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–v_{i} and vjsubscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—v_{j} on the graph, as clarified inВ (Gansner etВ al., 2004). Suppose that all springs are set to initial length 1, then the similarity Оҙi,j=1вҲ’li,jsubscriptрқӣҝрқ‘–рқ‘—1subscriptрқ‘ҷрқ‘–рқ‘—\delta_{i,j}=1-l_{i,j} indicates the displacement of the spring on (vi,vj)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—(v_{i},v_{j}).According to HookeвҖҷs law, as shown in Figure 3(b), wi,jвӢ…Оҙi,jвӢ…subscriptрқ‘Өрқ‘–рқ‘—subscriptрқӣҝрқ‘–рқ‘—w_{i,j}\cdot\delta_{i,j} is equal to the attractive force exerted by (vi,vj)subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘–subscriptрқ‘Јрқ‘—(v_{i},v_{j}).In contrast, group-level competitors in SectionВ 2.2 only consider the tie strengthВ (Song etВ al., 2020; Barbieri etВ al., 2014; De etВ al., 2016) or the in-group connectivity by the edge densityВ (Purohit etВ al., 2014; Qiu etВ al., 2016), whereas the homogeneity of group members is ignored.

In this part, we first elaborate on the experimental settings, and then evaluate the performance of behavior prediction and target recommendation tasks by employing the proposed measures.All of our experiments are conducted on an in-house cluster consisting of hundreds of machines, each of which runs CentOS, and has 16GB memory and 12 Intel Xeon Processor E5-2670 CPU cores.

Datasets.We use 3 friendship-enhancing event datasets from TencentвҖҷs first person shooter (FPS) and multiuser online battle arena (MOBA) games.A given event, whose procedure is explained in SectionВ 2.3, takes the snapshot of рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} before the release time as the input graph, since рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} for a particular online game evolves when new users are registered, or friendships are modified.We select events FPS and MOBA-A as datasets to evaluate the performance on target adoption and source invitation behavior predictions, and events MOBA-A and MOBA-B to evaluate the performance on target recommendation.The statistics of the graph snapshot, source, and target sets for events are summarized in Table 2.All datasets have been anonymized to avoid any leakage of privacy information.

Competitors.We compare the proposed рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based measures with 8 representative prior ones as mentioned in SectionВ 2.2: (i) individual-level measures: tie strength (рқ–ірқ—Ӯрқ–ҫрқ–ірқ—Ӯрқ–ҫ{\mathsf{Tie}})В (Fang etВ al., 2014), number of common neighbors (рқ–ўрқ–®рқ–¬рқ–ўрқ–®рқ–¬{\mathsf{COM}})В (Adamic and Adar, 2003), рқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{PPR}}В (Page etВ al., 1999), cosine and euclidean similarity between Node2Vec representations (рқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–өрқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–ө{\mathsf{N2V}}(рқ–јрқ—Ҳрқ—Ңрқ–јрқ—Ҳрқ—Ң{\mathsf{cos}}) and рқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–өрқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–ө{\mathsf{N2V}}(рқ–ҫрқ—Һрқ–јрқ–ҫрқ—Һрқ–ј{\mathsf{euc}}))В (Grover and Leskovec, 2016); (ii) group-level measures: structural diversity (##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}})В (Ugander etВ al., 2012), user-group tie strength (рқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{GT}})В (Barbieri etВ al., 2014) and group edge density (рқ–Ұрқ–Јрқ–Ұрқ–Ј{\mathsf{GD}})В (Purohit etВ al., 2014). All measures are computed based on the network structure as introduced in Table 2.Notice in TencentвҖҷs MOBA and FPS online gaming platforms that the edge weight between a pair of users is described by the intimacy score, which records the number of historical activities/interactions from one to the other, e.g., co-playing, gifting, etc.For a fair comparison, we employ intimacy values to measure the tie strength for all related рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based measures and competitors. Furthermore, we employ distributed frameworks inВ (Lin, 2019) and В (Lin, 2021; Lin etВ al., 2020) to compute рқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{PPR}} and рқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–өрқ–ӯрқҹӨрқ–ө{\mathsf{N2V}}-based measures, respectively, and set the final embedding dimension of the latent vector to 200.To accomplish downstream tasks, we treat our proposal called рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} (including ##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPPR}}, рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}, and рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}) or competing measures as input features forВ рқ–·рқ–Ұрқ–Ўрқ—Ҳрқ—Ҳрқ—Ңрқ—Қрқ–·рқ–Ұрқ–Ўрқ—Ҳрқ—Ҳрқ—Ңрқ—Қ{\mathsf{XGBoost}} as mentioned in SectionВ 3, in which the parameters are set following the original paperВ (Chen and Guestrin, 2016).

In this section, we separately train two sets of рқ–·рқ–Ұрқ–Ўрқ—Ҳрқ—Ҳрқ—Ңрқ—Қрқ–·рқ–Ұрқ–Ўрқ—Ҳрқ—Ҳрқ—Ңрқ—Қ{\mathsf{XGBoost}} models to evaluate the performance of the aforementioned methods in predicting target adoption and source invitation behaviors.Notice that user behaviors can be interfered with by the underlying exposure strategies, which will be illustrated in SectionВ 5.3.To eliminate the bias, we focus on randomly exposed source-target pairs, in which we select the pairs with invitation or adoption behaviors as positive data instances and also randomly select an equal number of pairs without behaviors as negative data instancesВ (Zhang etВ al., 2021). Finally, we obtain 116.3 (resp. 12.5) thousands of data instances for invitation (resp. adoption) prediction on FPS, and 776.4 (resp. 39.1) thousands of instances for invitation (resp. adoption) prediction on MOBA-A, where 80% are used for training and 20% for testing.To evaluate the effectiveness, we repeat each approach 3 times and report the average result under conventional metrics: Area Under Curve (AUC), accuracy and F1 score.In the following experiments, we compare our proposal called рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} with each competitor, every single dimension of рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} and their variants in order, followed by analyzing each рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} dimension. Note that we first show the results about target adoptions and then those about source invitations, as the primary goal of рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} to understand how targets are influenced.

Overall Performance.As illustrated in Table 3, our proposed рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} consistently outperforms other competitors in both FPS and MOBA-A datasets. Specifically, while predicting target adoption behaviors, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} is 11.8%, 3.5%, 2.5% (resp. 23.2% 12.6% 6.7%) better than the best competitor in terms of AUC, accuracy, and F1 score in FPS (resp. MOBA-A). For invitation behaviors, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} is 16.2%, 12.3%, 16.7% (resp. 12.0%, 8.1%, and 8.7%) better than the best competitor in terms of AUC, accuracy, and F1 score in FPS (resp. MOBA-A).The above results reflect that involving group information by рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} is necessary for understanding user behaviors in friendship-enhancing events.Regarding competitors, рқ–ірқ—Ӯрқ–ҫрқ–ірқ—Ӯрқ–ҫ{\mathsf{Tie}} is the best-performing competitor in terms of AUC and accuracy scores during the adoption prediction, which indicates that source-target pairs with more historical interactions (i.e., intimacy) are more likely to interact again. Regarding the structural diversity (##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}}), we evaluate its performance in the ablation study, as it is a dimension of рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}.

However, ##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}}-рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}} are still worse than some single dimensions (i.e., рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}} or рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}) and the proposed рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}.Regarding the reasons for the performance of each dimension in рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}, we leave them in the analysis parts.

Factor formulation.As mentioned in SectionВ 4, other possible formulations of proposed measures exist. Thus, except for the averaged aggregation operation and cosine similarity, we also evaluate the performance of variant measures with summation operation and euclidean similarity (denoted as sum and euc, respectively) in Table 4. To derive the euclidean similarity, we first normalize the euclidean distance between each node pair by the min-max scaler as the cosine similarity does, and then take the one minus normalized distance as the corresponding similarity.In particular, employing the summation operation is equivalent to hybrid the original measure with the factor of рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}}, which may dampen the original measure (i.e., рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPPR}}, рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}} or рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}) because of the weak correlation between рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}} and user behaviors, which will be shown in FiguresВ 4-5. For example, the result quality of the summation of рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}} is consistently worse than that of рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}} on both behaviors and datasets.As for different similarity measures w.r.t node representations, the effectiveness of utilizing cosine and euclidean similarity is comparable. In particular, the F1 scores of euclidean-based рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}} and рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}} are usually better than those of cosine-based. Meanwhile, negative results are reported regarding AUC and accuracy scores.

Conversion probability conditioned on рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і\boldsymbol{\mathsf{SIT}} dimensions.We investigate the relation between user behaviors and the aforementioned рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based measures: ##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}}, рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPPR}}, рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}, and рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}. Due to space limitations, we only report the analysis results on FPS, and the rest datasets derive similar results.Following the setting ofВ (Fang etВ al., 2014), we employ the density-based discretization to convert the scores of рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}-based measures into five different levels, where a higher level indicates a larger score.Moreover, we use conversion probabilityВ (Fang etВ al., 2014; Ugander etВ al., 2012) to evaluate how the user acts. In particular, given a level of certain рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} measure, the adoption (resp. invitation) conversion probability is the fraction of source-target pairs existing adoption (resp. invitation) behaviors over all pairs in this level.

We report the adoption and invitation conversion probabilities w.r.t each measure in Figure 4 and Figure 5, respectively. We can find an evident correlation between each behavior and each proposed measure except for рқ–Ұрқ–Ірқ–Ұрқ–І{\mathsf{GS}}.In terms of ##\#рқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ўрқ–ў{\mathsf{CC}}, contradicting the well-accepted insight of structural diversityВ (Ugander etВ al., 2012; Su etВ al., 2020; Fang etВ al., 2014), FiguresВ 4(a) and В 5(a) show that the target user with less number of candidate groups (i.e., a less diverse neighborhood) is more likely to be invited and further adopt this invitation. Recently, this phenomenon also emerged from other user behaviors, especially in Tencent instant messaging platformВ (Qiu etВ al., 2016; Zhang etВ al., 2021), but the underlying reason is still unclear. In contrast, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} can explain this as that a greater multi-membership makes the social identification phase of a target challenging due to the potential role conflictВ (Marks, 1977).Regarding the effect of group size, we can find in FiguresВ 4(b) and В 5(b) that the engagement willingness of users will be facilitated as the group scale increases, but subsequently hurdled if still going up. Notice that the source-target pair inside a medium-size group (i.e., level 3) is more likely to send an invitation and respond.

Recall in SectionВ 4 that рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}} and рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPPR}} measure the factors of social standing and centrality from the global and personalized view, respectively. The results in FiguresВ 4(d) and 5(d) show that the target user is more likely to choose a relatively important group, which confirms the claim of the centrality factor in рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}. However, FiguresВ 4(c) and 5(c) illustrate opposing results w.r.t рқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұрқ–Ұрқ–Ҝрқ–ұ{\mathsf{GPR}}. The reason is that, by definition, a user with a high PageRank value tends to have more neighbors, which consequently means each target friend has less probability of being exposed by the random algorithm.

To evaluate the tie strength and similarity parts in рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}, we separate them as рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-wрқ‘Өw and рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-Оҙрқӣҝ\delta respectively, in which рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-wрқ‘Өw measures the averaged intimacy values and рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-Оҙрқӣҝ\delta measures the averaged cosine similarity between the Node2Vec representations. The same operation is also performed for рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}}. As illustrated in FiguresВ 4(e) and 5(e), the behavior conversion probabilities increase as the levels of рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-wрқ‘Өw and рқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–ҙрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{UGT}}-Оҙрқӣҝ\delta go up, which match the factors of solidarity and self-stereotyping in рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}}. The same tendency for рқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–ірқ–Ёрқ–Ұрқ–і{\mathsf{IGT}} is shown in FiguresВ 4(f) and 5(f). This part briefly reviews other рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures and the related sociological theory.In particular,prior рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures can be explained by either selection or influence mechanism in the homophily principleВ (Easley etВ al., 2010).The selection indicates that people tend to form friendships with others with similar characteristics. In contrast, the influence can be treated as the inverse of selection, claiming that people may modify characteristics to conform to their friends. We categorize рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures based on the taxonomy of selection and influence.

Regarding the selection, conventional рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measures utilize the variant based on common neighborhood, e.g., Adamic/Adar scoreВ (Adamic and Adar, 2003). Furthermore, the selection can be measured by the similarity between node embeddings, e.g., (Roweis and Saul, 2000; Tang etВ al., 2015; Wang etВ al., 2016; Perozzi etВ al., 2014; Grover and Leskovec, 2016; Yang etВ al., 2020), which essentially rely on the factorization of a matrix of the рқҡғрқҷөрқҷІрқҡғрқҷөрқҷІ{\mathtt{TFC}} measure as mentioned in SectionВ 2.2В (Qiu etВ al., 2018).Regarding social influence, the related measures mainly focus on the local structure of a user. BurtВ (Burt, 1987) proposes to model social influence from cohesion and structural equivalence perspectives. In particular, cohesion describes the direct influence measured by tie strength and the number of influenced friendsВ (Granovetter, 1978; Fang etВ al., 2014). The structural equivalence describes the indirect influence between users and is measured by tie embeddednessВ (Easley etВ al., 2010).Recently, many learning-based methodsВ (Gomez-Rodriguez etВ al., 2011; Sankar etВ al., 2020; Li etВ al., 2020) are proposed to predict the influence probability based on historical cascades, among whichВ (Li etВ al., 2020) leverages another psychological concept called conformity to measure the social influence.

In contrast, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} is a group-level psychological framework, in which each dimension can reflect either selection or influence in the homophily principle. For instance, the factors of self-stereotyping and in-group homogeneity are coherent with the selection mechanism. Therefore, рқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–ірқ–Ірқ–Ёрқ–і{\mathsf{SIT}} can also provide theoretical support for the aforementioned measures and their integration, e.g., (Sankar etВ al., 2020).

In this work, we propose measures for friendship closeness based on the social identity theory, a fundamental concept in social psychology, and conduct extensive experiments and analysis on 3 TencentвҖҷs online gaming datasets.Compared with 8 state-of-the-art methods, our proposal achieves the highest effectiveness on both behavior prediction and target recommendation tasks. Regarding future works, we will employ presented measures for other scenarios, e.g., modeling the cascade of social influence and suggesting strangers to enrich usersвҖҷ friendships in the who-to-follow service.
