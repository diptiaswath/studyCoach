Positive-unlabeled (PU) learning can date back to 1990s [9, 8, 24], and there has been a surge ofinterest in this learning scenario in recent years because of the difficulty to annotate large-scale datasets [32, 11, 10, 5, 19, 31, 33, 20, 2, 14, 1, 39, 41].It is also fallen into different applications, such asknowledge-base completion [13, 28],text classification [23, 25], and medical diagnosis [6, 42].

PU learning can be divided into two different settings based on different data generation processes.The first setting is called censoring PU learning [12], which follows a one-sample configuration. Specifically, a sample S𝑆S is randomly drawn from the unlabeled data distribution Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}}, and a positive sample Spsubscript𝑆pS_{\mathrm{p}} is then distilled from it, i.e., randomly selecting some positive instances contained in the unlabeled data to be the positive sample. The second setting is called case-control PU learning [21]. In this setting, a positive sample Sp={xi}i=1ksubscript𝑆psubscriptsuperscriptsubscript𝑥𝑖𝑘𝑖1S_{\mathrm{p}}=\{x_{i}\}^{k}_{i=1} is randomly drawn from the positive class-conditional distribution Pp=P​(X|Y=1)subscript𝑃p𝑃conditional𝑋𝑌1P_{\mathrm{p}}=P(X|Y=1), and an unlabeled sample Su={xi}i=k+1nsubscript𝑆usubscriptsuperscriptsubscript𝑥𝑖𝑛𝑖𝑘1S_{\mathrm{u}}=\{x_{i}\}^{n}_{i=k+1} is randomly drawn from the unlabeled data distribution Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}}. Because case-control PU learning is more general than censoring PU learning [29], therefore, we will focus on the setting of case-control PU learning.

Under the setting of case-control PU learning, a lot of classification methods have been proposed [32, 11, 10, 5, 33, 20, 2, 22, 36, 14].However, the class-prior estimation (CPE) [12, 19, 31, 5, 20] has received less attention.Formally, CPE is defined as a problem of estimating π=P​(y=1)∈(0,1)𝜋𝑃𝑦101\pi=P(y=1)\in(0,1) given a sample from the marginal distribution Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}} and a sample from positive class-conditional distribution Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}}. The marginal distribution Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}} is mixed with both positive and negative class-conditional distributions, i.e., Pu=π​Pp+(1−π)​Pn.subscript𝑃u𝜋subscript𝑃p1𝜋subscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{u}}=\pi P_{\mathrm{p}}+(1-\pi)P_{\mathrm{n}}.CPE acts as a crucial building block for state-of-the-art PU classification methods, and it is essential to build statistically-consistent PU classifiers [11, 34, 19, 21]. The formulation of these classification methods involves the class-prior π𝜋\pi, but π𝜋\pi is usually unknown in practice. If π𝜋\pi is poorly estimated, the classification accuracy of the state-of-the-art PU classification methods [11, 10, 21] could be degraded.

The mixture proportion estimation (MPE) is closely related to CPE [4, 34]. In the setting of MPE, there is a mixture distributionF=(1−κ∗)​G+κ∗​H,𝐹1superscript𝜅𝐺superscript𝜅𝐻F=(1-\kappa^{*})G+\kappa^{*}H,(1)where H𝐻H and G𝐺G are called component distributions. Given the samples randomly drawn from F𝐹F and H𝐻H, respectively, MPE aims to estimate the maximum proportion κ∗∈(0,1)superscript𝜅01\kappa^{*}\in(0,1) of H𝐻H in F𝐹F. Thereby, if the maximum proportion κ∗superscript𝜅\kappa^{*} is identical to the class-prior π𝜋\pi, the MPE methods can be employed to obtain π𝜋\pi by letting Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}} and Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}} be the mixture distribution F𝐹F and the component distribution H𝐻H, respectively; otherwise, the MPE methods cannot be employed. To the best of our knowledge, most of state-of-the-art CPE methods [4, 26, 34, 31, 19] are based on MPE, which do not rely on assumptions that the data are drawn from a given parametric family of probability distributions (i.e., they are distributional-assumption-free methods).

To let these distributional-assumption-free methods can be used to identify class-prior π𝜋\pi, κ∗superscript𝜅\kappa^{*} must be identical to the class-prior π𝜋\pi. The irreducibility assumption [4] has been proposed to make them identical, which is employed by all these CPE methods implicitly or explicitly, to the best of our knowledge.It assumes that the support of the positive class-conditional distribution Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}} is not contained in the support of the negative class-conditional distribution Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}}.However, it is strong and hard to be verified in PU learning, since Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}} is a latent distribution, such that we do not have any prior knowledge about it. Additionally, since the applications of PU learning are diverse [16, 3], it is hard to guarantee that the support of Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}} is not in the support of Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}}.

If the irreducibility assumption cannot be satisfied, the existing distributional-assumption-free CPE methods will suffer from an overestimation of π𝜋\pi. When the irreducibility assumption is not held, how to improve the estimations of distributional-assumption-free PU learning methods is challenging but useful.

Because the irreducibility assumption is impossible to check without making any assumption on Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}}. Thereby, in this paper, we rethink those CPE methods and propose a novel method called Regrouping CPE (ReCPE) which improves the estimations of the current PU learning methods without irreducibility assumption.The main idea of our method is that, instead of estimating the maximum proportion of Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}} in Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}}, we build a new CPE problem by creating a new auxiliary distribution Pp′subscript𝑃superscriptp′P_{\mathrm{p^{\prime}}} always guaranteeing the irreducibility assumption. Then we use the existing CPE method to obtain the maximum proportion of Pp′subscript𝑃superscriptp′P_{\mathrm{p^{\prime}}} in Pusubscript𝑃uP_{\mathrm{u}}, which is denoted by π′superscript𝜋′\pi^{\prime}.We show that, with both theoretical analyses and experimental validations, when the irreducibility assumption holds, our ReCPE method does not affect the prediction of the existing estimators; when the irreducibility assumption does not hold, our method will help the current estimators have less estimation bias, which could improve the performances of PU classification tasks. In this section, we propose a general method named regrouping for CPE (ReCPE). We discuss how to theoretically and empirically mitigate the overestimation problem of the class-prior π𝜋\pi.

In general, it is impossible to verify the irreducibility assumption for CPE. To check the assumption, we need to make Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}} itself to be observable and verify that whether the distribution Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}} is a mixture containing the distribution Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}}, which obviously contradicts the setting of PU learning.However, in practice, the irreducibility assumption may not hold for many real-world problems, because the negative class is diverse [16, 3] in PU learning.If the assumption does not hold, Pnsubscript𝑃nP_{\mathrm{n}} is said to be reducible to Ppsubscript𝑃pP_{\mathrm{p}}, and distributional-assumption-free CPE methods will introduce an estimation bias.

According to Proposition 1, if the irreducibility assumption does not hold, then there exists β>0𝛽0\beta>0.
