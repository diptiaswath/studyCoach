Knowledge graphs (KGs) are directed graphs where nodes represent entities and (labeled) edges represent the types of relationships among entities. Each edge in a KG corresponds to a fact and can be represented as a tuple such as(рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’,рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫ,рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ)рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ(\mathsf{Mary},\mathsf{Liked},\mathsf{God~{}Father}) where рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’\mathsf{Mary} and рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ\mathsf{God~{}Father} are called the head and tail entities respectively and рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫрқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫ\mathsf{Liked} is a relation.An important problem, known as KG completion, is to infer new facts from a KG based on the existing ones. This problem has been extensively studied for static KGs (see [46, 62, 44] for a summary).KG embedding approaches have offered state-of-the-art results for KG completion on several benchmarks. These approaches map each entity and each relation type to a hidden representation and compute a score for each tuple by applying a score function to these representations.Different approaches differ in how they map the entities and relation types to hidden representations and in their score functions.

To capture the temporal aspect of the facts, KG edges are typically associated with a timestamp or time interval; e.g., (рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’,рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫ,рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ,1995)рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ1995(\mathsf{Mary},\mathsf{Liked},\mathsf{God~{}Father},1995). However, KG embedding approaches have been mostly designed for static KGs ignoring the temporal aspect.Recent work has shown a substantial boost in performance by extending these approaches to utilize time [21, 10, 41, 16]. The proposed extensions are mainly through computing a hidden representation for each timestamp and extending the score functions to utilize timestamp representations as well as entity and relation representations.

In this paper, we develop models for temporal KG completion (TKGC) based on an intuitive assumption: to provide a score for, e.g., (рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’,рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫ,рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ,рқҹЈрқҹ«рқҹ«рқҹ§)рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–«рқ—Ӯрқ—„рқ–ҫрқ–Ҫрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ1995(\mathsf{Mary},\mathsf{Liked},\mathsf{God~{}Father},\mathsf{1995}), one needs to know рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’\mathsf{Mary}вҖҷs and рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ\mathsf{God~{}Father}вҖҷs features on рқҹЈрқҹ«рқҹ«рқҹ§1995\mathsf{1995}; providing a score based on their current features may be misleading. That is because рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’рқ–¬рқ–әрқ—Ӣрқ—’\mathsf{Mary}вҖҷs personality and the sentiment towards рқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–ҪвҖӢрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣрқ–Ұрқ—Ҳрқ–Ҫрқ–Ҙрқ–әрқ—Қрқ—Ғрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣ\mathsf{God~{}Father} may have been quite different on рқҹЈрқҹ«рқҹ«рқҹ§1995\mathsf{1995} as compared to now. Consequently, learning a static representation for each entity вҖ“ as is done by existing approaches вҖ“ may be sub-optimal as such a representation only captures the entity features at the current time, or an aggregation of entity features during time.

To provide entity features at any given time, we define entity embedding as a function which takes an entity and a timestamp as input and provides a hidden representation for the entity at that time. Inspired by diachronic word embeddings, we call our proposed embedding diachronic embedding (DE). DE is model-agnostic: any static KG embedding model can be potentially extended to TKGC by leveraging DE. We prove that combining DE with SimplE [25] results in a fully expressive model for TKGC. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first TKGC model with a proof of fully expressiveness. We show the merit of our model onsubsets of ICEWS [5] andGDELT [38] datasets.

Temporal Knowledge Graph (Completion): Let рқ’ұрқ’ұ\mathcal{V} be a finite set of entities, в„ӣв„ӣ\mathcal{R} be a finite set of relation types, and рқ’Ҝрқ’Ҝ\mathcal{T} be a finite set of timestamps.Let рқ’ІвҠӮрқ’ұГ—в„ӣГ—рқ’ұГ—рқ’Ҝрқ’Ірқ’ұв„ӣрқ’ұрқ’Ҝ\mathcal{W}\subset\mathcal{V}\times\mathcal{R}\times\mathcal{V}\times\mathcal{T} represent the set of all temporal tuples (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қ(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t}) that are facts (i.e. true in a world), where рқ—Ҹ,рқ—ҺвҲҲрқ’ұрқ—Ҹрқ—Һрқ’ұ\mathsf{v},\mathsf{u}\in\mathcal{V}, рқ—ӢвҲҲв„ӣрқ—Ӣв„ӣ\mathsf{r}\in\mathcal{R}, and рқ—ҚвҲҲрқ’Ҝрқ—Қрқ’Ҝ\mathsf{t}\in\mathcal{T}. Let рқ’Іcsuperscriptрқ’Ірқ‘җ\mathcal{W}^{c} be the complement of рқ’Ірқ’І\mathcal{W}. A temporal knowledge graph (KG) рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G} is a subset of рқ’Ірқ’І\mathcal{W} (i.e. рқ’ўвҠӮрқ’Ірқ’ўрқ’І\mathcal{G}\subset\mathcal{W}). Temporal KG completion (TKGC) is the problem of inferring рқ’Ірқ’І\mathcal{W} from рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G}.

A relation рқ—Ӣisubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘–\mathsf{r}_{i} is the inverse of another relation рқ—Ӣjsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘—\mathsf{r}_{j} if (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣi,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)вҲҲрқ’ІвҮ”(рқ—Һ,рқ—Ӣj,рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Қ)вҲҲрқ’Іiffрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘–рқ—Һрқ—Қрқ’Ірқ—Һsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘—рқ—Ҹрқ—Қрқ’І(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r}_{i},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})\in\mathcal{W}\iff(\mathsf{u},\mathsf{r}_{j},\mathsf{v},\mathsf{t})\in\mathcal{W}. рқ—Ӣisubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘–\mathsf{r}_{i} entails рқ—Ӣjsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘—\mathsf{r}_{j} if (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣi,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)вҲҲрқ’ІвҮ’(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣj,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)вҲҲрқ’Ірқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘–рқ—Һрқ—Қрқ’ІвҮ’рқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ—Ӣрқ‘—рқ—Һрқ—Қрқ’І(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r}_{i},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})\in\mathcal{W}\Rightarrow(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r}_{j},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})\in\mathcal{W}.

KG Embedding: Formally, we define an entity embedding as follows.

A relation embedding (рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ:в„ӣвҶ’ПҲ:рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҶ’в„ӣрқң“\mathtt{REMB}:\mathcal{R}\rightarrow\psi) is defined similarly. We refer to the hidden representation of an entity (relation) as the embedding of the entity (relation). A KG embedding model defines two things: 1- the рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} and рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} functions, 2- a score function which takes рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} and рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} as input and provides a score for a given tuple. The parameters of hidden representations are learned from data.

In this section, we describe the existing approaches for static and temporal KG completion that will be used in the rest of the paper. For further detail on temporal KG completion approaches, we refer the reader to a recent survey [27]. We represent the score for a tuple by П•(.)\phi(.).

TransE (static) [4]: In TransE, рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ)=(рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ)рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\mathtt{EEMB}(\mathsf{v})=(\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}) for every рқ—ҸвҲҲрқ’ұрқ—Ҹрқ’ұ\mathsf{v}\in\mathcal{V} where рқ’ӣрқ—ҸвҲҲв„қdsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}, рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ӣ)=(рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ)рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ\mathtt{REMB}(\mathsf{r})=(\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}) for every рқ—ӢвҲҲв„ӣрқ—Ӣв„ӣ\mathsf{r}\in\mathcal{R} where рқ’ӣрқ—ӢвҲҲв„қdsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}, and П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)=вҲ’вҖ–рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ+рқ’ӣрқ—ӢвҲ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҺвҖ–italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һnormsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u})=-||\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}+\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}-\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}||.

DistMult (static) [64]: Same рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} and рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} as TransE but defining П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)=вҹЁрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ,рқ’ӣрқ—Һвҹ©italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u})=\langle\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle.

Tucker (static) [60, 2]: Same рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} and рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} as TransE but defining П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)=вҹЁрқ’ҳ,рқ’ӣрқ—ҸвҠ—рқ’ӣрқ—ӢвҠ—рқ’ӣрқ—Һвҹ©italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ’ҳtensor-productsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u})=\langle\boldsymbol{w},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}\otimes\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}\otimes\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle where рқ’ҳвҲҲв„қd3рқ’ҳsuperscriptв„қsuperscriptрқ‘‘3\boldsymbol{w}\in\mathbb{R}^{d^{3}} is a weight vector shared for all tuples.

RESCAL (static) [45]: Same рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} as TransE but defining рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ӣ)=(рқ’Ғрқ—Ӣ)рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣ\mathtt{REMB}(\mathsf{r})=(\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}}) for every рқ—ӢвҲҲв„ӣрқ—Ӣв„ӣ\mathsf{r}\in\mathcal{R} where рқ’Ғрқ—ӢвҲҲв„қdГ—dsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘рқ‘‘\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times d}, and defining П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)=рқ’ӣрқ—ҸTвҖӢрқ’Ғрқ—ӢвҖӢрқ’ӣрқ—Һitalic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һsubscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣTрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u})=\boldsymbol{z}^{\textsf{T}}_{\mathsf{v}}\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}}\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}.

Canonical Polyadic (CP) (static) [19]: Same рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} as TransE but defining рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ)=(рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ)рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\mathtt{EEMB}(\mathsf{v})=(\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}}) for every рқ—ҸвҲҲрқ’ұрқ—Ҹрқ’ұ\mathsf{v}\in\mathcal{V} where рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸвҲҲв„қdsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}. рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}} is used when рқ—Ҹрқ—Ҹ\mathsf{v} is the head and рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}} is used when рқ—Ҹрқ—Ҹ\mathsf{v} is the tail. In CP, П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)=вҹЁрқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Һвҹ©italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—ҺsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u})=\langle\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle. DistMult is a special case of CP where рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ=рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}}=\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}} for every рқ—ҸвҲҲрқ’ұрқ—Ҹрқ’ұ\mathsf{v}\in\mathcal{V}.

SimplE (static) [25]: Noticing an information flow issue between the two vectors рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}} and рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}} of an entity рқ—Ҹрқ—Ҹ\mathsf{v} in CP, Kazemi and Poole [25] take advantage of the inverse of the relations to address this issue. They define рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ӣ)=(рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ӣ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ӣ)рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ\mathtt{REMB}(\mathsf{r})=(\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{r}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{r}}) for every рқ—ӢвҲҲв„ӣрқ—Ӣв„ӣ\mathsf{r}\in\mathcal{R}, where рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{r}} is used as in CP and рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—ӢвҲҲв„қdsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{r}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d} is considered the embedding of рқ—ӢвҲ’1superscriptрқ—Ӣ1\mathsf{r}^{-1}, the inverse of рқ—Ӣрқ—Ӣ\mathsf{r}. In SimplE, П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u}) is defined as the average of two CP scores: 1- вҹЁрқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ӣ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Һвҹ©subscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҸsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Һ\langle\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}},\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{r}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle corresponding to the score for (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ)рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һ(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u}) and 2- вҹЁрқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Һ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ӣ,рқ’ӣвҶ’рқ—Ҹвҹ©subscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҺsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—ӢsubscriptвҶ’рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ\langle\vec{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{u}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{r}},\reflectbox{$\vec{\reflectbox{$\boldsymbol{z}$}}$}_{\mathsf{v}}\rangle corresponding to the score for (рқ—Һ,рқ—ӢвҲ’1,рқ—Ҹ)рқ—Һsuperscriptрқ—Ӣ1рқ—Ҹ(\mathsf{u},\mathsf{r}^{-1},\mathsf{v}). A similar extension of CP has been proposed in [34].

TTransE (temporal) [21]: An extension of TransE by adding one more embedding function mapping timestamps to hidden representations: рқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Қ)=(рқ’ӣрқ—Қ)рқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қ\mathtt{TEMB}(\mathsf{t})=(\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}) for every рқ—ҚвҲҲрқ’Ҝрқ—Қрқ’Ҝ\mathsf{t}\in\mathcal{T} where рқ’ӣрқ—ҚвҲҲв„қdsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}. In TTransE, П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)=вҲ’вҖ–рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ+рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ+рқ’ӣрқ—ҚвҲ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҺвҖ–italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қnormsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})=-||\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}+\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}+\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}-\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}||.

HyTE (temporal) [10]: Same рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB}, рқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡҒрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{REMB} and рқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{TEMB} as TTransE but defining П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)=вҲ’вҖ–рқ’ӣВҜрқ—Ҹ+рқ’ӣВҜрқ—ӢвҲ’рқ’ӣВҜрқ—ҺвҖ–italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—ҚnormsubscriptВҜрқ’ӣрқ—ҸsubscriptВҜрқ’ӣрқ—ӢsubscriptВҜрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})=-||\overline{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{v}}+\overline{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{r}}-\overline{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{u}}|| where рқ’ӣВҜрқ—‘=рқ’ӣрқ—‘вҲ’рқ’ӣрқ—ҚTвҖӢрқ’ӣрқ—‘вҖӢрқ’ӣрқ—ҚsubscriptВҜрқ’ӣрқ—‘subscriptрқ’ӣрқ—‘subscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣTрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—‘subscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қ\overline{\boldsymbol{z}}_{\mathsf{x}}=\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{x}}-\boldsymbol{z}^{\textsf{T}}_{\mathsf{t}}\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{x}}\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}} for рқ—‘вҲҲ{рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ}рқ—‘рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һ\mathsf{x}\in\{\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u}\}. Intuitively, HyTE first projects the head, relation, and tail embeddings to the space of the timestamp and then applies the TransE function on the projected embeddings.

ConT (temporal) [41]: Ma et al. [41] extend several static KG embedding models to TKGC. Their best performing model, ConT, is an extension of Tucker defining рқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Қ)=(рқ’ӣрқ—Қ)рқҡғрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қ\mathtt{TEMB}(\mathsf{t})=(\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}) for every рқ—ҚвҲҲрқ’Ҝрқ—Қрқ’Ҝ\mathsf{t}\in\mathcal{T} where рқ’ӣрқ—ҚвҲҲв„қd3subscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қsuperscriptв„қsuperscriptрқ‘‘3\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d^{3}} and changing the score function to П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)=вҹЁрқ’ӣрқ—Қ,рқ’ӣрқ—ҸвҠ—рқ’ӣрқ—ӢвҠ—рқ’ӣрқ—Һвҹ©italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қtensor-productsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})=\langle\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}}\otimes\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r}}\otimes\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle. Intuitively, ConT replaces the shared vector рқ’ҳрқ’ҳ\boldsymbol{w} in Tucker with timestamp embeddings рқ’ӣрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қ\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{t}}.

TA-DistMult (temporal) [16]: An extension of DistMult where each character cрқ‘җc in the timestamps is mapped to a vector (рқҷІрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(c)=рқ’ӣcрқҷІрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ‘җsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ‘җ\mathtt{CEMB}(c)=\boldsymbol{z}_{c}) where рқ’ӣcвҲҲв„қdsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ‘җsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{z}_{c}\in\mathbb{R}^{d}. Then, for a tuple (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қ(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t}), a temporal relation is created by considering рқ—Ӣрқ—Ӣ\mathsf{r} and the characters in рқ—Қрқ—Қ\mathsf{t} as a sequence and an embedding рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ,рқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣрқ—Қ\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r},\mathsf{t}} is computed for this temporal relation by feeding the embedding vectors for each element in the sequence to an LSTM and taking its final output. Finally, the score function of DistMult is employed: П•вҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)=вҹЁрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹ,рқ’ӣрқ—Ӣ,рқ—Қ,рқ’ӣрқ—Һвҹ©italic-П•рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Ӣрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\phi(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t})=\langle\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{v}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{r},\mathsf{t}},\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}}\rangle (TransE was employed as well but DistMult performed better).

According to DefinitionВ 1,an entity embedding function takes an entity as input and provides a hidden representation as output. We propose an alternative entity embedding function which, besides entity, takes time as input as well. Inspired by diachronic word embeddings, we call such an embedding function a diachronic entity embedding. Below is a formal definition of a diachronic entity embedding.

One may take their favorite static KG embedding score function and make it temporal by replacing their entity embeddings with diachronic entity embeddings. The choice of the рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{DEEMB} function can be different for various temporal KGs depending on their properties. Here, we propose a рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{DEEMB} function which performs well on our benchmarks. We give the definition for models where the output of the рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{DEEMB} function is a tuple of vectors but it can be generalized to other cases as well. Let рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹрқ—ҚвҲҲв„қdsubscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қрқ—Ҹsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathsf{t}}_{\mathsf{v}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d} be a vector in рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Қ)рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Ҹрқ—Қ\mathtt{DEEMB}(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{t}) (i.e. рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұвҖӢ(рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Қ)=(вҖҰ,рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹрқ—Қ,вҖҰ)рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқ—Ҹрқ—ҚвҖҰsubscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қрқ—ҸвҖҰ\mathtt{DEEMB}(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{t})=(\dots,\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathsf{t}}_{\mathsf{v}},\dots)). We define рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹрқ—Қsubscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қрқ—Ҹ\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathsf{t}}_{\mathsf{v}} as follows:рқ’ӣрқ—Ҹрқ—ҚвҖӢ[n]={рқ’Ӯрқ—ҸвҖӢ[n]вҖӢПғвҖӢ(рқ’ҳрқ—ҸвҖӢ[n]вҖӢрқ—Қ+рқ’ғрқ—ҸвҖӢ[n]),ifВ 1вүӨnвүӨОівҖӢd.рқ’Ӯрқ—ҸвҖӢ[n],ifВ ОівҖӢd<nвүӨd.subscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Қрқ—Ҹdelimited-[]рқ‘ӣcasessubscriptрқ’Ӯрқ—Ҹdelimited-[]рқ‘ӣрқңҺsubscriptрқ’ҳрқ—Ҹdelimited-[]рқ‘ӣрқ—Қsubscriptрқ’ғрқ—Ҹdelimited-[]рқ‘ӣifВ 1вүӨnвүӨОіdsubscriptрқ’Ӯрқ—Ҹdelimited-[]рқ‘ӣifВ Оіd<nвүӨd\boldsymbol{z}^{\mathsf{t}}_{\mathsf{v}}[n]=\begin{cases}\boldsymbol{a}_{\mathsf{v}}[n]\sigma(\boldsymbol{w}_{\mathsf{v}}[n]\mathsf{t}+\boldsymbol{b}_{\mathsf{v}}[n]),&\text{if~{}~{}$1\leq n\leq\gamma d$}.\\\boldsymbol{a}_{\mathsf{v}}[n],&\text{if~{}~{}$\gamma d<n\leq d$}.\end{cases}(1)where рқ’Ӯрқ—ҸвҲҲв„қdsubscriptрқ’Ӯрқ—Ҹsuperscriptв„қрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{a}_{\mathsf{v}}\in\mathbb{R}^{d} and рқ’ҳрқ—Ҹ,рқ’ғрқ—ҸвҲҲв„қОівҖӢdsubscriptрқ’ҳрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ғрқ—Ҹsuperscriptв„қрқӣҫрқ‘‘\boldsymbol{w}_{\mathsf{v}},\boldsymbol{b}_{\mathsf{v}}\in\mathbb{R}^{\gamma d} are (entity-specific) vectors with learnable parameters and ПғрқңҺ\sigma is an activation function. Intuitively, entities may have some features that change over time and some features that remain fixed.The first ОівҖӢdрқӣҫрқ‘‘\gamma d elements of the vector in EquationВ (1) capture temporal featuresand the other (1вҲ’Оі)вҖӢd1рқӣҫрқ‘‘(1-\gamma)d elements capture static features. 0вүӨОівүӨ10рқӣҫ10\leq\gamma\leq 1 is a hyper-parameter controlling the percentage of temporal features.While in EquationВ (1) static features can be potentially obtained from the temporal ones if the optimizer sets some elements of рқ’ҳрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ҳрқ—Ҹ\boldsymbol{w}_{\mathsf{v}} to zero, explicitly modeling static features helps reduce the number of learnable parameters and avoid overfitting to temporal signals (see SectionВ 5.2).

Intuitively, by learning рқ’ҳрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ҳрқ—Ҹ\boldsymbol{w}_{\mathsf{v}}s and рқ’ғрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’ғрқ—Ҹ\boldsymbol{b}_{\mathsf{v}}s, the model learns how to turn entity features on and off at different points in time so accurate temporal predictions can be made about them at any time. рқ’Ӯрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’Ӯрқ—Ҹ\boldsymbol{a}_{\mathsf{v}}s control the importance of the features. We mainly use sine as the activation function for EquationВ (1) because one sine function can model several on and off states. Our experiments explore other activation functions as well and provide more intuition.

Model-Agnosticism: The proposals in existing temporal KG embedding models can only extend one (or a few) static models to temporal KGs. As an example, it is not trivial how RESCAL can be extended to temporal KGs using the proposal in [16] (except for the naive approach of expecting the LSTM to output large рқ’Ғрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣ\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}} matrices) or in [21, 10]. Same goes for models other than RESCAL where the relation embeddings contain matrices (see, e.g., [43, 53, 39]). Using our proposal, one may construct temporal versions of TransE, DistMult, SimplE, Tucker, RESCAL, or other models by replacing their рқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{EEMB} function with рқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұрқҷірқҷҙрқҷҙрқҷјрқҷұ\mathtt{DEEMB} in EquationВ 1. We refer to the resulting models as DE-TransE, DE-DistMult, DE-SimplE and so forth, where DE is short for Diachronic Embedding.

For several static KG embedding models, it has been shown how certain types of domain knowledge (if exists) can be incorporated into the embeddings through parameter sharing (aka tying) and how it helps improve model performance (see, e.g., [25, 55, 42, 15]).Incorporating domain knowledge for these static models can be ported to their temporal version when they are extended to temporal KGs through our diachronic embeddings. Adding Diachronic Embedding for Relations: Compared to entities, we hypothesize that relations may evolve at a very lower rate or, for some relations, evolve only negligibly. To test this hypothesis, we ran DE-TransE and DE-DistMult on ICEWS14 where relation embeddings are also a function of time. From the obtained results in Table 3, one can see that the model with diachronic embeddings for both entities and relations performs on-par with the model with diachronic embedding only for entities. These results show that at least on our benchmarks, modeling the evolution of relations may not be helpful. Future work can test this hypothesis on datasets with other types of relations and longer horizons.

StaRAI: Statistical relational AI (StaRAI) [50, 31] approaches are mainly based on soft (hanf-crafted or learned) rules [51, 11, 29, 26] where the probability of a world is typically proportional to the number of rules that are satisfied/violated in that world and the confidence for each rule. A line of work in this area combines a stack of soft rules with embeddings for property prediction [54, 24]. Another line of work extends the soft rules to temporal KGs [52, 48, 14, 20, 9, 8]. The approaches based on soft rules have been generally shown to perform subpar to KG embedding models [46].

Graph Walk: These approaches define weighted template walks on a KG and then answer queries by template matching [35, 36]. They have been shown to be quite similar to, and in some cases subsumed by, the models based on soft rules [23].

Static KG Embedding: A large number of models have been developed for static KG embedding. A class of these models are the translational approaches corresponding to variations of TransE (see, e.g., [39, 61, 43]). Another class of approaches are based on a bilinear score function рқ’ӣрқ—ҸTвҖӢрқ’Ғрқ—ӢвҖӢрқ’ӣрқ—Һsubscriptsuperscriptрқ’ӣTрқ—Ҹsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’ӣрқ—Һ\boldsymbol{z}^{\textsf{T}}_{\mathsf{v}}\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}}\boldsymbol{z}_{\mathsf{u}} each imposing a different sparsity constraint on the рқ’Ғрқ—Ӣsubscriptрқ’Ғрқ—Ӣ\boldsymbol{Z}_{\mathsf{r}} matrices (see, e.g., [45, 58, 47, 25, 40]). A third class of models are based on deep learning approaches using feed-forward or convolutional layers on top of the embeddings (see, e.g., [53, 13, 12, 1]). These models can be potentially extended to TKGC through our diachronic embedding.

Temporal KG Embedding: Several works have extended the static KG embedding models to temporal KGs. Jiang et al. [21] extend TransE by adding atimestamp embedding into the score function. Dasgupta et al. [10] extend TransE by projecting the embeddings to the timestamp hyperplain and then using the TransE score on the projected space. Ma et al. [41] extend several models by adding a timestamp embedding to their score functions. These models may not work well when the number of timestamps is large. Furthermore, since they only learn embeddings for observed timestamps, they cannot generalize to unseen timestamps. GarcГӯa-DurГЎn etal. [16] extend TransE and DistMult by combining the relation and timestamp through a character LSTM. These models have been described in detail in SectionВ 2 and their performances have been reported in Table 2.

KG Embedding for Extrapolation: TKGC is an interpolation problem where given a set of temporal facts in a time frame, the goal is to predict the missing facts. A related problem is the extrapolation problem where future interactions are to be predicted (see, e.g., [56, 33, 57]). Despite some similarities in the employed approaches, KG extrapolation is fundamentally different from TKGC in that a score for an interaction (рқ—Ҹ,рқ—Ӣ,рқ—Һ,рқ—Қ)рқ—Ҹрқ—Ӣрқ—Һрқ—Қ(\mathsf{v},\mathsf{r},\mathsf{u},\mathsf{t}) is to be computed given only the past (i.e. facts before рқ—Қрқ—Қ\mathsf{t}) whereas in TKGC the score is to be computed given past, present, and future. A comprehensive analysis of the existing models for interpolation and extrapolation can be found in [27].

Diachronic Word Embeddings: The idea behind our proposed embeddings is similar to diachronic word embeddings where a corpus is typically broken temporally into slices (e.g., 20-year chuncks of a 200-year corpus) and embeddings are learned for words in each chunk thus providing word embeddings that are a function of time (see, e.g., [28, 32, 18, 3]). The main goal of diachronic word embeddings is to reveal how the meanings of the words have evolved over time. Our work can be viewed as an extension of diachronic word embeddings to continuous-time KG completion.

Temporal knowledge graph (KG) completion is an important problem and has been the focus of several recent studies. We developed a diachronic embedding function for temporal KG completion which provides a hidden representation for the entities of a temporal KG at any point in time. Our embedding is generic and can be combined with any score function. We proved that combining our diachronic embedding with SimplE results in a fully expressive model вҖ“ the first temporal KG embedding model for which such a result exists. We showed the superior performance of our model compared to existing work on several benchmarks. Future work includes designing functions other than the one proposed in EquationВ 1, a comprehensive study of which functions are favored by different types of KGs, and using our proposed embedding for diachronic word embedding.
