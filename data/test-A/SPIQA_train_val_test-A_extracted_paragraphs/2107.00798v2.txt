In this paper, we investigate the problem of explainablek𝑘k-means and k𝑘k-medians clustering which was recently introducedby Dasgupta, Frost, Moshkovitz, andRashtchian (2020). Suppose, we have a data set which we need to partition into k𝑘k clusters. How can we do it?Of course, we could use one of many standard algorithms for k𝑘k-means or k𝑘k-medians clustering. However, we want to find an explainable clustering– clustering which can be easily understood by a human being. Then, k𝑘k-means or k𝑘k-medians clustering may not be the best options for us.

Note that though every cluster in a k𝑘k-means and k𝑘k-medians clustering has a simple mathematical description, this description isnot necessarily easy to interpret for a human. Every k𝑘k-medians or k𝑘k-means clustering is defined by a set of k𝑘k centers c1,c2,…,cksuperscript𝑐1superscript𝑐2…superscript𝑐𝑘c^{1},c^{2},\dots,c^{k}, whereeach cluster is the set of points located closer to a fixed center cisuperscript𝑐𝑖c^{i} than to any other center cjsuperscript𝑐𝑗c^{j}. That is, for points in cluster i𝑖i, we musthave arg⁡minj⁡‖x−cj‖=isubscript𝑗norm𝑥superscript𝑐𝑗𝑖\arg\min_{j}\|x-c^{j}\|=i. Thus, in order to determineto which cluster a particular point belongs, we need to computedistances from point x𝑥x to all centers cjsuperscript𝑐𝑗c^{j}. Each distance dependson all coordinates of the points. Hence, for a human, it is noteven easy to figure out to which cluster in k𝑘k-means or k𝑘k-medians clustering a particular point belongs to; let alone interpret the entire clustering.

In every day life, we are surrounded by different types of classifications. Consider the following examples from Wikipedia:(1) Performance cars are capable of going from 0 to 60 mph in under 5 seconds; (2) Modern sources currently define skyscrapers as being at least 100 metres or 150 metres in height; (3) Very-low-calorie diets are diets of 800 kcal or less energy intake per day, whereas low-calorie diets are between 1000-1200 kcal per day. Note that all thesedefinitions depend on a single feature which makes them easyto understand.

The above discussion leads us to the idea of Dasgupta et al. (2020), who proposed to use threshold (decision) trees to describe clusters(see also Liu, Xia, and Yu (2005), Fraiman, Ghattas, andSvarc (2013), Bertsimas, Orfanoudaki, andWiberg (2018), and Saisubramanian, Galhotra, andZilberstein (2020)).

A threshold tree is a binary classification tree with k𝑘k leaves. Every internal node u𝑢u of the tree splits the data into two sets by comparing a single feature iusubscript𝑖𝑢i_{u} of each data point with a threshold θusubscript𝜃𝑢\theta_{u}. The first set is the set of points with xiu≤θusubscript𝑥subscript𝑖𝑢subscript𝜃𝑢x_{i_{u}}\leq\theta_{u}; the second set is the set of pointswith xiu>θusubscript𝑥subscript𝑖𝑢subscript𝜃𝑢x_{i_{u}}>\theta_{u}. These two sets are then recursively partitionedby the left and right children of u𝑢u. Thus, each point x𝑥x in the data set is eventually assigned to one of k𝑘k leaves of the threshold tree T𝑇T. This gives us a partitioning of the data set X𝑋X into clusters𝒫=(P1,…,Pk)𝒫subscript𝑃1…subscript𝑃𝑘\mathcal{P}=(P_{1},\dots,P_{k}). We note that threshold decision trees are special cases of binary space partitioning (BSP) trees and similar to k𝑘k-d trees Bentley (1975).

Dasgupta et al. (2020) introduced the explainable k𝑘k-medians and k𝑘k-means clustering problems and developed Iterative Mistake Minimization (IMM) algorithmsfor these problems. Later, Frost, Moshkovitz, and Rashtchian (2020) proposed algorithms that construct threshold trees with more than k𝑘k leaves.

Decision trees have been used for interpretable classification and clustering since 1980s. Breiman, Friedman, Olshen, andStone (1984) proposed a popular decision treealgorithm called CART for supervised classification. For unsupervised clustering, threshold decision trees are used in many empirical methods based on different criteria such as information gain Liu et al. (2005), local 111-means cost Fraiman et al. (2013),Silhouette Metric Bertsimas et al. (2018), and interpretability score Saisubramanian et al. (2020).

The k𝑘k-means and k𝑘k-medians clustering problems have been extensively studied in the literature. The k𝑘k-means++ algorithm proposed by Arthur and Vassilvitskii (2006) is the most widely used algorithm for k𝑘k-means clustering. It provides an O​(ln⁡k)𝑂𝑘O(\ln k) approximation. Li and Svensson (2016) provided a 1+3+ε13𝜀1+\sqrt{3}+\varepsilon approximation for k𝑘k-medians in general metric spaces,which was improved to 2.611+ε2.611𝜀2.611+\varepsilon by Byrka, Pensyl, Rybicki, Srinivasan, andTrinh (2014). Ahmadian, Norouzi-Fard, Svensson, andWard (2019) gave a 6.3576.3576.357 approximation algorithm for k𝑘k-means. The k𝑘k-medians and k𝑘k-means problems are NP-hard Megiddo and Supowit (1984); Dasgupta (2008); Aloise et al. (2009).Recently, Awasthi, Charikar, Krishnaswamy, andSinop (2015) showed that it is also NP-hard to approximate the k𝑘k-means objective within a factor of (1+ε)1𝜀(1+\varepsilon) for some positive constant ε𝜀\varepsilon (see also Lee et al. Bhattacharya, Goyal, andJaiswal (2020) showed that the Euclidean k𝑘k-medians can not be approximated within a factor of (1+ε)1𝜀(1+\varepsilon) for some constant ε𝜀\varepsilon assuming the unique games conjecture.
