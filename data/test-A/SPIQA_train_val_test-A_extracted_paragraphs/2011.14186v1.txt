Widespread testing has been promoted for combating the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.Samples are typically collected from nasal or oropharyngeal swabs, and then processed by a reverse transcription polymerase chain reaction (RT-PCR) machine.However, widespread testing is hindered by supply chain constraints and long testing times.

Pooled or group testing has been suggested for improving testing efficiencies [2].Group testing involves mixing a subset of nрқ‘ӣn individual samples into m<nрқ‘ҡрқ‘ӣm<n pools. Assuming reasonably high viral loads in рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x},Poisson effects in рқ’ҡрқ’ҡ\boldsymbol{y} can be ignored [4].

For both models, the objective is to estimate рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x} from рқ’ҡрқ’ҡ\boldsymbol{y} and рқ‘Ёрқ‘Ё\boldsymbol{A}.We use single-stage non-adaptive algorithms as in [3, 4], rather than two-stage algorithms,which employ a second stage of tests depending on results from the first stage, as inHeidarzadeh and NarayananВ [5] or the classical approach by DorfmanВ [6].The main advantage of non-adaptive algorithms is that they save on testing time, which is quite high for RT-PCR.Algorithms for estimation of рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x} from рқ’ҡрқ’ҡ\boldsymbol{y} and рқ‘Ёрқ‘Ё\boldsymbol{A}В [4, 7]rely primarily on the sparsity of рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x}, which is a valid assumption for COVID-19 due to low prevalence rates [8].Zhu et al.В [3] also exploit probabilistic information such as the prevalence rate, structure in рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x}, and side information (SI).Finally, Nikolopoulos et al.В [1] showed how SI aboutfamily-style structure can reduce the number of required tests, mрқ‘ҡm.

In this paper, we show how to estimate рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x} while utilizing contact tracing (CT) SI, which allows one to analyze the spread of the pandemic [9].Our contributions are twofold.First, we propose a generative model for a population of nрқ‘ӣn individuals that characterizes the spread of COVID-19 by explicitly using CT SI.Second, we show that CT SI, when used appropriately, can help algorithms such as generalized approximate message passingВ (GAMP)В [10]or LASSO variants [11, 12]better estimate рқ’ҷрқ’ҷ\boldsymbol{x} from рқ’ҡрқ’ҡ\boldsymbol{y} and рқ‘Ёрқ‘Ё\boldsymbol{A}.Our work uses more SI than Nikolopoulos et al.В [1], who only considered family-style structure in binary group testing.

For this experiment, we use a different and slightly more general contact tracing graph to simulate the spread of infection. Recall that the adjacency matrix of the contact graph has a block diagonal structure with sizes of cliques coming from the distribution of family sizes in India [17, pg. 18]. However, in this case, we allow two consecutive (according to the order in which cliques appear along the diagonal of the contact matrix) non-trivial cliques (i.e., cliques with more than one node) to have an overlap of one node with probability half. This assumption is reasonable since the concept of family encompasses more general groups such as people at the same workplace, students studying in the same classroom, etc. Furthermore, we remove Оұ=5%рқӣјpercent5\alpha=5\% of the edges from this block diagonal structure, thus converting the existing cliques into вҖңalmost-cliques.вҖқ This modified block diagonal structure is kept constant over time while the cross-clique contacts are updated every day. Except for the changes in the underlying contact tracing graph, the rest of the data generation method is the same as that described in Sec.В 2 of the main paper.

We use the four algorithms (including Comp) for multiplicative noise described in Sec.В 3 of the main paper. However, instead of using maximal cliques as groups in Comp-sqrt-oglasso, we use the decomposition of the contact tracing graph into overlapping 3-clique communities [23]. An algorithm for detecting kрқ‘ҳk-clique communities can be found in Sec.В 1 of [23, Supplementary Notes]. The first step of this algorithm involves finding the maximal cliques in the contact graph, for which we use the Bron-Kerbosch algorithm [21]. In the next step, we detect 3-clique communities and label each of those as groups. Further, we also label as groups the maximal cliquesthat are not part of any of these communities, in order to ensure that every contact is taken into account. The advantage of using 3-clique communities over just maximal cliques is that the former is able to capture вҖңalmost cliques,вҖқ i.e., cliques with a small fraction of absent pairwise contacts.
