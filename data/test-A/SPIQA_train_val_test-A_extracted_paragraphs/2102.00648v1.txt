Hashing has been widely used in image retrievalВ Cao et al. (2017); Jiang and Li (2018); Wu et al. (2019); Zhao et al. (2020), video retrievalВ Yuan et al. (2020), and cross-modal retrievalВ Jiang and Li (2017) due to its high computation efficiency and low storage cost. Traditional hashing methods are based on hand-crafted features. The representative methods include LSHВ Gionis et al. (1999), ITQВ Gong et al. (2012) and SDHВ Shen et al. (2015).

In recent years, deep hashing methods have greatly improved the retrieval performance due to their powerful representation ability. Large number of deep hashing methods have been proposed, including single-label hashing methodsВ Gui et al. (2017); Cao et al. (2018); Fan et al. (2020); Zhao et al. (2020); Li et al. (2020) and multi-label hashing methodsВ Zhao et al. (2015); Lai et al. (2016). However, for all these deep hashing methods, the optimization is an intractable problem because of the discrete property of the binary hash codes.

Previous methods usually adopt binary approximation to tackle the above problem. A common strategy in these methods is to adopt an activation function, e.g. sigmoidвҒЎ(вӢ…)sigmoidвӢ…\operatorname{sigmoid}(\cdot) or tanhвҒЎ(вӢ…)tanhвӢ…\operatorname{tanh}(\cdot), and minimize a quantization loss to approximate the discrete values. However, this paradigm may make more and more wrong hash bits stuck into the saturated area of the activation functions, and these wrong hash bits will never escapeВ (as shown in Figure 1). We call this problem вҖңDead Bits ProblemВ (DBP)вҖқ. Besides, we call the wrong bitsВ (red bar) stuck in the saturated area as вҖңDead BitsвҖқ, because the gradients are nearly zero. WhatвҖҷs more, the number of dead bits will increase with the training epoch. This is because different bits have different change trend, i.e. uncertaintyВ Fu et al. (2020). As a result, the direction of optimization of each hash bit is oscillatory. Some bits may вҖңincautiouslyвҖқ fall into the saturated area and hard to escape.

Moreover, the quantization loss widely used in hashing methods will also aggravate DBP, such as L2subscriptL2\text{L}_{2} quantization lossВ Li et al. (2015) or logвҒЎcosh\log\cosh quantization lossВ Zhu et al. (2016). These quantization loss functions drag bits into вҲ’11-1 or +11+1 simply according to their current sign, which may conflict with the similarity preserving requirement. For example, if the hash bits of two similar images fall into different sides of zero, the quantization loss will push them apart, while the similarity preserving loss will pull them together.

To address there issues, we propose a simple but effective deep hashing component named Gradient Amplifier which can detect and rescue dead bits by amplifying their gradients. Moreover, we devise an error-aware quantization loss function. For correct bits, it adaptively reduce their quantization loss to generate high-quality hash codes. For dead bits, they are propagated to the similarity preserving loss. This will avoid the negative effect brought by the original quantization loss.

The main contributions of this work can be summarized as follows:вҖўTo the best of our knowledge, we are the first to formalize the вҖңDead bits ProblemвҖқ: the saturated area of activation function and existing quantization loss will вҖңkillвҖқ more and more hash bits.вҖўWe propose a gradient amplifier which detects the dead bits and amplifies their gradients to rescue them. Furthermore, we design an error-aware quantization loss, which will further alleviate the DBP. The proposed gradient amplifier and error-aware quantization loss can be compatible with a various of deep hashing methods.вҖўExtensive experiments on three datasets demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed gradient amplifier and the error-aware quantization loss.

Learning to hash aims to project data from high-dimensional space into low-dimensional Hamming space and can be categorized into traditional hashing methods and deep hashing methods.

Traditional hashing methods leverage hand-crafted features to learn hash function. LSHВ Gionis et al. (1999) generates a random projection matrix to map the features into hash codes. ITQВ Gong et al. (2012) uses PCA to perform dimension reduction. SDHВ Shen et al. (2015) adopts discrete cyclic coordinate descentВ (DCC) to optimize hash codes directly.

Deep hashing methods involve CNN models into the learning of hash codes. CNNHВ Xia et al. (2014) is a two-stage hashing method that utilizes CNN model to generate hash codes. DNNHВ Lai et al. (2015) is the first end-to-end deep hashing method that learns features and hash codes simultaneously. DPSHВ Li et al. (2015) leverages pairwise supervised information to learn hash function. And DSDHВ Li et al. (2020) introduces classification information into the training process and optimizes database codes by DCCВ Shen et al. (2015). Recently, asymmetric architecture of deep hashing has shown great potential to improve the retrieval performance. DAPHВ Shen et al. (2017) adopts two different CNN model to learn a hash function simultaneously. ADSHВ Jiang and Li (2018) utilizes a CNN for query images, while the database codes are learned directly by DCC. DIHNВ Wu et al. (2019) aims to learn a hash function incrementally. CCDHВ Zhao et al. (2020) leverages variational autoencoder to update the CNN model and database codes efficiently.

For all the deep hashing methods, the optimization for binary hash codes is remain an intractable problem. To approximate the non-differentiable signвҒЎ(вӢ…)signвӢ…\operatorname{sign}(\cdot) function, tanhвҒЎ(вӢ…)tanhвӢ…\operatorname{tanh}(\cdot) and quantization loss are introduced and widely used in hashing methodsВ Zhu et al. (2016); Cao et al. (2017); Wu et al. (2019); Zhao et al. (2020). However, few research focuses at the problem leading by the saturated area of activation function and the quantization loss in pairwise learning. They will push bits toward wrong direction and can not escape from the saturated area.

In this paper, we have characterized a serious problem that caused by the saturated area in activation function, e.g. sigmoidвҒЎ(вӢ…)sigmoidвӢ…\operatorname{sigmoid}(\cdot) or tanhвҒЎ(вӢ…)tanhвӢ…\operatorname{tanh}(\cdot) and the traditional quantization loss, called DBP. To address this issue, we have proposed a gradient amplifier to detect and rescue the dead bits. Furthermore, an error-aware quantization loss is proposed to alleviate DBP. Extensive experiments have demonstrated that the proposed method can significantly decrease the number of dead bits and improve the performance of the baseline methods.
