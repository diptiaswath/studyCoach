To the best of our knowledge, for an input vector 𝐲∈ℝn𝐲superscriptℝ𝑛\mathbf{y}\in\mathbb{R}^{n}, the state-of-the-art algorithm [17] for solving the Projection (1) has a time complexity of 𝒪​(n2)𝒪superscript𝑛2\mathcal{O}(n^{2}).We found that a simple heuristic algorithm based on Newton’s method is able to solve the same problem to high precision with a complexity roughly 𝒪​(n)𝒪𝑛\mathcal{O}(n) on average, based on exploiting the 2nd-order information of a scalar minimization problem on the Lagrangian multiplier of Problem (1); see § 2.3.

We proposed a simple algorithm based on Newton’s method, as an alternative to the sorting-based method to solve Problem (1).We first convert the problem into a scalar minimization problem, then we show that such scalar problem can be solved using Newton’s iteration, see Theorem 1.We then show that the proposed method is superior to the current state-of-the-art method in terms of runtime.For a vector with dimension 106superscript10610^{6}, the proposed method is about 6-8 times faster than a commercial solver.Furthermore, we give numerical results to show the effectiveness of the proposed method on solving sparse regression in a bioinformatics problem on a real-world dataset with one million data points, see § 4.

We emphasize that the proposed method is not aimed at replacing all the well-established sorting-based methods in the literature, but to provide an alternative method for solving the projection problem in a special case.To be specific, we found that, when the input vector 𝐲𝐲\mathbf{y} has bounded elements (i.e., |yi|≤αsubscript𝑦𝑖𝛼|y_{i}|\leq\alpha, for α𝛼\alpha up to 1000), the proposed algorithm in this paper is able to solve the projection problem much faster than the existing methods in the literature.It is important to note that, when the bound α𝛼\alpha becomes very huge (say a million), the proposed method sometimes converges slower than existing methods.Although limited in the scope of application, however, we argue that the proposed method is still very useful for applications that the input vectors are bounded.Using bioinformatics as an example, on an ordinary PC, we are able to reduce the runtime on identifying the genetic factors that contribute to dental caries (i.e., tooth decay) from half-hour to just 3 minutes; see § 4.

We propose to use Newton’s method to solve the problem of projecting a vector onto the k𝑘k-capped simplex.On the theory side, we transform the problem to a scalar minimization problem and provide a theorem to characterize the derivatives of the cost function of such a problem.On the practical side, we show that the proposed method outperforms existing methods on solving the projection problem, and show that it can be used to accelerate the method on solving sparse regressions in bioinformatics on a huge dataset.
