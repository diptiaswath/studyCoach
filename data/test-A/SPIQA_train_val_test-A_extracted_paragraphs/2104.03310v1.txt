Generative adversarial networksВ (GANs)В [2, 7, 14, 47] have made significant progress in recent years on synthesizing high-fidelity images.The GAN models are the cornerstone techniques for numerous vision applications, such as data augmentationВ [12, 13], domain adaptationВ [19, 20], image extrapolationВ [10, 66], image-to-image translationВ [22, 37, 86], and visual content creationВ [1, 4, 21, 23, 70, 72].

The success of the GAN methods heavily relies on a large amount of diverse training data which is often labor-expensive or cumbersome to collectВ [76].As the example of the BigGANВ [7] model presented in Figure 1, the performance significantly deteriorates under the limited training data.Consequently, several very recent approachesВ [30, 82, 84] have been developed to address the data insufficiency issue.A representative task in this emerging research direction aims to learn a robust class-conditional GAN model when only a small proportion of the ImageNet dataВ [59] are available for the training.Generally, existing methods exploit data augmentation, either conventional or differentiable augmentation, to increase the diversity of the limited training data.These data augmentation approaches have shown promising results on several standard benchmarks.

In this paper, we address the GAN training task on limited data from a different perspective: model regularization.Although there are numerous regularization techniques for the GAN models in the literatureВ [15, 48, 50, 63, 85], none of them aim to improve the generalization of the GAN models trained on limited data.In contrast, our goal is to learn robust GAN models on limited training data that can generalize well on out-of-sample data.To this end, we introduce a novel regularization scheme to modulate the discriminatorвЂ™s prediction for learning a robust GAN model.Specifically, we impose an в„“2subscriptв„“2\ell_{2} norm between the current prediction of the real image and a moving average variable that tracks the historical predictions of the generated image, and vice versa.We theoretically show that, under mild assumptions, the regularization transforms the WGANВ [2] formulation towards minimizing an fрќ‘“f-divergence called LeCam-divergenceВ [36].We find that the LeCam-divergence is more robust under the limited training data setting.

We conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the three merits of the proposed regularization scheme.First, it improves the generalization performance of various GAN approaches, such as BigGANВ [7] and StyleGAN2В [32].Second, it stabilizes the training dynamics of the GAN models under the limited training data setting.Finally, our regularization approach is empirically complementary to the data augmentation methodsВ [30, 82].As presented in Figure 1, we obtain state-of-the-art performance on the limited (e.g.,В 10%percent1010\%) ImageNet dataset by combining our regularization (i.e.,В RLCsubscriptрќ‘…LCR_{\mathrm{LC}}) and the data augment methodВ [82].

Generative adversarial networksВ (GANs)В [2, 7, 14, 27, 32, 47, 79] aim to model the target distribution using adversarial learning.Various adversarial losses have been proposed to stabilize the training or improve the convergence of the GAN models, mainly based on the idea of minimizing the fрќ‘“f-divergence between the real and generated data distributionsВ [55].For example, GoodfellowВ etВ al.В [14] propose the saturated loss that minimizes the JS-divergence between the two distributions.Similarly, the LSGANВ [47] formulation leads to minimizing the П‡2superscriptрќњ’2\chi^{2}-divergenceВ [56], and the EBGANВ [81] approach optimizes the total variation distanceВ [2].On the other hand, some models are designed to minimize the integral probability metrics (IPM)В [52, 64], such as the WGANВ [2, 15] frameworks.In this work, we design a new regularization scheme that can be applied to different GAN loss functions for training the GAN models on the limited data.

With the objective of reducing the data collection effort, several studiesВ [16, 76] raise the concern of insufficient data for training the GAN models.Training the GAN models on limited data is challenging because the data scarcity leads to the problems such as unstable training dynamics, degraded fidelity of the generated images, and memorization of the training examples.To address these issues, recent methodsВ [30, 68, 80, 82, 83, 84] exploit data augmentation as a mean to increase data diversity, hence preventing the GAN models from overfitting the training data.For example, ZhangВ etВ al.В [80] augment the real images and introduce a consistency loss for training the discriminator.The DAВ [82] and ADAВ [30] approaches share a similar idea of applying differential data augmentation on both real and generated images, in which ADA further develops an adaptive strategy to adjust the probability of augmentation.In contrast to prior work, we tackle this problem from a different perspective of model regularization.We show that our method is conceptually and empirically complementary to the existing data augmentation approaches.

Most existing regularization methods for GAN models aim to accomplish two goals: 1) stabilizing the training to ensure the convergenceВ [48, 49], and 2) mitigating the mode-collapse issueВ [60].As the GAN frameworks are known for unstable training dynamics, numerous efforts have been made to address the issue using noiseВ [24, 63], gradient penaltyВ [15, 33, 48, 58], spectral normalizationВ [50], adversarial defenseВ [85], etc.On the other hand, a variety of regularization approachesВ [5, 8, 44, 46, 65, 78] are proposed to alleviate the model-collapse issue, thus increasing the diversity of the generated images.Compared with these methods, our work targets a different goal: improving the generalization of the GAN models trained on the limited training data.

Robust deep learning aims to prevent the deep neural networks from overfitting or memorizing the training data.Recent methods have shown successes in overcoming training data bias such as label noiseВ [3, 17, 25, 26, 43, 54, 57, 77] and biased data distributionsВ [9, 11, 38, 41, 61, 69, 71].Recently, few approachesВ [6, 28, 29, 67] have been proposed for learning the robust GAN model.While these approaches aredesigned to overcome label or image noise in a corrupted training set, we improve the generalization of the GAN models trained on the limited uncorrupted training data.

In this work, we present a regularization method to train the GAN models under the limited data setting.The proposed method achieves a more robust training objective for the GAN models by imposing a regularization loss to the discriminator during the training stage.In the experiments, we conduct experiments on various image generation datasets with different GAN backbones to demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed scheme that 1) improves the performance of the GAN models, especially under the limited data setting and 2) can be applied along with the data augmentation methods to further enhance the performance.In future, we plan the training data scarcity issue for 1) the conditional GAN tasks such as image extrapolation, image-to-image translation, etc, and 2) the robust GAN learning on large-scale noisy training data.
