Submodular function maximization has aroused great interests from both academic and industrial societies due to its wide applications such as crowdsourcingВ [47], information gatheringВ [35], sensor placementВ [33], influence maximizationВ [37, 48] and exemplar-based clusteringВ [30]. Due to the large volume of data and heterogeneous application scenarios in practice, there is a growing demand for designing accurate and efficient submodular maximization algorithms subject to various constraints.

Matroid is an important structure in combinatorial optimization that abstracts and generalizes the notion of linear independence in vector spacesВ [8]. The problem of submodular maximization subject to a matroid constraint (SMM) has attracted considerable attention since the 1970s. It is noted that most of the current deterministic algorithms for non-monontone submodular maximizationВ (e.g., [44, 25, 45]) leverage the вҖңrepeated greedy-searchвҖқ framework proposed byВ Gupta etВ al. Our approach is based on a novel вҖңsimultaneous greedy-searchвҖқ framework different from theirs, where two disjoint candidate solution sets S1subscriptрқ‘Ҷ1S_{1} and S2subscriptрқ‘Ҷ2S_{2} are built simultaneously with only single-pass greedy searching, without calling a USM algorithm. We call these two solution sets S1subscriptрқ‘Ҷ1S_{1} and S2subscriptрқ‘Ҷ2S_{2} as вҖңtwin setsвҖқ because they вҖңgrow upвҖқ simultaneously. We evaluate the performance of TwinGreedyFast on two applications: social network monitoring and multi-product viral marketing. The experimental results show that TwinGreedyFast runs more than an order of magnitude faster than the state-of-the-art efficient algorithms for our problem, and also achieves better solution quality than the currently fastest randomized algorithms in the literature.

We have proposed the first deterministic algorithm to achieve an approximation ratio of 1/4141/4 for maximizing a non-monotone, non-negative submodular function subject to a matroid constraint, and our algorithm can also be accelerated to achieve nearly-linear running time. In contrast to the existing algorithms adopting the вҖңrepeated greedy-searchвҖқ framework proposed byВ [31], our algorithms are designed based on a novel вҖңsimultaneous greedy-searchвҖқ framework, where two candidate solutions are constructed simultaneously, and a pair of an element and a candidate solution is greedily selected at each step to maximize the marginal gain. Moreover, our algoirthms can also be directly used to handle a more general pрқ‘қp-set system constraint or monotone submodular functions, while still achieving nice performance bounds. For example, by similar reasoning with that in SectionsВ 3-4, it can be seen that our algorithms can achieve 1p+11рқ‘қ1\frac{1}{p+1} ratio for monotone fвҖӢ(вӢ…)рқ‘“вӢ…f(\cdot) under a pрқ‘қp-set system constraint, which is almost best possibleВ [2]. We have evaluated the performance of our algorithms in two concrete applications for social network monitoring and multi-product viral marketing, and the extensive experimental results demonstrate that our algorithms runs in orders of magnitude faster than the state-of-the-art algorithms, while achieving approximately the same utility.
