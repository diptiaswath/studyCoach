The Hausdorff Dimension. Due to their recursive nature, Markov processes often generate ‘random fractals’ [Xia03] and understanding the structure of such fractals has been a major challenge in modern probability theory [BP17, Kho09, KX17, Yan18, LG19, LY19]. In this paper, we are interested in identifying the complexity of the fractals generated by a Feller process that approximates SGD.

The intrinsic complexity of a fractal is typically characterized by a notion called the Hausdorff dimension [Fal04], which extends the usual notion of dimension (e.g., a line segment is one-dimensional, a plane is two-dimensional) to fractional orders. Informally, this notion measures the ‘roughness’ of an object (i.e., a set) and in the context of Lévy processes, they are deeply connected to the tail properties of the corresponding Lévy measure. [Sch98, Xia03, Yan18].

Uniform Hausdorff dimension and Feller processes. In this part, we introduce the ‘uniform Hausdorff dimension’ property for a training algorithm 𝒜𝒜\mathcal{A}, which is a notion of complexity based on the Hausdorff dimension of the trajectories generated by 𝒜𝒜\mathcal{A}. By translating [Sch98] into our context, we will then show that decomposable Feller processes possess this property.

Since 𝒲S⊂𝒲⊂ℝdsubscript𝒲𝑆𝒲superscriptℝ𝑑\mathcal{W}_{S}\subset\mathcal{W}\subset\mathbb{R}^{d}, by the definition of Hausdorff dimension, any algorithm 𝒜𝒜\mathcal{A} possesses the uniform Hausdorff dimension property trivially with dH=dsubscript𝑑H𝑑d_{\mathrm{H}}=d. We provide all the proofs in the supplementary document.Informally, this result can be interpreted as follows. Thanks to the decomposability property, for each S𝑆S, the process W(S)superscriptW𝑆\mathrm{W}^{(S)} behaves like a Lévy motion around wSsubscript𝑤𝑆w_{S}, and the characteristic exponent is given by the sub-symbol ψSsubscript𝜓𝑆\psi_{S}. Because of this locally regular behavior, the Hausdorff dimension of the image of W(S)superscriptW𝑆\mathrm{W}^{(S)} can be bounded by βSsubscript𝛽𝑆\beta_{S}, which only depends on tail behavior of the Lévy process whose exponent is the sub-symbol ψSsubscript𝜓𝑆\psi_{S}.

The term βSsubscript𝛽𝑆\beta_{S} is often termed as the upper Blumenthal-Getoor (BG) index of the Lévy process with an exponent ψSsubscript𝜓𝑆\psi_{S} [BG60], and it is directly related to the tail-behavior of the corresponding Lévy measure. In general, the value of βSsubscript𝛽𝑆\beta_{S} decreases as the process gets heavier-tailed, which implies that the heavier-tailed processes have smaller Hausdorff dimension; thus, they have smaller complexity.

In this paper, we rigorously tied the generalization in a learning taskto the tail properties of the underlying training algorithm, shedding light on an empirically observed phenomenon.We established this relationship through the Hausdorff dimension of the SDE approximating the algorithm,and proved a generalization error bound based on this notion of complexity.Unlike the standard ambient dimension, our bounds do not necessarily grow with the number of parameters in the network,and they solely depend on the tail behavior of the training process,providing an explanation for theimplicit regularization effect of heavy-tailed SGD.
