In this work, we contend that momentum is evidently useful for FW. Specifically, we prove that the heavy ball momentum leads to tightened and efficiently computed primal-dual error bound, as well as numerical improvement. To this end, we outline first the primal convergence.

Primal convergence. The primal error refers to fвҖӢ(рқҗұk)вҲ’fвҖӢ(рқҗұвҲ—)рқ‘“subscriptрқҗұрқ‘ҳрқ‘“superscriptрқҗұf(\mathbf{x}_{k})-f(\mathbf{x}^{*}). It is guaranteed for FW that fвҖӢ(рқҗұk)вҲ’fвҖӢ(рқҗұвҲ—)=рқ’ӘвҖӢ(1/k),вҲҖkвүҘ1formulae-sequenceрқ‘“subscriptрқҗұрқ‘ҳрқ‘“superscriptрқҗұрқ’Ә1рқ‘ҳfor-allрқ‘ҳ1f(\mathbf{x}_{k})-f(\mathbf{x}^{*})={\cal O}\big{(}1/k\big{)},\forall k\geq 1 [16, 22]. This rate is tight in general since it matches to the lower bound [20, 16]. Other FW variants also ensure the same order of primal error; see e.g., [20, 21].

Primal-dual convergence.The primal-dual (PD) error quantifies the difference between both the primal and the вҖҳdualвҖҷ functions from the optimal objective, hence it is an upper bound on the primal error. When the PD error is shown to converge, it can be safely used as the stopping criterion: whenever the PD error is less than some prescribed Пө>0italic-Пө0\epsilon>0, fвҖӢ(рқҗұk)вҲ’fвҖӢ(рқҗұвҲ—)вүӨПөрқ‘“subscriptрқҗұрқ‘ҳрқ‘“superscriptрқҗұitalic-Пөf(\mathbf{x}_{k})-f(\mathbf{x}^{*})\leq\epsilon is ensured automatically. The PD error of FW is convenient to compute, hence FW is suitable for the requirement of вҖңsolving problems to some desirable accuracy;вҖқ see e.g., [33]. For pruning (two-layer) neural networks [34], the extra training loss incurred by removing neurons can be estimated via the PD error. However, due to technical difficulties, existing analyses on PD error are not satisfactory enough and lack of unification. It is established in [6, 16, 10] that the minimum PD error is sufficiently small, namely minkвҲҲ{1,вҖҰ,K}вҒЎPDErrork=рқ’ӘвҖӢ(1K)subscriptрқ‘ҳ1вҖҰрқҗҫsubscriptPDErrorрқ‘ҳрқ’Ә1рқҗҫ\min_{k\in\{1,\ldots,K\}}{\rm PDError}_{k}={\cal O}\big{(}\frac{1}{K}\big{)}, where KрқҗҫK is the total number of iterations. In this work, we show that a computationally affordable Type II bound can be obtained by simply relying on heavy ball momentum. Interestingly, FW based on heavy ball momentum (HFW) also maintains FWвҖҷs neat geometric interpretation. Through unified analysis, the resultant type II PD error improves over existing bounds; see Table 1. This PD error of HFW is further tightened using restart. вҖўWe show through unified analysis that HFW enables a tighter type II guarantee for PD error for multiple choices of the step size. This work demonstrated the merits of heavy ball momentum for FW. Multiple choices of the step size ensured a tighter Type II primal-dual error bound that can be efficiently computed when adopted as stopping criterion. An even tighter PD error bound can be achieved by relying jointly on heavy ball momentum and restart. A novel and general approach was developed to compute local Lipschitz constants in FW type algorithms. Numerical tests in the paradigms of logistic regression and matrix completion demonstrated the effectiveness of heavy ball momentum in FW. Our future research agenda includes performance evaluation of heavy ball momentum for various learning tasks. For example, HFW holds great potential when fairness is to be accounted for [35].
