Learning a deep generative model for the structured image data is difficult because this task is not simply modeling a many-to-one mapping function such as the classification, instead it is often required to generate diverse outputs for similar codes sampled from a simple distribution. Furthermore, image 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} in the high dimension space often lies in a complex manifold, thus the generative model should capture the underlying data distribution p​(𝐱)𝑝𝐱p(\bm{\mathrm{x}}).

Basically, Variational Auto-Encoder (VAE) [34, 20] and Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) [13, 25] are two strategies for structured data generation. In VAE, the encoder qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) maps data 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} into the code 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}} in latent space. The decoder, represented by pθ​(𝐱|𝐳)subscript𝑝𝜃conditional𝐱𝐳p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}|\bm{\mathrm{z}}), is given a latent code 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}} sampled from a distribution specified by the encoder and tries to reconstruct 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}}. The encoder and decoder in VAE are trained together mainly based on the data reconstruction loss. At the same time, it requires to regularize the distribution qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) to be simple (e.g. Gaussian) based on the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between q​(𝐳|𝐱)𝑞conditional𝐳𝐱q(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) and p​(𝐳)=𝒩​(0,𝐈)𝑝𝐳𝒩0𝐈p(\bm{\mathrm{z}})=\mathcal{N}(0,\bm{\mathrm{I}}), so that the sampling in latent space is easy. Optimization for VAE is quite stable, but results from it are blurry. Mainly because the posterior defined by qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) is not complex enough to capture the true posterior, also known for ”posterior collapse”. On the other hand, GAN treats the data generation task as a min/max game between a generator G​(𝐳)𝐺𝐳G(\bm{\mathrm{z}}) and discriminator D​(𝐱)𝐷𝐱D(\bm{\mathrm{x}}). The adversarial loss computed from the discriminator makes generated image more realistic, but its training becomes more unstable. In [10, 22, 28], VAE and GAN are integrated together so that they can benefit each other.

Both VAE and GAN work in an unsupervised way without giving any condition of the label on the generated image. Instead, conditional VAE (cVAE) [39, 3] extends it by showing the label c𝑐c for both encoder and decoder. It learns data distribution conditioned on the given label. Hence, the encoder and decoder become qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱,c)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱𝑐q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}},c) and pθ​(𝐱|𝐳,c)subscript𝑝𝜃conditional𝐱𝐳𝑐p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}|\bm{\mathrm{z}},c). Similarly, in conditional GAN (cGAN) [9, 18, 33, 30] label c𝑐c is given to both generator G​(𝐳,c)𝐺𝐳𝑐G(\bm{\mathrm{z}},c) and discriminator D​(𝐱,c)𝐷𝐱𝑐D(\bm{\mathrm{x}},c). Theoretically, feeding label c𝑐c to either the encoder in VAE or decoder in VAE or GAN helps increasing the mutual information between the generated 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} and the label c𝑐c. Thus, it can improve the quality of generated image.

This paper deals with image generation problem in VAE with two separate encoders. For a single input 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}}, our goal is to disentangle the latent space code 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}}, computed by encoders, into the label relevant dimensions 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} and irrelevant ones 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u}. We emphasize the difference between 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} and 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u}, and their corresponding encoders. For 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}, since label c𝑐c is known during training, it should be more accurate and specific. While without any label constraint, 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u} should be general. Specifically, the two encoders are constrained with different priors on their posterior distributions qϕs​(𝐳s|𝐱)subscript𝑞subscriptitalic-ϕ𝑠conditionalsubscript𝐳𝑠𝐱q_{\phi_{s}}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) and qϕu​(𝐳u|𝐱)subscript𝑞subscriptitalic-ϕ𝑢conditionalsubscript𝐳𝑢𝐱q_{\phi_{u}}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}). Similar with VAE or cVAE, in which the full code 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}} is label irrelevant, the prior for 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u} is also chosen 𝒩​(0,𝐈)𝒩0𝐈\mathcal{N}(0,\bm{\mathrm{I}}). But different from previous works, the prior p​(𝐳s)𝑝subscript𝐳𝑠p(\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}) becomes complex to capture the label relevant distribution. From the decoder’s perspective, it takes the concatenation of 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} and 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u} to reconstruct the input 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}}. Here the distinction with cVAE and cGAN is that they uses the fixed, one-hot encoding label, while our work applies 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}, which is considered to be a variational, soft label.

Note that there are two stages for training our model. First, the encoder for 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} gets trained for classification task under the supervision of label c𝑐c. Here instead of the softmax cross entropy loss, Gaussian mixture cross entropy loss proposed in [44] is adopted since it accumulates the mean 𝝁csubscript𝝁𝑐\bm{\mathrm{\mu}}_{c} and variance 𝝈csubscript𝝈𝑐\bm{\mathrm{\sigma}}_{c} for samples with the same label c𝑐c, and models it as the Gaussian 𝒩​(𝝁c,𝝈c)𝒩subscript𝝁𝑐subscript𝝈𝑐\mathcal{N}(\bm{\mathrm{\mu}}_{c},\bm{\mathrm{\sigma}}_{c}), hence 𝐳s∼𝒩​(𝝁c,𝝈c)similar-tosubscript𝐳𝑠𝒩subscript𝝁𝑐subscript𝝈𝑐\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}\sim\mathcal{N}(\bm{\mathrm{\mu}}_{c},\bm{\mathrm{\sigma}}_{c}). The first stage specifies the label relevant distribution. In the second stage, the two encoders and the decoder are trained jointly in an end-to-end manner based on the reconstruction loss. Meanwhile, priors of 𝐳s∼𝒩​(𝝁c,𝝈c)similar-tosubscript𝐳𝑠𝒩subscript𝝁𝑐subscript𝝈𝑐\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s}\sim\mathcal{N}(\bm{\mathrm{\mu}}_{c},\bm{\mathrm{\sigma}}_{c}) and 𝐳u∼𝒩​(0,𝐈)similar-tosubscript𝐳𝑢𝒩0𝐈\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\bm{\mathrm{I}}) are also considered.

The main contribution of this paper lies in following aspects: (1) for a single input 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} to the encoder, we provide an algorithm to disentangle the latent space into label relevant and irrelevant dimensions in VAE. Previous works like [15, 4, 37] disentangle the latent space in AE not VAE. So it is impossible to make the inference from their model. Moreover, [27, 4, 23] requires at least two inputs for training. (2) we find the Gaussian mixture loss function is suitable way for estimating the parameters of the prior distribution, and it can be optimized in VAE framework. (3) we give both a theoretical derivation and a variety of detailed experiments to explain the effectiveness of our work.

Two types of methods for the structured image generation are VAE and GAN. VAE [20] is a type of parametric model defined by pθ​(𝐱|𝐳)subscript𝑝𝜃conditional𝐱𝐳p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}|\bm{\mathrm{z}}) and qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}), which employs the idea of variational inference to maximize the evidence lower bound (ELBO), as is shown in (1).logp(𝐱)≥𝔼qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)(logpθ(𝐱|𝐳))−DKL(qϕ(𝐳|𝐱)||p(𝐳))\log p(\bm{\mathrm{x}})\geq\mathbb{E}_{q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}})}(\log p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}|\bm{\mathrm{z}}))-D_{\text{KL}}(q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}})||p(\bm{\mathrm{z}}))(1)The right side of the above is the ELBO, which is the lower bound of maximum likelihood. In VAE, a differentiable encoder-decoder are connected, and they are parameterized by ϕitalic-ϕ\phi and θ𝜃\theta, respectively. Eqϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)​(log⁡pθ​(𝐱|𝐳))subscript𝐸subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱subscript𝑝𝜃conditional𝐱𝐳E_{q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}})}(\log p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}|\bm{\mathrm{z}})) represents the end-to-end reconstruction loss, and KL(qϕ(𝐳|𝐱)||p(𝐳))\text{KL}(q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}})||p(\bm{\mathrm{z}})) is the KL divergence between the encoder’s output distribution qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) and the prior p​(𝐳)𝑝𝐳p(\bm{\mathrm{z}}), which is usually modeled by standard normal distribution 𝒩​(0,𝐈)𝒩0𝐈\mathcal{N}(0,\bm{\mathrm{I}}). Note that VAE assumes that the posterior qϕ​(𝐳|𝐱)subscript𝑞italic-ϕconditional𝐳𝐱q_{\phi}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}|\bm{\mathrm{x}}) is of Gaussian, and the 𝝁𝝁\bm{\mathrm{\mu}} and 𝝈𝝈\bm{\mathrm{\sigma}} are estimated for every single input 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} by the encoder. This strategy is named amortized variational inference (AVI), and it is more efficiency than stochastic variational inference (SVI) [17].

VAE’s advantage is that its loss is easy to optimize, but the simple prior in latent space may not capture the complex data patterns which often leads to the mode collapse in latent space. Moreover, VAE’s code is hard to be interpreted. Thus, many works focus on improving VAE on these two aspects. cVAE [39] adds the label vector as the input for both the encoder and decoder, so that the latent code and generated image are conditioned on the label, and potentially prevent the latent collapse. On the other hand, β𝛽\beta-VAE [16, 7] is a unsupervised approach for the latent space disentanglement. It introduces a simple hyper-parameter β𝛽\beta to balance the two loss term in (1). A scheme named infinite mixture of VAEs is proposed and applied in semi-supervised generation [1]. It uses multiple number of VAEs and combines them as a non-parametric mixture model. In [19], the semi-amortized VAE is proposed. It combines AVI with SVI in VAE. Here the SVI estimates the distribution parameters on the whole training set, while the AVI in traditional VAE gives this estimation for a single input.

GAN [13] is another technique to model the data distribution pD​(𝐱)subscript𝑝𝐷𝐱p_{D}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}). It starts from a random 𝐳∼p​(𝐳)similar-to𝐳𝑝𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}}\sim p(\bm{\mathrm{z}}), where p​(𝐳)𝑝𝐳p(\bm{\mathrm{z}}) is simple, e.g. Gaussian, and trains a transform network gθ​(𝐳)subscript𝑔𝜃𝐳g_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}) under the help of discriminator Dϕ​(⋅)subscript𝐷italic-ϕ⋅D_{\phi}(\cdot) so that pθ​(𝐳)subscript𝑝𝜃𝐳p_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{z}}) approximates pD​(𝐱)subscript𝑝𝐷𝐱p_{D}(\bm{\mathrm{x}}). The later works [32, 26, 2, 14, 29] try to stabilize GAN’s training. Traditional GAN works in a fully supervised manner, while cGAN [18, 33, 30, 6] aims to generate images conditioned on labels. In cGAN, the label is given as an input to both the generator and discriminator as a condition for the distribution. The encoder-decoder architecture like AE or VAE can also be used in GAN. In ALI [11] and BiGAN [10], the encoder maps 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}} to 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}}, while the decoder reverses it. The discriminator takes the pair of 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}} and 𝐱𝐱\bm{\mathrm{x}}, and is trained to determine whether it comes from the encoder or decoder in an adversarial manner. In VAE-GAN [22, 24], VAE’s generated data are improved by a discriminator. Similar idea also applies to cVAE in [3]. VAE-GAN also applies in some specific applications like [4, 12].

Since code 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}} potentially affects the generated data, some works try to model its effect and disentangle the dimensions of 𝐳𝐳\bm{\mathrm{z}}. InfoGAN [9] reveals the effect of latent space code c𝑐c by maximizing the mutual information between c𝑐c and the synthetic data gθ​(𝐳,𝐜)subscript𝑔𝜃𝐳𝐜g_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{z,c}}). Its generator outputs gθ​(𝐳,c)subscript𝑔𝜃𝐳𝑐g_{\theta}(\bm{\mathrm{z}},c) which is inspected by the discriminator Dϕ​(⋅)subscript𝐷italic-ϕ⋅D_{\phi}(\cdot). Dϕ​(⋅)subscript𝐷italic-ϕ⋅D_{\phi}(\cdot) also tries to reconstruct the code c𝑐c. In [27], the latent dimension is disentangled in VAE based on the specified factors and unspecified ones, which is similar with our work. But its encoder takes multiple inputs, and the decoder combines codes from different inputs for reconstruction. The work in [15] modifies [27] by taking a single input. To stabilize training, its model is built in AE not VAE, hence it can’t perform variational inference. Other works in [37, 4, 23] are also built in AE and more than two inputs. Moreover they only apply in a particular domain like face [37, 4] or image-to-image translation [23], while our work is built in VAE and takes only a single input for a more general case.

We propose a image generation algorithm based on VAE which divides the encoder into two separate ones, one encoding label relevant representation 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} and the other encoding label irrelevant information 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u}. 𝐳ssubscript𝐳𝑠\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{s} is learned with supervision of the categorical class label and it is required to follow a Gaussian mixture distribution, while 𝐳usubscript𝐳𝑢\bm{\mathrm{z}}_{u} is wished to contain other common information irrelevant to the label and is made close to standard Gaussian 𝒩​(𝟎,𝑰)𝒩0𝑰\mathcal{N}({\bm{0}},{\bm{I}}).

According to the details in Section 3.6, the experiments on semi-supervised image generation are conducted. We find our method can learn well disentangled latent representation when the unlabeled extra data are available. To validate that, we randomly select 200 identities of about 21k images from CASIA [47] dataset and remove their labels to form unlabeled dataset 𝒟usubscript𝒟𝑢\mathcal{D}_{u}. Note that the identities in 𝒟usubscript𝒟𝑢\mathcal{D}_{u} are totally different with those in FaceScrub. Our method can also be applied to image inpainting. It means that given a partly corrupted image, we can extract meaningful latent code to reconstruct the original image. Note that in cVAE-GAN [3], an extra class label c𝑐c should be provided for reconstruction while it’s needless in our method. We propose a latent space disentangling algorithm on VAE baseline. Our model learns two separated encoders and divides the latent code into label relevant and irrelevant dimensions. Together with a discriminator in pixel domain, we show that our model can generate high quality and diverse images, and it can also be applied in semi-supervised image generation in which unlabeled data with unseen classes are given to the encoders. Future research includes building more interpretable latent dimensions with help of more labels, and reducing the correlation between the label relevant and irrelevant codes in our framework.
