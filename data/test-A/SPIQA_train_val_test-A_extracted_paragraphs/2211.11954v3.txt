Our framework, coined DEEPSTORM, is a single-loop algorithm with an attractive property that, besides the initial iteration, each agent only needs m=рқ’ӘвҖӢ(1)рқ‘ҡрқ’Ә1m=\mathcal{O}\left(1\right) stochastic samples to compute a gradient estimate. Intuitively, DEEPSTORM utilizes a momentum based variance reduction techniqueВ (Cutkosky and Orabona 2019; Xu and Xu 2023; Levy, Kavis, and Cevher 2021; Tran-Dinh etВ al. 2022) to guarantee convergence under a small batch size. The use of momentum simultaneously accelerates the computation and communication complexities over non-momentum based methods in the small batch setting; see Table 1 for a comparison. The recent ProxGT-SR-O/EВ (Xin etВ al. 2021) method can also achieve optimal sample complexity for solvingВ (2), but at the expense of performing a double-loop which requires a large (stochastic) gradient computation every time the inner loop is completed. In scenarios where the batch size is uncontrollable, such as streaming or online learning, DEEPSTORM is advantageous.

When discussing sample complexity, it is paramount to specify the impact of the communication graph рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G}. With a constant step size, we show that under a sufficient amount of initial, or transient, iterations and proper initialization, DEEPSTORM behaves similarly to its centralized counterpartsВ (Cutkosky and Orabona 2019; Levy, Kavis, and Cevher 2021; Tran-Dinh etВ al. 2022), while enjoying a linear speed-up with respect to Nрқ‘ҒN.

We summarize the contributions of this work below:

вҖўWe propose a novel decentralized framework, DEEPSTORM, for nonconvex stochastic composite optimization problems. We show that DEEPSTORM achieves the optimal sample complexity with respect to solution accuracy, where each agent needs only рқ’ӘвҖӢ(1)рқ’Ә1\mathcal{O}\left(1\right) samples to compute a local stochastic gradient. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first decentralized method that achieves optimal sample complexity for solving stochastic composite problems by using only small batches.вҖўAdditionally, we establish convergence guarantees of DEEPSTORM with both constant and diminishing step sizes. When a constant step size is used, we show that under sufficiently many transient iterations and proper initialization, DEEPSTORM achieves a linear speed-up with respect to Nрқ‘ҒN, signifying an advantage over analogous centralized variance reduction methodsВ (Cutkosky and Orabona 2019; Levy, Kavis, and Cevher 2021; Tran-Dinh etВ al. 2022).
