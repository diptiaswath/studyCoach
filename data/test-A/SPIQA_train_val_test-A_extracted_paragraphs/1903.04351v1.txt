We study data reduction (namely, coresets) for a class of clustering problems,called ordered weighted clustering,which generalizes the classical k𝑘k-Center and k𝑘k-Median problems.In these clustering problems, the objective function is computedby ordering the n𝑛n data points by their distance to their closest center,then taking a weighted sum of these distances,using predefined weights v1≥⋯≥vn≥0subscript𝑣1⋯subscript𝑣𝑛0v_{1}\geq\cdots\geq v_{n}\geq 0.These clustering problems can interpolate betweenk𝑘k-Center (the special case where v1=1subscript𝑣11v_{1}=1 is the only non-zero weight)and k𝑘k-Median (unit weights vi=1subscript𝑣𝑖1v_{i}=1 for all i𝑖i),and therefore offer flexibility in prioritizing points with large service cost,which may be important for applicationslike Pareto (multi-objective) optimization and fair clustering.In general, fairness in machine learning is seeing a surge in interest,and is well-known to have many facets.In the context of clustering, previous worksuch as the fairlets approach of  [CKLV17],has addressed protected classes, which must be identified in advance.In contrast, ordered weighted clustering addresses fairnesstowards remote points (which can be underprivileged communities),without specifying them in advance.This is starkly different from many application domains,where remote points are considered as outliers (to be ignored)or anomalies (to be detected),see e.g., the well-known survey by  [CBK09].

We start with discussing Theorem 1.2(which is a building block for Theorem 1.1).Its proof is inspired by  [HK07],who constructed coresets for k𝑘k-Median clustering in ℝdsuperscriptℝ𝑑\mathbb{R}^{d}by reducing the problem to its one-dimensional case.We can apply a similar reduction,but the one-dimensional case of p𝑝p-Centrum is significantly differentfrom k𝑘k-Median.One fundamental difference is that the objective counts only the p𝑝p largest distances, hence the subset of “contributing” points depends on the center.We deal with this issue by introducing a new bucketing schemeand a charging argument that relates the error to the p𝑝p largest distances.See Section 3 for more details.

The problem of constructing strong coresets for k𝑘k-Means, k𝑘k-Median, and other objectives has received significant attention from the research community [FMSW10, FL11, LS10, BHPI02, Che09].For example,  [HM04]designed the first strong coreset for k𝑘k-Means. [FSS13] provided coresets for k𝑘k-Means,PCA and projective clustering that are independent of the dimension.Recently,  [SW18] generalized the results of  [FSS13] and obtained strong coresets for k𝑘k-Median and for subspace approximation that are independent of the dimension d𝑑d.

Previous work on fairness in clusteringhas followed the disparate impact doctrine of  [FFM+15],and addressed fairness with respect to protected classes,where each cluster in the solution should fairly represent every class. [CKLV17]have designed approximation algorithms for k𝑘k-Center and k𝑘k-Median,and their results were refined and extendedby  [RS18] and  [BCN19].Recent work by  [SSS18] designs coresets for fair k𝑘k-Means clustering.However, these results are not applicable to ordered weighted clustering.
