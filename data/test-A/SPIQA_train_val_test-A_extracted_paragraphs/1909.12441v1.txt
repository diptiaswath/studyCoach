In the least squares regression problem, we are given an m×n𝑚𝑛m\times n matrix A𝐴A and an m×1𝑚1m\times 1 vector b𝑏b, and we seek to find an x∈ℝn𝑥superscriptℝ𝑛x\in\mathbb{R}^{n} which minimizes ‖A​x−b‖22superscriptsubscriptnorm𝐴𝑥𝑏22\|Ax-b\|_{2}^{2}. A natural geometric interpretation is that there is an unknown hyperplane in ℝn+1superscriptℝ𝑛1\mathbb{R}^{n+1}, specified by the normal vector x𝑥x, for which we have m𝑚m points on this hyperplane, the i𝑖i-th of which is given by (Ai,⟨Ai,x⟩)subscript𝐴𝑖subscript𝐴𝑖𝑥(A_{i},\langle A_{i},x\rangle), where Aisubscript𝐴𝑖A_{i} is the i𝑖i-th row of A𝐴A. However, due to noisy observations, we do not see the points (Ai,⟨Ai,x⟩)subscript𝐴𝑖subscript𝐴𝑖𝑥(A_{i},\langle A_{i},x\rangle), but rather only see the point (Ai,bi)subscript𝐴𝑖subscript𝑏𝑖(A_{i},b_{i}), and we seek to find the hyperplane which best fits these points, where we measure (squared) distance only on the (n+1)𝑛1(n+1)-st coordinate. This naturally generalizes to the setting in which B𝐵B is an m×d𝑚𝑑m\times d matrix, and the true points have the form (Ai,Ai​X)subscript𝐴𝑖subscript𝐴𝑖𝑋(A_{i},A_{i}X) for some unknown n×d𝑛𝑑n\times d matrix X𝑋X. This setting is called multiple-response regression, in which one seeks to find X𝑋X to minimize ‖A​X−B‖F2superscriptsubscriptnorm𝐴𝑋𝐵𝐹2\|AX-B\|_{F}^{2}, where for a matrix Y𝑌Y, ‖Y‖F2superscriptsubscriptnorm𝑌𝐹2\|Y\|_{F}^{2} is its squared Frobenius norm, i.e., the sum of squares of each of its entries. This geometrically corresponds to the setting when the points live in a lower n𝑛n-dimensional flat of ℝn+dsuperscriptℝ𝑛𝑑\mathbb{R}^{n+d}, rather than in a hyperplane.

While extremely useful, in some settings the above regression model may not be entirely realistic. For example, it is quite natural that the matrix A𝐴A may also have been corrupted by measurement noise. In this case, one should also be allowed to first change entries of A𝐴A, obtaining a new m×n𝑚𝑛m\times n matrix A^^𝐴\widehat{A}, then try to fit B𝐵B to A^^𝐴\widehat{A} by solving a multiple-response regression problem. One should again be penalized for how much one changes the entries of A𝐴A, and this leads to a popular formulation known as the total least squares optimization problem minA^,X⁡‖A−A^‖F2+‖A^​X−B‖F2subscript^𝐴𝑋superscriptsubscriptnorm𝐴^𝐴𝐹2superscriptsubscriptnorm^𝐴𝑋𝐵𝐹2\min_{\widehat{A},X}\|A-\widehat{A}\|_{F}^{2}+\|\widehat{A}X-B\|_{F}^{2}. Notice that C^^𝐶\widehat{C} has rank n𝑛n, and therefore the optimal cost is at least ‖C−Cn‖F2superscriptsubscriptnorm𝐶subscript𝐶𝑛𝐹2\|C-C_{n}\|_{F}^{2}, where Cnsubscript𝐶𝑛C_{n} is the best rank-n𝑛n approximation to C𝐶C.
