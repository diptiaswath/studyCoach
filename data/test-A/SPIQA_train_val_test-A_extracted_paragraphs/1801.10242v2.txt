By exploiting a low-rank structural condition that naturally emerges in dynamic pricing problems, this work introduces an online bandit optimization algorithm whose regret provably depends only on the intrinsic rank of the problem rather than the ambient dimensionality of the action space. Our low-rank bandit approach to dynamic pricing scales to a large number of products with intercorrelated demand curves, even if the underlying demand model varies over time in an adversarial fashion.
