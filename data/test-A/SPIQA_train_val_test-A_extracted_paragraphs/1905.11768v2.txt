⋄⋄\diamond Simulations. We illustrate the promise of our approach numerically by performing experiments with SPLA. We first apply SPLA to a stochastic and nonsmooth toy model with a ground truth. As expected, the numerical experiments show the advantage of using stochastic proximity operators instead of stochastic subgradients. It is a standard fact that proximity operators are better than subgradients to tackle ℓ1superscriptℓ1\ell^{1}-norm terms [2]. Our figures show that stochastic proximity operators are numerically more stable than alternatives [43]. Our figures also show the advantage of stochastic methods (SSLA or SPLA) over deterministic ones for large scale problems. The SSLA and the SPLA provide iterates about one hundred times more frequently than ProxLA, and are faster in the first iterations.
