Online learning algorithms are designed to solve prediction andlearning problems for streaming dataor batch data whose volume is too large to be processed all at once.Applications include online auctions [1], online classification and regression [2],online subspace identification [3],as well as online resource allocation [4].

The general procedure for online learning algorithmsis as follows:at each time t𝑡t, before the true time-dependent objective function ft​(θ)subscript𝑓𝑡𝜃f_{t}(\theta) is revealed,we need to make the prediction, θtsubscript𝜃𝑡\theta_{t},based on the history of the observations fi​(θ)subscript𝑓𝑖𝜃f_{i}(\theta), i<t𝑖𝑡i<t.Then the value of ft​(θt)subscript𝑓𝑡subscript𝜃𝑡f_{t}(\theta_{t}) is the loss suffered due to the lackof the knowledge for the true objective function ft​(θ)subscript𝑓𝑡𝜃f_{t}(\theta).Our prediction of θ𝜃\theta is then updated to include the information of ft​(θ)subscript𝑓𝑡𝜃f_{t}(\theta).This whole process is repeated until termination. The functions,ft​(θ)subscript𝑓𝑡𝜃f_{t}(\theta),can be chosen from a function class in an arbitrary, possiblyadversarial manner.
