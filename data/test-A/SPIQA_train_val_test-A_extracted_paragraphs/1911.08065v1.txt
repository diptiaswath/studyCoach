This insight can be incorporated into TAAN by introducing regularization term on αlsubscript𝛼𝑙\alpha_{l} during training. We propose two functional regularization methods to further enhance the performances of TAAN.

We first define a baseline method that is introduced from literature work. In the experiment section, we will show that this baseline has little benefit for the MTL performance of TAAN.

The proposed regularization term ℒt​nsubscriptℒ𝑡𝑛\mathcal{L}_{tn} corresponds to the hypothesis on linear dependency. As the relationship between the coordinate αlsubscript𝛼𝑙\alpha_{l} and the corresponding function is ambiguous, ℒt​nsubscriptℒ𝑡𝑛\mathcal{L}_{tn} may not introduce beneficial structure into the activation functions.

In this paper, we propose two functional regularization methods that penalize the dissimilarity of the task-specific activation functions.
