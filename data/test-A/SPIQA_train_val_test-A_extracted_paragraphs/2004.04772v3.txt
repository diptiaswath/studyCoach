In this work, we studied composable sampling sketches under two beyond-worst-case scenarios. In the first, we assumed additional information about the input distribution in the form of advice. We designed and analyzed a sampling sketch based on the advice, and demonstrated its performance on real-world datasets.

In the second scenario, we proposed a framework where the performance and statistical guarantees of sampling sketches were analyzed in terms of supported frequency-function combinations.We demonstrated analytically and empirically that sketches originallydesigned to sample according to ‚Äúeasy‚ÄĚ functions of frequencies on ‚Äúhard‚ÄĚ frequencydistributions turned out to be accurate for sampling according to ‚Äúhard‚ÄĚ functions of frequencieson ‚Äúpractical‚ÄĚ frequency distributions.In particular, on ‚Äúpractical‚ÄĚ distributions we could accuratelyapproximate high frequency moments (p>2ūĚĎĚ2p>2) and the rank versusfrequency distribution using small composable sketches.
