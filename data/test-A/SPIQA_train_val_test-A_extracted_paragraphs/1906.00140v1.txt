Online social networks (e.g., Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and Sina Weibo) have become vital platforms for connecting users to share information, post daily life events, and spread influence Kempe et al. (2003); Wilder et al. (2018); Zhang et al. (2017, 2018b). Due to privacy concerns, users tend to hide their connections, leading such private relationships not visible to other users in public but only themselves. For instance, Facebook users are likely to conceal their friend-list Dey et al. (2012); Weibo users may prefer using the secret following feature, which hides their interested followees. Public-private graphs are developed to model this kind of social networks Chierichetti et al. (2015). A public-private network contains a public graph which is visible and accessible to everyone; in addition, each vertex has a personal private graph only visible to its owner. Therefore, in the view of each user, the social network is a union of the public graph and its own private graph which can be significantly different for distinct users. Recently, many graph analytic tasks have been investigated on public-private networks, such as all-pairs shortest path distances, node similarities, and correlation clustering.

Dense subgraph discovery is a fundamental problem of many network analysis tasks. Numerous definitions of dense subgraphs have been proposed and investigated, e.g., clique, quasi-clique Pei et al. (2005), nрқ‘ӣn-clan Mokken (1979), nрқ‘ӣn-club Mokken (1979), and kрқ‘ҳk-plex Xiao et al. (2017). Recently, a popular notion of dense subgraphs that has been studied is kрқ‘ҳk-truss. A kрқ‘ҳk-truss is the largest subgraph of a graph such that each edge is contained in at least kвҲ’2рқ‘ҳ2k-2 triangles within this subgraph. Finding kрқ‘ҳk-trusses has many useful applications such as community search Jiang et al. (2018), complex network visualization Zhao and Tung (2012), and task-driven team formation Huang et al. (2016). To the best of our knowledge, finding kрқ‘ҳk-truss over public-private networks has not yet been studied in the literature. In this paper, we formulate the problem of finding public-private kрқ‘ҳk-truss as follows. Given a query vertex and parameter kрқ‘ҳk, the problem is to find kрқ‘ҳk-truss in the public-private graph owned by this query vertex.

Efficient extraction of public-private kрқ‘ҳk-truss raises significant challenges. A straightforward approach is to ignore usersвҖҷ private edges, which can lead to inaccurate results. Another approach is to applytruss decomposition on the public-private graph of the query vertex to extract the kрқ‘ҳk-truss with the given parameter kрқ‘ҳk. However, this method computes kрқ‘ҳk-truss from scratch, which is particularly inefficient for large-scale networks. To tackle these challenges, we develop an index-based computational paradigm to efficiently updatea truss indexon a public graph using the edges in a private graph, with a minimal amount of recomputation on the public graph.

To summarize, we make the following contributions:

вҲҷвҲҷ\bulletWe formulate a new problem of finding kрқ‘ҳk-truss over public-private graphs, that is finding kрқ‘ҳk-truss in public-private graph owned by a given query vertex (SectionВ 3).вҲҷвҲҷ\bulletWe analyze the structural properties of kрқ‘ҳk-truss on public-private networks. Based on the observations, we develop kрқ‘ҳk-truss updating algorithm using a hybrid strategy of node/edge insertions/deletions (SectionВ 4).вҲҷвҲҷ\bulletWe validate the efficiency of our proposed methods through extensive experiments on real-world datasets of public-private networks (SectionВ 5).

The problem of public-private kрқ‘ҳk-truss discovery studied in this paper is formulated as follows.

Problem formulation: Given a public-private graph рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G}, a vertex uвҲҲVрқ‘ўрқ‘үu\in V, and an integer kвүҘ2рқ‘ҳ2k\geq 2, the problem is to find the kрқ‘ҳk-truss in the personalized pp-graph gusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўg_{u} where gu=GвҲӘGusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўрқҗәsubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўg_{u}=G\cup G_{u}.

This section introduces our algorithms for finding kрқ‘ҳk-truss in personalized pp-graph gusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўg_{u}, w.r.t. a query vertex uрқ‘ўu. We first give an overview of our ideas in Section 4.1, and then present a well thorough description of technical details afterward.

We consider two different ideas.

Solution 1: online search algorithm. One intuitive approach is to apply truss decomposition on pp-graph gusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўg_{u} to iteratively remove edges with less than kвҲ’2рқ‘ҳ2k-2 triangles and output the remaining graph as answers. However, such computing kрқ‘ҳk-truss from scratch on gusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўg_{u} for each query vertex uрқ‘ўu is obviously inefficient for big graphs with a large number of vertices.

Solution 2: index-based search algorithm. Recall that personalized pp-graph gu=GвҲӘGusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўрқҗәsubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўg_{u}=G\cup G_{u} has a public graph GрқҗәG and a private graph Gusubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўG_{u} only available to uрқ‘ўu. The public graph GрқҗәG is available to everyone, and the structure of GрқҗәG is identical to each query vertex uрқ‘ўu. The idea of index-based search algorithms is to construct a structural index of public graph GрқҗәG offline, and then online find kрқ‘ҳk-truss based on the precomputed index of GрқҗәG and additional graph Gusubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўG_{u}. In the following, we introduce a concept of trussness, which is useful for constructing the truss-index for kрқ‘ҳk-truss discovery.

We drop the subscript and denote П„GвҖӢ(e)subscriptрқңҸрқҗәрқ‘’\tau_{G}(e) as П„вҖӢ(e)рқңҸрқ‘’\tau(e) when the context is obvious. According to Def.В 4, kрқ‘ҳk-truss of GрқҗәG is the union of all edges eрқ‘’e with П„вҖӢ(e)=kрқңҸрқ‘’рқ‘ҳ\tau(e)=k. The truss-index of public graph GрқҗәG keeps the trussness of all edges in GрқҗәG. Given a truss-index of GрқҗәG, the remaining issue is how to update the truss-index for pp-graph GвҲӘGuрқҗәsubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўG\cup G_{u}, w.r.t. the additional Gusubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўG_{u}.

Updating truss-index using edge insertions. A simple approach is to add edges of Gusubscriptрқҗәрқ‘ўG_{u} one-by-one into GрқҗәG and update the truss-index accordingly, by using an existing edge-insertion algorithm Huang et al. (2014). Our approach.To address the above issue, we propose a batch-update algorithm using node-insertion. The idea is to simultaneously insert a new node uрқ‘ўu with all its incident edges into graph GрқҗәG at the same time, and call node-insertion algorithm only once. In the above example, it needs only one node-insertion of the isolated node v5subscriptрқ‘Ј5v_{5} and all of its private edges. To handle the truss-index update with node insertions efficiently, the key is to identify the affected region in the graph precisely. We provide a theoretical analysis to define the affected scope in Section 4.2, and the detailed algorithm of node-insertion in SectionВ 4.3.However, when uрқ‘ўu has both public and private edges, we can first remove uрқ‘ўu with its public edges, and re-insert it with both public and private edges incident to uрқ‘ўu using node-insertion. This method has significant advantages outperforming edge-insertion when private edges of uрқ‘ўu are much larger than public edges of uрқ‘ўu. On other cases, edge-insertion method may perform better.Therefore, we construct a classifier to determine which algorithms of node-insertion and edge-insertion should be applied. This classification-based hybrid approach is developed to fastfind kрқ‘ҳk-truss in pp-graph gusubscriptрқ‘”рқ‘ўg_{u}, which is presented in SectionВ 4.4.

This paper studies the problem of finding k-truss on public-private graphs. We develop a novel hybrid algorithm of k-truss updating with node/edge insertions/deletions, which can incrementally compute k-truss on public-private networks.This work opens up several interesting problems. First, developing further efficient and clever algorithms for finding kрқ‘ҳk-truss in pp-graphs is important, instead of deleting and re-inserting nodes as рқ–өрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣрқ—Қрқ–ҫрқ—‘рқ–өрқ–ҫрқ—Ӣрқ—Қрқ–ҫрқ—‘\mathsf{Vertex}-рқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝрқ–Ҝ\mathsf{PP}. Second, finding other kinds of dense subgraphs on public-private graph is also wide open.
