The classical Vertex Cover problem is among the most well-studied NP-hard problems on static graphs especially in the context of parameterized algorithmics.In fact, it is often referred to as the Drosophila of parameterized complexity[8, 11].Real-world graphs, however, are often dynamic and change over time.In modern applications, this temporal information is readily available and allows for more realistic models.This leads algorithmic research to focus on temporal graphs[2, 4, 6, 9, 10, 14, 22, 28].In a temporal graph, edges can appear and disappear over time, which is formalized by a sequence E1,…,Eτsubscript𝐸1…subscript𝐸𝜏E_{1},\ldots,E_{\tau} of edge sets over a fixed set V𝑉V of vertices, where τ≥1𝜏1\tau\geq 1 denotes the lifetime of the temporal graph.Several classical graph problems have been studied on temporal graphs [1, 5, 17, 20, 21, 29].

In this paper, we study the network untangling problem introduced by Rozenshtein et al. [26, 27], which is a temporal variant of Vertex Cover and motivated by data mining applications such as discovering event timelines and summarizing temporal networks.Here, edges in the temporal graph model certain interactions between entities (vertices).The goal is to explain the observed interactions by selecting few (and short) activity intervals for each entity such that at the time of an interaction at least one of the two entities is active.The formal definition is as follows:Let 𝒢=(V,(Ei)i∈[τ])𝒢𝑉subscriptsubscript𝐸𝑖𝑖delimited-[]𝜏\mathcal{G}=(V,(E_{i})_{i\in[\tau]}) be a temporal graph.A k𝑘k-activity timeline is a set 𝒯𝒯\mathcal{T} containing at most k𝑘k time intervals for each vertex, that is, 𝒯⊆{(v,a,b)∈V×[τ]×[τ]∣a≤b}𝒯conditional-set𝑣𝑎𝑏𝑉delimited-[]𝜏delimited-[]𝜏𝑎𝑏\mathcal{T}\subseteq\{(v,a,b)\in V\times[\tau]\times[\tau]\mid a\leq b\} such that |{(v,a,b)∈𝒯}|≤k𝑣𝑎𝑏𝒯𝑘|\{(v,a,b)\in\mathcal{T}\}|\leq k for each v∈V𝑣𝑉v\in V.We say that 𝒯𝒯\mathcal{T} covers 𝒢𝒢\mathcal{G} if for each t∈[τ]𝑡delimited-[]𝜏t\in[\tau] and each {u,v}∈Et𝑢𝑣subscript𝐸𝑡\{u,v\}\in E_{t}, 𝒯𝒯\mathcal{T} contains some (u,a,b)𝑢𝑎𝑏(u,a,b) with t∈[a,b]𝑡𝑎𝑏t\in[a,b] or some (v,a,b)𝑣𝑎𝑏(v,a,b) with t∈[a,b]𝑡𝑎𝑏t\in[a,b].That is, for each time step t𝑡t, the set {v∈V∣(v,a,b)∈𝒯,t∈[a,b]}conditional-set𝑣𝑉formulae-sequence𝑣𝑎𝑏𝒯𝑡𝑎𝑏\{v\in V\mid(v,a,b)\in\mathcal{T},t\in[a,b]\} must be a vertex cover for the graph Gt=(V,Et)subscript𝐺𝑡𝑉subscript𝐸𝑡G_{t}=(V,E_{t}).The task is to find a k𝑘k-activity timeline that minimizes some objective regarding the interval lengths.Rozenshtein et al. Rozenshtein et al. [27] showed that both problems are NP-hard and hard to approximate in polynomial time.They focus on developing heuristics and show in a case study that activity timelines can successfully be recovered for real-world data of Twitter users.In this work, we provide a deeper complexity-theoretic understanding of both problemsthrough the lens of parameterized complexity theory.To this end, we study the influence of several natural problem parameters on the algorithmic complexity, namely, the number n𝑛n of vertices, the lifetime τ𝜏\tau, the maximum number k𝑘k of intervals per vertex, and the interval length bound ℓℓ\ell.In our multivariate analysis, we identify for both problems which parameter combinations yield fixed-parameter tractability and which combinations are intractable.In doing so, we reveal interesting connections to seemingly unrelated problems like graph coloring and bin packing.Our results yield an (almost) tight characterization and pave the way for a comprehensive picture of the computational complexity landscape of network untangling.

As the literature on edge covering is extremely rich,we only consider studies closely related to our setting.For a broader overview on the topic of temporal network mining, we refer to a recent tutorial [25].Our main reference is the work by Rozenshtein et al. [27] who introduced both problems and showed that MinTimeline∞ is polynomial-time solvable for k=1𝑘1k=1, whereas MinTimeline+ is NP-hard for k=1𝑘1k=1.Moreover, they showed that both problems are NP-hard for ℓ=0ℓ0\ell=0 with unbounded k𝑘k and thus not approximable in polynomial time.They develop efficient heuristics to solve the problems and provide experiments to evaluate the performance of their approaches.

Akrida et al. [1] studied a different variant of Vertex Cover on temporal graphs.Their model expects an edge to be covered at least once over every time window of some given size ΔΔ\Delta.That is,they define a temporal vertex cover as a set S⊆V×[τ]𝑆𝑉delimited-[]𝜏S\subseteq V\times[\tau] such that,for every time window of size ΔΔ\Delta and for each edge e={v,w}𝑒𝑣𝑤e=\{v,w\} appearing in a time step contained in the time window,it holds that (v,t)∈S𝑣𝑡𝑆(v,t)\in S or (w,t)∈S𝑤𝑡𝑆(w,t)\in S for some t𝑡t in the time window with time-edge (e,t)𝑒𝑡(e,t).Among other results, they provide NP-hardness results in very restricted settings.

Fluschnik et al. [12] and Heeger et al. [13]studied the parameterized complexity of a “multistage” variant of Vertex Cover.Here, one is given a temporal graphand seeks a sequenceof vertex covers of size at most k𝑘k(one for each layer of the temporal graph)such that consecutive vertex covers are in some sense similar,e.g., the symmetric difference of two consecutive vertex covers is upper-bounded by some value [12] orthe sum of symmetric differences of all consecutive vertex covers is upper-bounded by some value [13].
