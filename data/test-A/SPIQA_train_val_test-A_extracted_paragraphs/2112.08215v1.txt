Walrasian equilibrium (WE) is a classical and appealing market equilibrium notion that dates back to the 70’s (Walras [25]). In a WE, despite competition among buyers, every buyer is maximally happy with her bundle and the market clears.That is, a WE is given by a tuple (S,p)\textbf{S},\textbf{p}) satisfying: (i) Utility maximization: ui​(Si,p)≥ui​(T,p)subscript𝑢𝑖subscript𝑆𝑖psubscript𝑢𝑖𝑇pu_{i}(S_{i},\textbf{p})\geq u_{i}(T,\textbf{p}) for every bundle T⊆[m]𝑇delimited-[]𝑚T\subseteq[m], and (ii) Market clearance: all items are sold.Moreover, by the first welfare theorem (Bikhchandani and Mamer [3]), any allocation supported in a WE has optimal social welfare.

Furthermore, we find an interesting connection between 2PE and pure Nash equilibria (PNE) of simultaneous item auctions [5].
