In this paper, we propose a simple, effective, and efficient model, FlowSeq, which models expressive prior distribution pОёвҖӢ(рқҗі|рқҗұ)subscriptрқ‘қрқңғconditionalрқҗірқҗұp_{\theta}(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x}) using a powerful mathematical framework called generative flow Rezende and Mohamed (2015).This framework can elegantly model complex distributions, and has obtained remarkable success in modeling continuous data such as images and speech through efficient density estimation and sampling Kingma and Dhariwal (2018); Prenger etВ al. FlowSeq is a flow-based sequence-to-sequence model, which is (to our knowledge) the first non-autoregressive seq2seq model utilizing generative flows.It allows for efficient parallel decoding while modeling the joint distribution of the output sequence.Experimentally, on three benchmark datasets for machine translation вҖ“ WMT2014, WMT2016 and IWSLT-2014,FlowSeq achieves comparable performance with state-of-the-art non-autoregressive models, and almost constant decoding time w.r.t.В the sequence length compared to a typical left-to-right Transformer model, which is super-linear.

As noted above, incorporating expressive latent variables рқҗірқҗі\mathbf{z} is essential to decouple the dependencies between tokens in the target sequence in non-autoregressive models.However, in order to model all of the complexities of sequence generation to the point that we can read off all of the words in the output in an independent fashion (as in Eq.В (4)), the prior distribution pОёвҖӢ(рқҗі|рқҗұ)subscriptрқ‘қрқңғconditionalрқҗірқҗұp_{\theta}(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x}) will necessarily be quite complex.In this section, we describe generative flows Rezende and Mohamed (2015), an effective method for arbitrary modeling of complicated distributions, before describing how we apply them to sequence-to-sequence generation in В§3.

We propose FlowSeq, an efficient and effective model for non-autoregressive sequence generation by using generative flows.One potential direction for future work is to leverage iterative refinement techniques such as masked language models to further improve translation quality.Another exciting direction is to, theoretically and empirically, investigate the latent space in FlowSeq, hence providing deeper insights into the model, and allowing for additional applications such as controllable text generation.
