The method by which to achieve this goal however, remains an open research question. Weak and semi-supervised settings, e.g. using data pairs or auxiliary variables, can provably offer disentanglement (Bouchacourt etВ al., 2018; Locatello etВ al., 2020b; Khemakhem etВ al., 2020; Klindt etВ al., 2021).But fully unsupervised disentanglement вҖ“ our focus in this study вҖ“ is in theory impossible to achieve in the general unconstrained nonlinear caseВ (HyvГӨrinen & Pajunen, 1999; Locatello etВ al., 2019b).In practice however the inductive biases embodied in common autoencoder architectures allow for effective practical disentanglementВ (Rolinek etВ al., 2019).Perhaps more problematic, standard unsupervised disentanglement methodsВ (s.a. Higgins etВ al. (2017); Kim & Mnih (2018); Chen etВ al. (2018)) rely on an unrealistic assumption of statistical independence of ground truth factors.Real data however contains correlations (TrГӨuble etВ al., 2021).Even with well defined factors (s.a. shape, color or background), correlations are pervasiveвҖ”yellow bananas are more frequent than red ones; cows more often on grass than sand.In more realistic settings with correlations, prior work (e.g. TrГӨuble etВ al. (2021); Dittadi etВ al. (2021)) has shown existing disentanglement methods to fail.

To address this limitation, we propose to relax the unrealistic assumption of statistical independence of factors (i.e. that they have a factorial distribution), and only assume the (bounded) support of the factorsвҖҷ distribution factorizes вҖ“a much weaker but more realistic constraint.For example, in a dataset of animal images (Fig. 1), background and animal are heavily correlated (camels most likely on sand, cows on grass), resulting in most datapoints being distributed along the diagonal as opposed to uniformly.Under the original assumption of factor independence, a model likely learns a shortcut solution where animal and landscape share the same latent correspondence (Beery etВ al., 2018).On the other hand with a factorized support, learned factors should be such that any combination of their values has some grounding in reality: a cow on sand is an unlikely, yet not impossible combination.We still rely, just as standard unsupervised disentanglement methods, on the inductive bias of encoder-decoder architectures to recover factors (Rolinek etВ al., 2019).However, we expect our method to facilitate robustness to any distribution shifts within the support (TrГӨuble etВ al., 2021; Dittadi etВ al., 2021),as it makes no assumptions on the distribution beyond its factorized support.We arrived at this factorized support principle from the perspective of relaxing the independence assumption to be robust to factor correlations, while remaining agnostic to how they may arise. Disentangled Representation Learningaims to recover representation spaces where each ground-truth generative factor is encoded in a unique entry or subspace (Bengio etВ al., 2013; Higgins etВ al., 2018) to benefit subsequent downstream transfer (Bengio etВ al., 2013; Peters etВ al., 2017; Tschannen etВ al., 2018; Locatello etВ al., 2019b; Montero etВ al., 2021; Mancini etВ al., 2021; Roth etВ al., 2020; Funke etВ al., 2022), interpretability (Chen etВ al., 2016; Esser etВ al., 2018; Niemeyer & Geiger, 2021) and fairness (Locatello etВ al., 2019a; TrГӨuble etВ al., 2021; Dullerud etВ al., 2022) via compositionality of representations.Methods often rely on Variational AutoEncoders (VAEs) variants (Kingma & Welling, 2014; Rezende etВ al., 2014) to constrain the (aggregate) posterior of the encoder, e.g. via penalties on the bottleneck capacity (ОІрқӣҪ\beta-VAE (Higgins etВ al., 2017)) with progressive constraints or network growing (AnnealedVAE (Burgess etВ al., 2018), ProVAE (Li etВ al., 2020)), the total correlation (ОІрқӣҪ\beta-TCVAE (Chen etВ al., 2018), FactorVAE (Kim & Mnih, 2018)) or the mismatch to some factorized prior (DIP-VAE (Kumar etВ al., 2018), DoubleVAE (Mita etВ al., 2020)).These approaches assume statistically independent factors, which is invalid for realistic data as motivated in В§1.

Disentanglement under correlated factors. Consequently, while most methods have been shown to perform well on toy datasets and ones with known independent factors such as Shapes3D (Kim & Mnih, 2018), MPI3D (Gondal etВ al., 2019), DSprites (Higgins etВ al., 2017), SmallNorb (LeCun etВ al., 2004) or Cars3D (Reed etВ al., 2015), recent research (Montero etВ al., 2021; TrГӨuble etВ al., 2021; Montero etВ al., 2022; Funke etВ al., 2022; Dittadi etВ al., 2021) has started to connect these setups to more realistic scenarios with factor correlations: TrГӨuble etВ al. (2021) introduce artificial correlations between two factors, and Montero etВ al. (2021) exclude value combinations for recombination studies. In such settings, Montero etВ al. (2021; 2022); TrГӨuble etВ al. (2021); Dittadi etВ al. (2021) show that unsupervised disentanglement methods that assume independent factors fail to disentangle, with potentially negative impact to OOD generalization. Suter etВ al. (2019) propose a causal metric to evaluate disentanglement when assuming confounders between the ground-truth factors. Choi etВ al. (2020) introduce a Gaussian mixture model for dependencies between continuous and discrete variables in a structured setup with number of mixtures known.By contrast, we investigate a generic remedy without explicit auxiliary variables or prior models by relaxing the independence assumption to only a pairwise factorized support. Pfau etВ al. (2020) propose geometrically motivated non-parametric unsupervised disentanglement following the symmetry-based definition in Higgins etВ al. (2018) by leveraging holonomy of manifold geometries as learning signal to find disentangled subspaces.This does not assume statistical independence, but requires non-trivial holonomy for each factor manifold, and struggles in high-dimensional spaces and generalization to new data. For domain adaptation shifts, Tong etВ al. (2022) propose adversarial support matching, highlighting that operating on the support can be beneficial for related settings as well.To evaluate disentanglement, we utilize DCI-D (part of DCI вҖ“ Disentanglement, Completeness, Informativeness, see Eastwood & Williams (2018)) as leading metric. As opposed to other metrics s.a. Beta-/FactorVAE scores (Higgins etВ al., 2017; Kim & Mnih, 2018), MI Gap (Chen etВ al., 2018), Modularity (Ridgeway & Mozer, 2018) or SAP (Kumar etВ al., 2018), Locatello etВ al. To avoid the unrealistic assumption of factors independence (i.e. factorial distribution) as in traditional disentanglement, which stands in contrast to realistic data being correlated, we thoroughly investigate an approach that only aims at recovering a factorized support. Doing so achieves disentanglement by ensuring the model can encode many possible combinations of generative factors in the learned latent space, while allowing for arbitrary distributions over the support вҖ“ in particular those with correlations.Indeed, through a practical criterion using pairwise Hausdorff set-distances вҖ“ HFS вҖ“ we show that encouraging a pairwise factorized support is sufficient to match traditional disentanglement methods.Furthermore we show that HFS can steer existing disentanglement methods towards a more factorized support, giving large relative improvements of over +60%percent60+60\% on common benchmarks across a large variety ofcorrelation shifts.We find this improvement in disentanglement across correlation shifts to be also reflected in improved out-of-distribution generalization especially as these shifts become more severe; tackling a key promise for disentangled representation learning.
