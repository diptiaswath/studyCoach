Recently learned sparse retrieval techniques (Zamani et al., 2018; Dai and Callan, 2020; Mallia et al., 2021; Lin and Ma, 2021; Gao et al., 2021; Formal et al., 2021b, a, 2022)have become attractivebecause such a representation can deliver a strong relevanceby leveraging transformer-based models to expand document tokens with learned weightsand can take advantage of traditional inverted index based retrieval techniques (Mallia et al., 2017, 2022).Its query processing is cheaper than a dense representation which requires GPU support(e.g.  (Xiong et al., 2021; Ren et al., 2021; Santhanam et al., 2021))even with efficiency optimization through approximate nearest neighborsearch (Johnson et al., 2019; Zhan et al., 2022; Xiao et al., 2022).

This paper focuses on the SPLADE family of sparserepresentations (Formal et al., 2021b, a, 2022)because it can delivera high MRR@10 score for MS MARCO passage ranking (Craswell et al., 2020) anda strong zero-shot performance for the BEIR datasets (Thakur et al., 2021), which are well-recognized IR benchmarks.The sparsification optimization in SPLADE has used L1 and FLOPS regularization to minimize non-zero weightsduring model learning, and our objective is to exploit additional opportunities to further increase the sparsity ofinverted indices produced by SPLADE.Earlier static inverted index pruning research  (Carmel et al., 2001; Blanco and Barreiro, 2007; Büttcher and Clarke, 2006) for a lexical model has shownthe usefulness of trimming a term posting list or a document by a limit.Yang et al. (Yang et al., 2021) conduct top token masking by limiting the top activated weight count uniformlyper documentand gradually reducing this weight count limit to a targeted constant during training.Motivated by these studies (Carmel et al., 2001; Blanco and Barreiro, 2007; Büttcher and Clarke, 2006; Yang et al., 2021) andsince they have not addressed the learnability of a pruning limit through relevance-driven training,this paper exploits a learnable thresholding architectureto filter out unimportant neural weights produced by the SPLADE model through joint training.

The contribution of this paper isa learnable hybrid hard and soft thresholding scheme with an inverted index approximation toincrease the sparsity of SPLADE-based document and query feature vectors for faster retrieval.In addition to experimental validation with MS MARCO and BEIR datasets,we provide an analysis of the impact of hybrid thresholding with joint training onindex approximation errors and training update effectiveness.

Related work.Other than SPLADE, sparse retrieval studies includeSNRM  (Zamani et al., 2018),DeepCT (Dai and Callan, 2020),DeepImpact (Mallia et al., 2021), and uniCOIL (Lin and Ma, 2021; Gao et al., 2021).The sparsity of a neural network is studied in the deep learning community.Soft thresholding in (Kusupati et al., 2020)adopts a learnable threshold with function S​(x,t)=R​e​L​U​(x−t)𝑆𝑥𝑡𝑅𝑒𝐿𝑈𝑥𝑡S(x,t)=ReLU(x-t) to make parameter x𝑥x zero under threshold t𝑡t.A hard thresholding function H​(x,t)=x𝐻𝑥𝑡𝑥H(x,t)=x when x≥t𝑥𝑡x\geq t otherwise 0.Approximate hard thresholding (Park and Lee, 2020) uses a Gauss error function to approximate H​(x,t)𝐻𝑥𝑡H(x,t) with smoothgradients.Dynamic sparse training (LIU et al., 2020) finds a dynamic threshold with marked layers.These works including the recent ones (Frantar and Alistarh, 2022)are targeted for sparsification of parameter edges in a deep neural network.In our context, a token weight wjsubscript𝑤𝑗w_{j} is an output node in a network.The sparsification of output nodesis addressed in activation map compression (Georgiadis, 2019)using ReLU as soft thresholding together with L1 regularization.The work of  (Kurtz et al., 2020) further boostssparsity with the Hoyer regularization and a variant of ReLU.The above techniques have not been investigated in the context of sparse retrieval, andthe impact of thresholding on relevance and query processing time with inverted indices,requires new design considerations and model structuring for document retrieval, even the previous work can be leveraged.

Design considerations.To zero outa token weight below a learnable threshold,there are two options: soft thresholding (Kusupati et al., 2020),and approximate hard thresholding (Park and Lee, 2020).For query token weights, we find that soft thresholding does not affect relevance significantly.For document token weights, our study finds thatcompared to soft thresholding,hard thresholdingcan retain relevance bettersince it does not change token weights when exceeding a threshold.Since the subgradient for hard thresholding with respect to a threshold is always 0,an approximation needs to be carried outfor training.For search index generation, an inverted index produced with the same approximate hard thresholding as trainingkeeps many unnecessary non-zero document token weights, slowing down retrieval significantly.Thus we directly apply hard thresholding with a threshold learned from training,as shown in Figure 1.There is a gap between trained document token weights and actual weights used in our inverted index generationand online inference, and we intend to minimize this gap (called an index approximation error).

Thus our design takes a hybrid approach that applies soft thresholding to query token weights during training and inferenceand applies approximate hard thresholding to document token weights during training while using hard thresholding for documentsduring indexgeneration.For approximate hard thresholding,we propose to use a logistic sigmoid-based function instead of a Gauss error function (Park and Lee, 2020).This sigmoid thresholding simplifies our analysisof the impact of its hyperparameter choice toindex approximation errors, and to training stability.

Our evaluation shows thatlearnable hybrid thresholding with index approximation can effectively increasethe sparsity of inverted indices with 2-3x faster retrieval and competitive or slightly degraded relevance (0.28% - 0.6% MRR@10 drop).Its trainability allows relevance and sparsity guided threshold learning andit can outperform index pruning without such an optimization.Our scheme retains a non-uniform number of non-zero token weights per vector based on a trainableweight and threshold differencefor flexibility in relevance optimization.Our analysis shows that hyperparameter K𝐾K in sigmoid thresholdingneeds to be chosenjudiciously for a small index approximation error without hurting training stability.

If a small relevance tradeoff is allowed, more retrieval time reduction is possiblewhen applying other related orthogonal efficiency optimization techniques (Lassance and Clinchant, 2022; Mallia et al., 2022; Qiao et al., 2022, 2023; Lin and Trotman, 2015; Mackenzie et al., 2021).Applying hybrid thresholding HT3 to a checkpoint of a recent efficiency-driven SPLADEmodel (Lassance and Clinchant, 2022) with 0.3799 MRR@10on the MS MARCO passage Dev set, decreasesthe response time from 36.6ms to 21.7ms (1.7x faster)when k𝑘k=1000 while having 0.3868 MRR@10.This latency can be further reduced to 14.2ms with the same MRR@10 number (0.3868) when 2GTI (Qiao et al., 2023) is applied to the above index.

A future study is to investigate the use of the proposed hybrid thresholding schemefor other learned sparse models (Mallia et al., 2021; Lin and Ma, 2021; Gao et al., 2021).
