Column Subset Selection (k𝑘k-CSS) is a widely studied approach for low-rank approximation and feature selection.In k𝑘k-CSS, on an input data matrix A∈ℝd×n𝐴superscriptℝ𝑑𝑛A\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times n}, we seek a small subset AIsubscript𝐴𝐼A_{I} of k𝑘k columns from A𝐴A such that minV⁡‖AI​V−A‖subscript𝑉normsubscript𝐴𝐼𝑉𝐴\min_{V}\|A_{I}V-A\| is minimized for some norm ∥⋅∥\|\cdot\|.In contrast to general low-rank approximation, where one finds U∈ℝd×k𝑈superscriptℝ𝑑𝑘U\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times k} and V∈ℝk×n𝑉superscriptℝ𝑘𝑛V\in\mathbb{R}^{k\times n} such that ‖U​V−A‖norm𝑈𝑉𝐴\|UV-A\| is minimized [1, 2],k𝑘k-CSS outputs an actual subset of the columns of A𝐴A as the left factor U𝑈U.The main advantage of k𝑘k-CSS over general low-rank approximation is that the resulting factorization is more interpretable.For instance, the subset AIsubscript𝐴𝐼A_{I} can represent salient features of A𝐴A, while in general low-rank approximation, the left factor U𝑈U may not be easy to relate to the original dataset. In addition, the subset AIsubscript𝐴𝐼A_{I} preserves sparsity of the original matrix A𝐴A.

k𝑘k-CSS has been extensively studied in the Frobenius norm [3, 4, 5, 6] and also the operator norm [7, 8].A number of recent works  [9, 10, 11, 12, 13] studied this problem in the ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} norm for 1≤p<21𝑝21\leq p<2, due to its robustness properties.The ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1} norm, especially, is less sensitive to outliers, and better at handling missing data and non-Gaussian noise, than the Frobenius norm [9].Using the ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1} norm loss has been shown to lead to improved performance in many real-world applications of low-rank approximation, such as structure-from-motion [14] and image denoising [15].

In this work, we give algorithms for k𝑘k-CSS in the ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} norm, or k𝑘k-CSSp, in the streaming and distributed settings for 1≤p<21𝑝21\leq p<2.The streaming algorithm can be used on small devices with memory constraints, when the elements of a large dataset are arriving one at a time and storing the entire stream is not possible.The distributed algorithm is useful when a large dataset is partitioned across multiple devices.Each device only sees a subset of the entire dataset, and it is expensive to communicate or transmit data across devices.

We study the column-update streaming model (See Definition 2.1) where the dataset A𝐴A is a d×n𝑑𝑛d\times n matrix with n≫dmuch-greater-than𝑛𝑑n\gg d, and the columns of A𝐴A arrive one by one.In this setting, our goal is to maintain a good subset of columns of A𝐴A, while using space that can be linear in d𝑑d and k𝑘k, but sublinear in n𝑛n.This model is relevant in settings where memory is limited, and the algorithm can only make one pass over the input data, e.g. [16].

k𝑘k-CSS and low-rank approximation with the Frobenius norm loss have been extensively studied in the column-update streaming model (or equivalently the row-update streaming model), e.g. [17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 22].However, k𝑘k-CSS in the streaming model has not been studied in the more robust ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} norm, and it is not clear how to adapt existing offline k𝑘k-CSSp or ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} low-rank approximation algorithms into streaming k𝑘k-CSSp algorithms.In fact, the only work which studies ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} low-rank approximation in the column-update streaming model is [9].They obtain a poly​(k,log⁡d)poly𝑘𝑑\text{poly}(k,\log d)-approximate algorithm with O~​(d​k)~𝑂𝑑𝑘\widetilde{O}(dk) space, and using the techniques of [23] this approximation factor can be further improved.However, it is not clear how to turn this streaming algorithm of [9] into a streaming k𝑘k-CSSp algorithm.

In addition, it is not clear how to adapt many known algorithms for ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} low-rank approximation and column subset selection into one-pass streaming algorithms for k𝑘k-CSSp.For instance, one family of offline algorithms for k𝑘k-CSSp, studied by [10, 11, 24, 13], requires O​(log⁡n)𝑂𝑛O(\log n) iterations.In each of these iterations, the algorithm selects O~​(k)~𝑂𝑘\widetilde{O}(k) columns of A𝐴A and uses their span to approximate a constant fraction of the remaining columns, which are then discarded.Since these rounds are adaptive, this algorithm would require O​(log⁡n)𝑂𝑛O(\log n) passes over the columns of A𝐴A if implemented as a streaming algorithm, making it unsuitable.

In this work, we give the first non-trivial single-pass algorithm for k𝑘k-CSSp in the column-update streaming model (Algorithm 6).The space complexity of our algorithm, O~​(k​d)~𝑂𝑘𝑑\widetilde{O}(kd), is nearly optimal up to a logarithmic factor, since for k𝑘k columns, each having d𝑑d entries, Ω​(d​k)Ω𝑑𝑘\Omega(dk) words of space are needed. Our first key insight is that we need to maintain a small subset of columns with size independent of n𝑛n that globally approximates all columns of A𝐴A well throughout the stream under the desired norm. A good data summarization technique for this is a strong coreset, which can be a subsampled and reweighted subset of columns that preserves the cost of projecting onto all subspaces (i.e., the span of the columns we ultimately choose in our subset). However, strong coresets are not known to exist in the ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} norm for 1≤p<21𝑝21\leq p<2. Thus, we reduce to low rank approximation in the ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2} norm, which is the sum of the p𝑝p-th powers of the Euclidean norms of all columns (see Definition 2.3).Strong coresets in the ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2} norm have been extensively studied and developed, see, e.g., [26].However, to the best of our knowledge, ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2} strong coresets have not been used for developing ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} low-rank approximation or k𝑘k-CSS algorithms prior to our work.

The next question to address is how to construct and maintain strong coresets of A𝐴A during the stream. First we observe that strong coresets are mergeable. If two coresets C1subscript𝐶1C_{1}, C2subscript𝐶2C_{2} provide a (1±ϵ)plus-or-minus1italic-ϵ(1\pm\epsilon)-approximation to the ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2}-norm cost of projecting two sets of columns AMsubscript𝐴𝑀A_{M} and ANsubscript𝐴𝑁A_{N} respectively, to any subspace, then the coreset of C1∪C2subscript𝐶1subscript𝐶2C_{1}\cup C_{2} gives a (1±ϵ)2superscriptplus-or-minus1italic-ϵ2(1\pm\epsilon)^{2}-approximation to the cost of projecting AM∪ANsubscript𝐴𝑀subscript𝐴𝑁A_{M}\cup A_{N} onto any subspace. Thus, we can construct strong coresets for batches of input columns from A𝐴A and merge these coresets to save space while processing the stream. In order to reduce the number of merges, and hence the approximation error, we apply the Merge-and-Reduce framework (see, e.g., [27]). Our algorithm greedily merges the strong coresets in a binary tree fashion, where the leaves correspond to batches of the input columns and the root is the single strong coreset remaining at the end. This further enables us to maintain only O​(log⁡n)𝑂𝑛O(\log n) coresets of size O~​(k)~𝑂𝑘\widetilde{O}(k) throughout the stream, and hence achieve a space complexity of O~​(k​d)~𝑂𝑘𝑑\widetilde{O}(kd) words.

One problem with reducing to the ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2} norm is that this leads to an approximation factor of d1/p−1/2superscript𝑑1𝑝12d^{1/p-1/2}. Our second key insight is to apply a dimensionality reduction technique in the ℓpsubscriptℓ𝑝\ell_{p} norm, which reduces the row dimension from d𝑑d to k​poly​(log⁡n​d)𝑘poly𝑛𝑑k\text{poly}(\log nd) via data-independent (i.e., oblivious) sketching matrices of i.i.d. p𝑝p-stable random variables (see Definition 2.4), which only increases the approximation error by a factor of O​(log⁡n​d)𝑂𝑛𝑑O(\log nd). The overall approximation error is thus reduced to O​(k1/p−1/2​poly​(log⁡n​d))𝑂superscript𝑘1𝑝12poly𝑛𝑑O(k^{1/p-1/2}\text{poly}(\log nd)).

As a result, our algorithm constructs coresets for the sketched columns instead of the original columns. However, since we do not know which subset of columns will be selected a priori, we need approximation guarantees of dimensionality reduction via oblivious skecthing matrices for all possible subsets of columns.We combine a net argument with a union bound over all possible subspaces spanned by column subsets of A𝐴A of size O~​(k)~𝑂𝑘\widetilde{O}(k) (see Lemma 2). Previous arguments involving sketching for low-rank approximation algorithms, such as those by [9, 12, 13], only consider a single subspace at a time.

At the end of the stream we will have a single coreset of size k⋅poly​(log⁡n​d)⋅𝑘poly𝑛𝑑k\cdot\text{poly}(\log nd). Our streaming algorithm and techniques can be extended to an efficient one-round distributed protocol for k𝑘k-CSSp (1≤p<21𝑝21\leq p<2).We consider the column partition model in the distributed setting (see Definition 2.2), where s𝑠s servers communicate to a central coordinator via 2-way channels. This model can simulate arbitrary point-to-point communication by having the coordinator forward a message from one server to another; this increases the total communication by a factor of 222 and an additive log⁡s𝑠\log s bits per message to identify the destination server.

Distributed low-rank approximation arises naturally when a dataset is too large to store on one machine, takes a prohibitively long time for a single machine to compute a rank-k𝑘k approximation, or is collected simultaneously on multiple machines.The column partition model arises naturally in many real world scenarios such as federated learning [28, 21, 29]. Despite the flurry of recent work on k𝑘k-CSSp, this problem remains largely unexplored in the distributed setting. This should be contrasted to Frobenius norm column subset selection and low-rank approximation, for which a number of results in the distributed model are known, see, e.g., [21, 30, 31, 22].

In this work, we give the first one-pass streaming algorithm for k𝑘k-CSSp (1≤p<21𝑝21\leq p<2) in the column-update model and the first one-round distributed protocol in the column-partition model. Both of our algorithms achieve O~​(k1/p−1/2)~𝑂superscript𝑘1𝑝12\widetilde{O}(k^{1/p-1/2})-approximation to the optimal column subset. The streaming algorithm uses nearly optimal space complexity of O~​(k​d)~𝑂𝑘𝑑\widetilde{O}(kd), and the distributed protocol uses nearly optimal O~​(s​d​k)~𝑂𝑠𝑑𝑘\widetilde{O}(sdk) communication cost. We introduce novel analysis techniques for k𝑘k-CSS. To achieve a good approximation factor, we use dense p𝑝p-stable sketching and work with the ℓp,2subscriptℓ𝑝2\ell_{p,2} norm, which enables us to use an efficient construction of strong coresets and an O​(1)𝑂1O(1)-approximation bi-criteria k𝑘k-CSSp,2 algorithm as a subroutine of our algorithms. We further propose a greedy alternative for k𝑘k-CSSp,2 and show the first additive error upper bound. Our experimental results confirm that our algorithms give stable low ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1} error in both distributed and streaming settings. We further demonstrate the robustness of the ℓ1subscriptℓ1\ell_{1} norm loss for k𝑘k-CSS.
