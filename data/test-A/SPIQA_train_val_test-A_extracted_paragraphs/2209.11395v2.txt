The study of the universal approximation property (UAP) of neural networks is fundamental for deep learning and has a long history. Early studies, such as Cybenkot (1989); Hornik etВ al. Further research, such as Telgarsky (2016), indicated that increasing the depth can improve the expression power of neural networks. Let us summarize the main results and implications of this paper. After giving the universal lower bound of the minimum width for the UAP, we proved that the bound is optimal by constructing neural networks with some activation functions.

For the Lpsuperscriptрқҗҝрқ‘қL^{p}-UAP, our construction to achieve the critical width was based on the approximation power of neural ODEs, which bridges the feed-forward networks to the flow maps corresponding to the ODEs. This allowed us to understand the UAP of the FNN through topology theory. Moreover, we obtained not only the lower bound but also the upper bound.

For the Cрқҗ¶C-UAP, our construction was based on the encoder-memorizer-decoder approach in Park etВ al. (2021), where the activation sets contain a discontinuous function вҢҠxвҢӢрқ‘Ҙ\lfloor x\rfloor. It is still an open question whether we can achieve the critical width by continuous activation functions. Johnson (2019) proved that continuous and monotone activation functions need at least width dx+1subscriptрқ‘‘рқ‘Ҙ1d_{x}+1. This implies that nonmonotone activation functions are needed. By using the UOE activation, we calculated the critical width for the case of dx=1subscriptрқ‘‘рқ‘Ҙ1d_{x}=1. It would be of interest to study the case of dxвүҘ2subscriptрқ‘‘рқ‘Ҙ2d_{x}\geq 2 in future research.

We remark that our UAP is for functions on a compact domain.Examining the critical width of the UAP for functions on unbounded domains is desirable for future research.
