Matrix multiplication refers to computing the product X​YT𝑋superscript𝑌𝑇XY^{T} of two matrices X∈ℝmx×n𝑋superscriptℝsubscript𝑚𝑥𝑛X\in\mathbb{R}^{m_{x}\times n} and Y∈ℝmy×n𝑌superscriptℝsubscript𝑚𝑦𝑛Y\in\mathbb{R}^{m_{y}\times n}, which is a fundamental task in many machine learning applications such as regression (Naseem et al., 2010; Cohen et al., 2015), online learning (Hazan et al., 2007; Chu et al., 2011), information retrieval (Eriksson-Bique et al., 2011) and canonical correlation analysis (Hotelling, 1936; Chen et al., 2015). Recently, the scales of data and models in these applications have increased dramatically, which results in very large data matrices. As a result, it requires unacceptable time and space to directly compute X​YT𝑋superscript𝑌𝑇XY^{T} in the main memory. To reduce both time and space complexities, approximate matrix multiplication (AMM) with limited space, which can efficiently compute a good approximation of the matrix product, has been a substitute and received ever-increasing attention (Ye et al., 2016; Kuzborskij et al., 2019).
