Finding cohesive groups (or communities) has received a lot of attention from various areas such as social network analysis and web mining, and is also a fundamental problem in graph algorithms.The community can be modeled in many ways. For example, the notion of clique is the strictest and arguably the most studied community model. A clique is a subgraph in which vertices are pairwise connected, i.e., a complete subgraph. A large body of literature dedicated to related problems has emerged, e.g., enumerating cliques in graphs (Xiao andNagamochi, 2017), sparse graphs (Changet al., 2013; Eppstein andStrash, 2011), uncertain graphs (Mukherjeeet al., 2016), limited main memory (Chenget al., 2012), and optimizing the running time as a function of the output (Conteet al., 2016).The clique model has also been applied in many domains such as data mining (Chenget al., 2012), bio-informatics (Butenko andWilhelm, 2006) and ad-hoc wireless network (Chenet al., 2004).

In real-world graphs, due to various reasons such as the existence of data noise, communities rarely appear in the form of cliques (Balasundaramet al., 2011; Conte et al., 2017, 2018).Therefore, other forms of relaxed cliques are proposed as relaxations of the notion of clique.For example, the k𝑘k-core (Chenget al., 2011) relaxes the vertex degree, k𝑘k-club (Pajouhet al., 2016) relaxes pairwise distance of vertices and k𝑘k-clique densest subgraph (Tsourakakis, 2015) relaxes density of induced subgraph.In this paper, we continue on this line of research by focusing on k𝑘k-plex, – the notion that has been receiving increasing attention and popularity in recent years (Zhouet al., 2020; Conte et al., 2018; Xiaoet al., 2017; Conte et al., 2017).

A k𝑘k-plex is a relaxed clique model first proposed in (Seidman andFoster, 1978).A k𝑘k-plex is a graph in which each vertex’s degree is at least n−k𝑛𝑘n-k, where n𝑛n is the number of vertices in the graph.In other words, a k𝑘k-plex allows every vertex missing at most k𝑘k links to other vertices (including itself) compared to the clique.Note that a 111-plex is just a clique.A k𝑘k-plex in a graph is called maximal if and only if it is not a subgraph of any larger k𝑘k-plex.

In this paper, we will study the problem of listing all maximal k𝑘k-plexes from a given graph. It would seem that the listing of maximal k𝑘k-plexes will be also useful in applications where maximal clique listing is applied.Additionally, the k𝑘k-plex listing has other potential applications like link prediction.

From the theoretical point of view, listing all maximal k𝑘k-plexes is hard.In fact, for any given k𝑘k, this problem is NP-hard (Balasundaramet al., 2011) and it is known that the number of maximal k𝑘k-plexes is exponential in the worst-case (Moon and Moser, 1965).Therefore, a large number of existing studies focus on the design of practically efficient methods.The majority of these algorithms have been derived and motivated by the Bron-Kerbosch algorithm (Bron and Kerbosch, 1973), though it was originally designed to only list maximal cliques.Wu and Pei ((2007)) adapted the Bron-Kerbosch algorithm to list maximal k𝑘k-plexes with a few new rules to prune unnecessary searches (Wu and Pei, 2007).Wanget al. ((2017)) integrated more heuristic pruning rules and applied multi-thread parallelization technique (Wanget al., 2017).Zhouet al. ((2020)) devised a novel branch heuristic with a worst-case time complexity proof.With their branch heuristic, the running time of Bron-Kerbosch algorithm is improved from O∗​(2n)superscript𝑂superscript2𝑛O^{*}(2^{n}) to O∗​(γkn)superscript𝑂superscriptsubscript𝛾𝑘𝑛O^{*}(\gamma_{k}^{n}) where γksubscript𝛾𝑘\gamma_{k} is related to k𝑘k but strictly smaller than 222 111The notation O∗superscript𝑂O^{*} omits the polynomial factors..Aside from the Bron-Kerbosch variants, there is another type of algorithms which have bounded delay between the output of two consecutive solutions.Berlowitzet al. ((2020)) initialized such kind of study by providing a polynomial-time delay algorithm for the problem (Berlowitzet al., 2015; Cohenet al., 2008).

We also study the problem of listing large maximal k𝑘k-plexes, i.e., listing maximal k𝑘k-plexes which have at least l𝑙l vertices, l𝑙l being a large number, say at least 2​k−12𝑘12k-1.The problem was originally proposed to amend two issues that arise in modeling the communities by maximal k𝑘k-plexes (Conte et al., 2017; Conteet al., 2021; Conte et al., 2018; Zhouet al., 2020).First, it is observed that there are enormous maximal k𝑘k-plexes in real-world graphs, and empirically most of them are small or even unconnected.However, in community detection application, communities should be large and densely connected subgraphs.Second, existing maximal k𝑘k-plex listing algorithms can only handle graphs with hundreds to thousands of vertices in days.But large sparse graphs are ubiquitous these days, e.g., the webbase-2001 web-graph has more than a hundred million vertices and more than a billion edges (Boldi and Vigna, 2004)).

Fortunately, by requiring that the output k𝑘k-plexes must be at least larger than a threshold l𝑙l (l≥2​k−1𝑙2𝑘1l\geq 2k-1), the two issues can be alleviated.Due to the structural property of k𝑘k-plexes (Property 3 in (Xiaoet al., 2017)), a k𝑘k-plex with at least 2​k−12𝑘12k-1 vertices is densely connected, i.e., the shortest length of paths between every two vertices is not larger than 2. Therefore, the first issue does not exist.For the second issue, with a lower bound requirement on the size of output k𝑘k-plexes, the performance of listing algorithm can be also accelerated with many powerful strategies (Conte et al., 2017; Conteet al., 2021; Conte et al., 2018). For instance, in (Conte et al., 2017; Conteet al., 2021), Conte et al. ((2017)) took advantage of the size constraint to remove a large portion of unfruitful vertices from the input graph, which made Berlowitzet al.’s listing algorithm possible to run on graphs of millions of vertices.Conte et al. ((2018)) further used decomposition and parallel techniques, leading to a listing algorithm capable of running on some web-scale graphs, e.g., the it-2004 graph.Zhouet al. ((2020)) used the same decomposition framework as in (Conte et al., 2018) so that their Bron-Kerbosch pivot heuristic can accommodate large real-world graphs.

Motivated by the aforementioned studies, we develop the most efficient algorithm for listing both maximal k𝑘k-plexes and large maximal k𝑘k-plexes from sparse real-world graphs.

1. We propose ListPlex, an algorithm that lists all maximal k𝑘k-plexes with provably worst-case running time.The general idea of ListPlex is a marriage of new decomposition scheme and an efficient Bron-Kerboch search.Our analysis discloses that for each constant k𝑘k, ListPlex has a worst-case time bound O∗​(γkD)superscript𝑂superscriptsubscript𝛾𝑘𝐷O^{*}(\gamma_{k}^{D}) where D𝐷D is the degeneracy number of the input graph and γksubscript𝛾𝑘\gamma_{k} is related to k𝑘k but strictly smaller than 2.As far as we know, it is the first algorithm that reduces the exponent of running time from n𝑛n to D𝐷D.Due to the power-law distribution of most real-world graphs, D≪nmuch-less-than𝐷𝑛D\ll n in most cases, e.g., the webbase-2001 has more than a thousand million vertices but its degeneracy number is only 1506. To some extent, this bound provides theoretical evidence for the good performance of our algorithm.

2. We optimize the practical performance of ListPlex for listing large maximal k𝑘k-plexes of size at least 2​k−12𝑘12k-1.It is known that listing large maximal k𝑘k-plexes is of more real-world importance than purely listing all maximal k𝑘k-plexes. Thus, we study efficient implementation techniques from multiple perspectives.From algorithmic perspective, we suggest strong prune rules to reduce the search space of our algorithm. From the computational system perspective, we propose new data structures to reduce cache misses and increase parallelism.All optimization techniques bring evident speedup for processing large sparse real-world graphs.

Our experiments show that ListPlex outperforms the state-of-the-art approaches in terms of both problems.For example, our parallel algorithm can list all large maximal 222-plexes (with l=800𝑙800l=800) from the huge webbase-2001 graph with over one billion edges in 1 minutes. This is almost an order of magnitude speedup compared to the best-known parallel approach.

ListPlex also owns appealing parallel features. We introduce a shared-memory parallel version of ListPlex in this subsection.

It is observed that searches of maximal visubscript𝑣𝑖v_{i}-leaded k𝑘k-plexes are independent for each visubscript𝑣𝑖v_{i}.Thus, for each vertex visubscript𝑣𝑖v_{i}, we create a task, say Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i}, to process the search of all maximal visubscript𝑣𝑖v_{i}-leaded k𝑘k-plexes.Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} owns its private seed graph Gisubscript𝐺𝑖G_{i} and bipartite graph Bisubscript𝐵𝑖B_{i}. Tasks T1,…,Tnsubscript𝑇1…subscript𝑇𝑛T_{1},...,T_{n} can be executed in parallel.

However, it could happen that most tasks stop but a few heavy tasks are still running.Specifically, when the number of running tasks is less than the number of available cores, computational resources are wasted.In such case, we split the branches of a running task Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} into new subtasks for the idle cores.Assume that Tnsubscript𝑇𝑛T_{n} has been dispatched for execution but Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} (i<n𝑖𝑛i<n) is still staying in the BKPivot(Gi,k,P,C,X)subscript𝐺𝑖𝑘𝑃𝐶𝑋(G_{i},k,P,C,X) procedure.Then, when Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} detects some idle cores, it spawns recursive calls to BKPivot(Gi,k,P,C,X)subscript𝐺𝑖𝑘𝑃𝐶𝑋(G_{i},k,P,C,X) as subtasks of Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} and dispatches them to idle cores.A subtask of Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i} owns its sets P𝑃P, C𝐶C and X𝑋X but shares Gisubscript𝐺𝑖G_{i} and Bisubscript𝐵𝑖B_{i} with Tisubscript𝑇𝑖T_{i}.Indeed, the schedule follows the work-stealing scheduling algorithm which accommodates well with the Bron-Kerbosch algorithm (Blanuša et al., 2020).

In parallel computing, the granularity of subtasks substantially affects the performance.Empirically, simple small tasks should not be spawned due to the overhead.In our implementation, we measure the complexity of a subtask, i.e., the time of executing BKPivot(Gi,k,P,C,X)subscript𝐺𝑖𝑘𝑃𝐶𝑋(G_{i},k,P,C,X), by the size of C𝐶C.Particularly, if |C|>10𝐶10|C|>10 and there are some idle cores, we spawn new subtasks and assign them to available cores.

We studied the problems of listing maximal k𝑘k-plexes and maximal k𝑘k-plexes of prescribed size.We proposed ListPlex, a fast and scalable algorithm that efficiently solves the two problems in real-world graphs.Especially, ListPlex combines a new decomposition scheme with the branching algorithm, achieving a better theoretical complexity.When maximal k𝑘k-plexes of size at least l𝑙l (l≥2​k−1𝑙2𝑘1l\geq 2k-1) are asked, ListPLex can be also used for listing these large maximal k𝑘k-plexes.For practical considerations, we designed some additional prune rules for listing large maximal k𝑘k-plexes.These prune rules work very well in the context of large real-world graphs.Furthermore, we designed a new local bipartite graph to improve the cache performance of the algorithm, and parallel scheduling strategies to increase parallelism.Extensive empirical evaluations show the superiority of ListPlex over the state-of-the-art approaches for both problems.
