Deception is a core challenge for building safe and cooperative AI Park etВ al. [2023], Hubinger etВ al. [2019], Dafoe etВ al. [2020]. AI tools can be used to deceive [Nafees etВ al., 2020, Gorwa and Guilbeault, 2020, Marra etВ al., 2019], and agent-based systems might learn to do so to optimize their objectives Lewis etВ al. [2017], Hubinger etВ al. [2019], Floreano etВ al. [2007]. As increasingly capable AI agents become deployed in multi-agent settings,comprising humans and other AI agents,deception may be learned as an effective strategy for achieving a wide range of goals [Roff, 2021, Hubinger etВ al., 2019].Furthermore, as language models (LMs) become ubiquitous [Vaswani etВ al., 2017, Hoffmann etВ al., 2022, Smith etВ al., 2022, Rae etВ al., 2021, Chowdhery etВ al., 2022], we must decide how to measure and implement desired standards for honesty in AI systems Kenton etВ al. [2021], Evans etВ al. [2021], LinВ et al. [2022], especially as regulation of deceptive AI systems becomes legislated [Atleson, 2023, Uuk, 2022, Anderljung etВ al., 2023, Shevlane etВ al., 2023].

There is no overarching theory of deception for AI agents. There are several definitions in the literature on game theory Baston and Bostock [1988], Davis [2016], Fristedt [1997] and symbolic AI [Sarkadi etВ al., , Sarkadi etВ al., 2019, Sakama, 2020, Bonnet etВ al., 2020], but these frameworks are insufficient to address deception by learning agents in the general case [Herzig etВ al., 2017, Guerra-HernГЎndezВ etal., 2004, PhungВ et al., 2005, BaltagВ et al., 2008].

We formalize a philosophical definition of deception [Carson, 2010, Mahon, 2016], wherebyan agent Sрқ‘ҶS deceives another agent Tрқ‘ҮT if Sрқ‘ҶS intentionally causes Tрқ‘ҮT to believe П•italic-П•\phi, where П•italic-П•\phi is false and Sрқ‘ҶS does not believe that П•italic-П•\phi is true.This requires notions of intent and belief and we present functional definitions of these concepts thatdepend on the behaviour of the agents.Regarding intention, we build on the definition of Halpern and Kleiman-Weiner [2018] (from now, H&KW). Intent relates to the reasons for acting and is connected to instrumental goals [Omohundro, 2008].As for belief, we present a novel definition which operationalizes belief as acceptance, where, essentially, an agent accepts a proposition if they act as though they are certain it is true [Schwitzgebel, 2021, Cohen, 1995]. Our definitions have a number of advantages: 1) Functional definitions provide observable criteria by which to infer agent intent and belief from behaviour, without making the contentious ascription of theory of mind to AI systems [Kenton etВ al., 2021, Shanahan, 2022], or requiring a mechanistic understanding of a systems internals Olah [2022]; 2) Our definition provides a natural way to distinguish between belief and ignorance (and thereby between deception and concealing), which is a challenge for Bayesian epistemology [Norton, 2008, Levi, 1982, BenГ©treau-Dupin, 2015], and game theory [DengВ et al., 2019, Seidenfeld, 1983]; 3) Agents that intentionally deceive in order to achieve their goals seem less safe a priori than those which do so merely as a side-effect. In sectionВ 5, we also reflect on the limitations of our approach.

We utilize the setting of structural causal games (SCGs), which offer a representation of causality in games and are used to model agent incentives [Hammond etВ al., 2023, Everitt etВ al., 2021a]. In contrast to past frameworks for deception, SCGs can model stochastic games and MDPs, and can capture both game theory and learning systems [Everitt etВ al., 2021b].In addition, SCGs enable us to reason about the path-specific effects of an agentвҖҷs decisions. Hence, our main theoretical result is to show graphical criteria, i.e., necessary graphical patterns in the SCG, for intention and deception. These can be used to train agents that do not optimise over selected paths (containing the decisions of other agents) and are therefore not deceptive [FarquharВ et al., 2022].

Finally, we empirically ground the theory. First, we show how our graphical criteria can be used to train a non-deceptive reinforcement learning (RL) agent in a toy game from the signalling literature [Cho and Kreps, 1987]. Then, we demonstrate how to apply our theory to LMs by either prompting or fine-tuning LMs towards goals which incentivize instrumental deception. We show that LMs fine-tuned to be evaluated as truthful are in fact deceptive, and we mitigate this with the path-specific objectives framework.

After covering the necessary background (sectionВ 2), we contribute: First, novel formal definitions of belief and deception, and an extension of a definition of intention (sectionВ 3). Examples and results illustrate that our formalizations capture the philosophical concepts.Second, graphical criteria for intention and deception, with soundness and completeness results (fig.В 4).Third, experimental results, which show how the graphical criteria can be used to mitigate deception in RL agents and LMs (sectionВ 4).Finally, we discuss the limitations of our approach, and conclude (sectionВ 5). Below we discuss related work on belief, intention, and deception.

Belief.The standard philosophical account is that belief is a propositional attitude: a mental state expressing some attitude towards the truth of a proposition [Schwitzgebel, 2021]. By utilizing a functional notion of belief which depends on agent behaviour, we avoid the need to represent the mental-states of agents Kenton etВ al. [2021]. Belief-Desire-Intention (BDI) frameworks and epistemic logics provide natural languages to discuss belief and agent theory of mind (indeed, much of the literature on deceptive AI is grounded in these frameworks [Panisson etВ al., 2018, Sarkadi etВ al., 2019, Bonnet etВ al., 2020, Sakama, 2020, van Ditmarsch etВ al., 2020]). Two major limitations to these approaches are 1) a proper integration with game theory [DengВ et al., 2019, Seidenfeld, 1983]; and 2) incorporating statistical learning and belief-revision [Herzig etВ al., 2017, Guerra-HernГЎndezВ etal., 2004, PhungВ et al., 2005, BaltagВ et al., 2008]. In contrast, SCGs capture both game theory and learning systems [Hammond etВ al., 2021, Everitt etВ al., 2021b]. 

Intention.There is no universally accepted philosophical theory of intention [Setiya, 2022, Anscombe, 2000], and ascribing intent to artificial agents may be contentious [Shanahan, 2022]. However, the question of intent is difficult to avoid when characterizing deception [Mahon, 2016]. We build on H&KWвҖҷs definition of intent in causal models. This ties intent to the reasons for action and instrumental goals [Omohundro, 2008, Everitt etВ al., 2021a]. In short, agents that (learn to) deceive because it is instrumentally useful in achieving utility seem less safe a priori than those which do so merely as a side-effect.In contrast, other work considers side-effects to be intentional [Ashton, 2021] or equates intent with вҖңknowingly seeing to it that" [Bonnet etВ al., 2020, Sakama, 2020] or takes intent as primitive (as in BDI frameworks) [Sarkadi etВ al., 2019, Panisson etВ al., 2018]. Cohen and Levesque [1990] present seminal work on computational intention. Kleiman-Weiner etВ al. [2015] model intent in influence diagrams. Ashton [2022] surveys algorithmic intent.

Deception.We formalize a philosophical definition of deception [Mahon, 2016, Carson, 2010, VanВ Fraassen, 1988], whereby an agent Sрқ‘ҶS deceives another agent Tрқ‘ҮT if Sрқ‘ҶS intentionally causes Tрқ‘ҮT to believe П•italic-П•\phi, where П•italic-П•\phi is false and Sрқ‘ҶS does not believe that П•italic-П•\phi is true.Under our definition, deception only occurs if a false belief in the target is successfully achieved Ryle [2009]. We reject cases of negative deception, in which a target is made ignorant by loss of a true belief Mahon [2016].In contrast to lying, deception does not require a linguistic statement and may be achieved through any form of action [Mahon, 2016], including making true statements [Rogers etВ al., 2017], or deception by omission [Chisholm and Feehan, 1977]. Some work on deceptive AI assumes a linguistic framework [Sakama, 2020, Sarkadi etВ al., 2019]. Existing models in the game theory literature present particular forms of signalling or deception games Davis [2016], Baston and Bostock [1988], Fristedt [1997], Cho and Kreps [1987], KovachВ et al. [2015]. In contrast, our definition is applicable to any SCG. AI systems may be vulnerable to deception; adversarial attacks Madry etВ al. [2017], data-poisoning SteinhardtВ et al. [2017], attacks on gradients [Blanchard etВ al., 2017], reward function tampering Everitt etВ al. [2021b], and manipulating human feedback Carroll etВ al. [2023], WardВ et al. [2022] are ways of deceiving AI systems. Further work researches mechanisms for detecting and defending against deception Peskov etВ al. [2020], Siegelmann [2019], Conroy etВ al. [2015], Galindo etВ al. [2022], Nafees etВ al. [2020], Vargas and Pardo [2020]. On the other hand, AI tools can be used to deceive other software agents Gorwa and Guilbeault [2020], or humans (cf.В the use of generative models to produce fake media [Marra etВ al., 2019, Nafees etВ al., 2020]). Furthermore, AI agents might learn deceptive strategies in pursuit of their goals Hubinger etВ al. [2019], Roff [2021]. В Lewis etВ al.вҖҷsnegotiation agent learnt to deceive from self-play Lewis etВ al. [2017],Floreano etВ al.вҖҷs robots evolved deceptive communication strategies Floreano etВ al. [2007], Bakhtin etВ al.вҖҷs agent exhibited deceptive behaviour in Diplomacy Bakhtin etВ al. [2022], Perolat etВ al.вҖҷs agent learned deception and bluffing in Stratego Perolat etВ al. [2022], and Hubinger etВ al. [2019] raise concerns about deceptive learned optimizers. Park etВ al. [2023] survey cases of AI deception.Language is a natural medium for deception Kenton etВ al. [2021], and it has been demonstrated that LMs have the capability to deceive humans to achieve goals Pacchiardi etВ al. [2023], OpenAI [2023]. How to measure and implement standards for honesty in AI systems is an open question Evans etВ al. [2021]; LinВ et al. [2022] propose the TruthfulQA benchmark used in sectionВ 4. As increasingly capable AI agents become deployed in settings alongside humans and other artificial agents, deception may be learned as an effective strategy for achieving a wide range of goals.

Graphical criteria for deception. We provide soundness and completeness results for graphical criteria of deception (fig.В 4).Results for graphical criteria are common in the literature on probabilistic graphical models [Pearl, 2009, Koller and Milch, 2003]. In the next section, we use FarquharВ et al.вҖҷs PSO framework FarquharВ et al. [2022] to train non-deceptive agents.

There are two graphical criteria for intent. First, an agent iрқ‘–i intentionally causes an outcome XвҖӢ(рққ…,рқ’Ҷ)рқ‘Ӣрққ…рқ’ҶX(\bm{\pi},\bm{e}) only if it is instrumental in achieving utility. Summary. We define deception in SCGs. Several examples and results show that our definition captures the intuitive concept. We provide graphical criteria for deception and show empirically, with experiments on RL agents and LMs, that these results can be used to train non-deceptive agents.

Limitations & future work.  Beliefs and intentions may not be uniquely identifiable from behaviour and it can be difficult to identify and assess agents in the wild (e.g., LMs).We assume that agents have correct causal models of the world, hence we are unable to account for cases where an agent intends to deceive someone because they (falsely) believe it is possible to do so. Also, our formalisation of intention relies on the agentвҖҷs utility and we are working on a purely behavioural notion of intent Ward etВ al. [Forthcomingb]. Finally, PSO is limited as a mitigation strategy as it prevents all influence on the targetвҖҷs decision; in Sahbane etВ al. [2023] we present a shielding algorithm which prevents deception whilst allowing other types of influence.

Ethical issues. Our formalization covers cases of misuse and accidents, and we acknowledge the role of developers in using AI tools to deceive Goldstein etВ al. [2023]. Finally, whilst we wish to avoid anthropormorphizing AI systems, especially when using theory-of-mind laden terms such as вҖңbelief" and вҖңintention" [Shanahan, 2022], we take seriously the possibility of catastrophic risks from advanced AI agents [Hendrycks etВ al., 2023, Cirkovic, 2008, Carlsmith, 2022].
