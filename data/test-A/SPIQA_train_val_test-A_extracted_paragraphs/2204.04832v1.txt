A great variety of modern, as well as of traditional networks, are dynamic in natureas their link availability changes over time.Information and communication networks, social networks, transportation networks,and various physical systems are only a few indicative examples of such inherently dynamic networksВ [24, 34].All these application areas share the common characteristic that the network structure,i.e.В the underlying graph topology, is subject to discrete changes over time.In this paper, embarking from the foundational work of Kempe et al.В [27],we adopt the following simple and natural model for time-varying networks,which is given by a graph with sets of time-labels associated with its edges, while the vertex set is fixed.

For an edgeВ eвҲҲEрқ‘’рқҗёe\in E in the underlying graphВ GрқҗәG of a temporal graphВ (G,О»)рқҗәрқңҶ(G,\lambda),if tвҲҲО»вҖӢ(e)рқ‘ЎрқңҶрқ‘’t\in\lambda(e) then we say that eрқ‘’e is active at timeВ tрқ‘Ўt in (G,О»)рқҗәрқңҶ(G,\lambda).That is, for every edge eвҲҲEрқ‘’рқҗёe\in E, О»вҖӢ(e)рқңҶрқ‘’\lambda(e) denotes the set of time slots at which eрқ‘’e is active.Due to their relevance and applicability in many areas,temporal graphs have been studied from various perspectives and under different namessuch as dynamicВ [21, 9],evolvingВ [6, 18, 12],time-varyingВ [19, 38, 1],and graphs over time [29].For a comprehensive overview on the existing models and results on temporal graphs from a distributed computing perspective see thesurveysВ [9, 7, 8].

Mainly motivated by the fact that, due to causality, information can betransfered in a temporal graph along sequences of edges whose time-labelsare increasing, the most traditional research on temporal graphs has focusedon temporal paths and other вҖңpath-relatedвҖқ notions,such as e.g.В temporal analogues of distance, reachability, exploration and centralityВ [28, 22, 2, 17, 30, 33, 3, 16, 41, 10].To complement this direction, several attempts have been recently made todefine meaningful вҖңnon-pathвҖқ temporal graph problems which appropriatelymodel specific applications.Motivated by the contact patterns among high-school students,Viard et al.В [39],introduced О”О”\Delta-cliques, an extension of the concept of cliques to temporal graphs (see alsoВ [23, 5]).Chen et al.В [11] presented an extension of the cluster editing problem to temporal graphs,in which all vertices interact with each other at least once every О”О”\Delta consecutive time steps within a given time interval.Furthermore, Akrida et al.В [4] introduced the notion of temporal vertex cover (also with a sliding time window),motivated by applications of covering problems in sensor networks.Further examples of meaningful вҖңnon-pathвҖқ temporal graph problems include variations of temporal graph coloringВ [32, 40, 20] in the context of planning and channel assignment in mobile sensor networks,and the temporally transitive orientations of temporal graphsВ [31].

The problems Temporal Vertex Cover (or TVC)and Sliding-Window Temporal Vertex Cover (or О”О”\Delta-TVC for time-windows of a fixed-length О”О”\Delta) have been establishedas natural extensions of the well-known Vertex Cover problem on static graphsВ [4].Given a temporal graph рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G}, the aim of TVC is to cover every edge at least once during the lifetime Tрқ‘ҮT of рқ’ўрқ’ў\mathcal{G},where an edge can be covered by one of its endpoints, and only at a time step when it is active.For any О”вҲҲв„•О”в„•\Delta\in\mathbb{N}, the aim of the more вҖңpragmaticвҖқ problem О”О”\Delta-TVC is to cover every edge at least once at every О”О”\Delta consecutive time steps.In both problems, we try to minimize the number of вҖңvertex appearancesвҖқ in the resulting cover, where a vertex appearance is a pair (v,t)рқ‘Јрқ‘Ў(v,t)for some vertex vрқ‘Јv and tвҲҲ{1,2,вҖҰ,T}рқ‘Ў12вҖҰрқ‘Үt\in\{1,2,\ldots,T\}.

TVC and О”О”\Delta-TVC naturally generalize the applications of the static problem Vertex Cover to more dynamic inputs, especially in the areas of wireless ad hoc networks, as well as network security and scheduling.In the case of a static graph, the vertex cover can contain trusted vertices which have the ability to monitor/surveil all transmissionsВ [25, 37] or all link failuresВ [26] between any pair of vertices through the edges of the graph. In the temporal setting, it makes sense to monitor the transmissions and to check for link failures within every sliding time window of an appropriate length О”О”\Delta (which is exactly modeled by О”О”\Delta-TVC).

It is already known that both TVC and О”О”\Delta-TVC are NP-hard; for О”О”\Delta-TVCthis is even the case when О”=2О”2\Delta=2 and the minimum degree of theunderlying graph GрқҗәG is just 3В [4]. One of the most intriguing questions left open (see Problem 1 inВ [4]) iswhether О”О”\Delta-TVC (or, more generally, Sliding-Window Temporal Vertex Cover) can be solved in polynomial timeon always degree at most 222 temporal graphs, that is, on temporal graphs where the maximum degree of the graph at each time step is at most 2.

Our Contribution.In this paper we initiate the study of the complexity of TVC and О”О”\Delta-TVC on sparse graphs.Our main result (seeВ SectionВ 3.1) is that, for every О”вүҘ2О”2\Delta\geq 2, О”О”\Delta-TVC is NP-hard even when GрқҗәG is a path or a cycle, while TVC can besolved in polynomial time on paths and cycles. This resolves the first open question (Problem 1) ofВ [4].In contrast, we show that TVC (seeВ SectionВ 3.2) can be solved in polynomial time on temporal paths and cycles.Moreover, for any О”вүҘ2О”2\Delta\geq 2, we provide a Polynomial-Time Approximation Scheme (PTAS) for О”О”\Delta-TVC on temporal paths and cycles (seeВ SectionВ 3.2), which also complements our hardness result for paths.

The \NP-hardness of SectionВ 3.1 signifies that an optimum solution for О”О”\Delta-TVC is hard to compute, even for О”=2О”2\Delta=2 and under severe degree restrictions of the input instance.To counter this hardness for more general temporal graphs than those with underlying paths and cycles as in SectionВ 3, in SectionВ 4 we give three algorithms for every О”вүҘ2О”2\Delta\geq 2:First we present an exact algorithm for О”О”\Delta-TVC withexponential running time dependency on the number of edges in the underlying graph(see SectionВ 4.1).Using this algorithm we are able to devise, for any dвүҘ3рқ‘‘3d\geq 3, a polynomial-time (dвҲ’1)рқ‘‘1(d-1)-approximation (see SectionВ 4.2), where dрқ‘‘d is the maximum vertex degree in any time step, i.вҖүe.,В in any part of the temporal graph that is active at the same time.This improves the currently best known dрқ‘‘d-approximation algorithm for О”О”\Delta-TVCВ [4] and thus also answers another open question (Problem 2 inВ [4]).Finally, we present a simple fixed-parameter tractable algorithm with respect to the size of an optimum solution (see SectionВ 4.3).
