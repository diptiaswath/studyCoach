Targeted aspect-based sentiment analysis (TABSA) aims at detecting aspects according to the specific target and inferring sentiment polarities corresponding to different target-aspect pairs simultaneouslyВ Saeidi etВ al. (2016). For example, in sentence вҖңlocation1 is your best bet for secure although expensive and location2 is too far.вҖқ, for target вҖңlocation1вҖқ, the sentiment polarity is positive towards aspect вҖңSAFETYвҖқ but is negative towards aspect вҖңPRICEвҖқ. While вҖңlocation2вҖқ only express negative polarity about aspect вҖңTRANSIT-LOCATIONвҖқ. This can be seen in Figure 1, e.g., where opinions on the aspects вҖңSAFETYвҖқ and вҖңPRICEвҖқ are expressed for target вҖңlocation1вҖқ but not for target вҖңlocation2вҖқ, whose corresponding aspect is вҖңTRANSIT-LOCATION вҖқ. Here, an interesting phenomenon is that, the opinion вҖңPositiveвҖқ towards aspect вҖңSAFETYвҖқ is expressed for target вҖңlocation1вҖқ will be change if вҖңlocation1вҖқ and вҖңlocation2вҖқ are exchanged. That is to say, the representation of target and aspect should take full account of context information rather than use context-independent representation.

Aspect-based sentiment analysis (ABSA) is a basic subtask of TABSA, which aims at inferring the sentiment polarities of different aspects in the sentenceВ Ruder etВ al. (2016); Chen etВ al. (2017); Gui etВ al. (2017); Peng etВ al. (2018); Ma etВ al. (2018a). Recently, attention-based neural models achieve remarkable success in ABSAВ Fan etВ al. (2018); Wang etВ al. (2016); Tang etВ al. (2016). In TABSA task, the attention-based sentiment LSTMВ Ma etВ al. (2018b) is proposed to tackle the challenges of both aspect-based sentiment analysis and targeted sentiment analysis by incorporating external knowledge. For neural model improvement, a delayed memory is proposed to track and update the states of targets at the right time with external memoryВ Liu etВ al. (2018).

Despite the remarkable progress made by the previous works, they usually utilize context-independent or randomly initialized vectors to represent targets and aspects, which losses the semantic information and ignores the interdependence among specific target, corresponding aspects and the context. Because the targets themselves have no expression of sentiment, and the opinions of the given sentence are generally composed of words highly correlative to the targets. For example, if the words вҖңpriceвҖқ and вҖңexpensiveвҖқ are in the sentence, it probably expresses the вҖңNegativeвҖқ sentiment polarity about aspect вҖңPRICEвҖқ.

To alleviate these problems above, we propose a novel embedding refinement method to obtain context-aware embedding for TABSA. Specifically, we use a sparse coefficient vector to select highly correlated words from the sentence, and then adjust the representations of target and aspect to make them more valuable. The main contributions of our work can be summarized as follows:вҖўWe reconstruct the vector representation for target from the context by means of a sparse coefficient vector, hence the representation of target is generated from highly correlative words rather than using context-independent or randomly initialized embedding.вҖўThe aspect embedding is fine-tuned to be close to the highly correlated target and be away from the irrelevant targets.вҖўExperiment results on SentiHood and Semeval 2015 show that our proposed method can be directly incorporated into embedding-based TABSA models and achieve state-of-the-art performance.

This section evaluates several deep neural models based on our proposed embedding refinement method for TABSA.

Two benchmark datasets: SentiHoodВ Saeidi etВ al. (2016) and Task 12 of Semeval 2015В Pontiki etВ al. (2015) are used to evaluate our proposed method. SentiHood contains annotated sentences containing one or two location target mentions. The whole dataset contains 5215 sentences with 3862 sentences containing a single location and 1353 sentences containing multiple (two) locations. Location target names are masked by рқҗӢрқҗҺрқҗӮрқҗҖрқҗ“рқҗҲрқҗҺрқҗҚрқҹҸрқҗӢрқҗҺрқҗӮрқҗҖрқҗ“рқҗҲрқҗҺрқҗҚрқҹҸ\bf LOCATION1 and рқҗӢрқҗҺрқҗӮрқҗҖрқҗ“рқҗҲрқҗҺрқҗҚрқҹҗрқҗӢрқҗҺрқҗӮрқҗҖрқҗ“рқҗҲрқҗҺрқҗҚрқҹҗ\bf LOCATION2 in the whole dataset. Following В Saeidi etВ al. (2016), we only consider the top 4 aspects (вҖңGENERALвҖқ, вҖңPRICEвҖқ, вҖңTRANSIT-LOCATIONвҖқ and вҖңSAFETYвҖқ) when evaluate aspect detection and sentiment classification. To show the generalizability of our method, we evaluate our works in another dataset: restaurants domain in Task 12 for TABSA from Semeval 2015. We remove sentences containing no targets as well as NULL targets like the work ofВ Ma etВ al. (2018b). The whole dataset contains 1,197 targets in the training set and 542 targets in the testing set.

We use GloveВ Pennington etВ al. (2014)111http://nlp.stanford.edu/projects/glove/ to initialize the word embeddings in our experiments, and target embeddings (location1 and location2) are randomly initialized. We initialize рқҗ–рқҗ–\bf W and рқҗӣрқҗӣ\bf b with random initialization. The parameters of cрқ‘җc, Оұрқӣј\alpha and ОІрқӣҪ\beta in our experiment are 4, 1 and 0.5 respectively. Given a unit of text sрқ‘ s, a list of labels (t,a,p)рқ‘Ўрқ‘Һрқ‘қ(t,a,p) is provided correspondingly, the overall task of TABSA can be defined as a three-class classification task for each (t,a)рқ‘Ўрқ‘Һ(t,a) pair with labels Positive, Negative, None. We use macro-average F1, Strict accuracy (Acc.) and AUC for aspect detection, and Acc. and AUC for sentiment classification ignoring the class of None, which indicates that a sentence does not contain an opinion for the target-aspect pair (t,a)рқ‘Ўрқ‘Һ(t,a).

We compare our method with several typical baseline systemsВ Saeidi etВ al. (2016) and remarkable modelsВ Ma etВ al. (2018b); Liu etВ al. (2018) proposed for the task of TABSA.

(1) LSTM-FinalВ Saeidi etВ al. (2016): A bidirectional LSTM model takes the final states to represent the information.

(2) LSTM-LocВ Saeidi etВ al. (2016): A bidirectional LSTM model takes the output representation at the index corresponding to the location target.

(3) SenticLSTMВ Ma etВ al. (2018b): A bidirectional LSTM model incorporates external SenticNet knowledge.

(4) Delayed-memoryВ Liu etВ al. (2018): A memory-based model utilizes a delayed memory mechanism.

(5) RE+SenticLSTM: Incorporating our proposed method into SenticLSTM.

(6) RE+Delayed-memory: Incorporating our proposed method into Delayed-memory.

The comprehensive results show that by incorporating refined context-aware embeddings of targets and aspects into the neural models can substantially improve the performance of aspect detection. This indicates that the refined representation is more learnable and is able to extract the interdependence between aspect and the corresponding target in the context. On the other hand, the performance of sentiment classification is improved certainly in comparison with the remarkable models (Delayed-memory and SenticLSTM). It indicates that our context-aware embeddings can capture sentiment information better than the models using traditional embeddings even incorporating external knowledge.

The results verify that encoding aspect by leveraging context information is more effective for aspect detection and sentiment classification in TABSA task.

In this paper, we proposed a novel method for refining representations of targets and aspects. The proposed method is able to select a set of highly correlated words from the context via a sparse coefficient vector and then adjust the representations of targets and aspects. Hence, the interdependence among specific target, corresponding aspect, and the context can be extracted to generate superior embedding. Experimental results demonstrated the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed method on two benchmark datasets over the task of TABSA. In future works, we will explore the extension of this approach for other tasks.
