Recommendation systems are essential in addressing information overload by filtering unintended information and have benefited many high-tech companies (Chen et al., 2020a). Bayesian Personalized Ranking (BPR) (Rendle et al., 2009) is a common choice for learning recommender models from implicit feedback, which randomly draws negative items for the sake of efficiency and approximately optimizes the AUC metric. However, uniformly sampled negative items may not be informative, contributing little to the gradients and the convergence (Rendle and Freudenthaler, 2014; Zhang et al., 2013). To overcome this obstacle, researchers have proposed many Hard Negative Sampling (HNS) methods, such as Dynamic Negative Sampling (DNS) (Zhang et al., 2013) and Softmax-based Sampling methods (Wang et al., 2017; Lian et al., 2020; Chen et al., 2022a). Superior to uniform sampling, HNS methods oversample high-scored negative items, which are more informative with large gradients and thus accelerate the convergence (Chen et al., 2021).

While existing work usually attributes the superior performance of HNS to its better convergence, we find that the merits of HNS are beyond this thought.To validate it, we conduct empirical analyses on three widely used datasets in Figure 1. We compare two HNS strategies with a strong baseline named Non-Sampling111All compared methods optimize the same loss of BPR. (Chen et al., 2022b) that computes the gradient over the whole data (including all negative items). As such, the Non-Sampling strategy is supposed to converge to a better optimum more stably (Chen et al., 2020c, b; He et al., 2016; Bayer et al., 2017).Nevertheless, to our surprise, both HNS strategies substantially outperform the Non-Sampling strategy. It indicates that fast convergence may not be the only justification for the impressive performance of HNS. There must be other reasons for its superior performance, which motivates us to delve into HNS and explore its theoretical foundation. Our findings are twofold:

•Optimizing the BPR loss equipped with HNS is equivalent to optimizing the One-way Partial AUC (OPAUC), whereas the original BPR loss only optimizes AUC. OPAUC puts a restriction on the range of false positive rate (FPR) ∈[0,β]absent0𝛽\in[0,\beta] (Dodd and Pepe, 2003), as shown in Figure 2(b), which emphasizes the ranking of top-ranked negative items. In contrast, AUC is a special case of OPAUC(β𝛽\beta) with β=1𝛽1\beta=1, which considers the whole ranking list. Our proof of the equivalence is based on the Distributionally Robust Optimization (DRO) framework (Rahimian and Mehrotra, 2019) (cf. Section 3).•Compared to AUC, OPAUC has a stronger connection with Top-K𝐾K metrics. To illustrate it, we conduct simulation studies with randomly generated ranking lists, showing that OPAUC exhibits a much higher correlation with Top-K𝐾K metrics like Recall, Precision and NDCG by tuning β𝛽\beta (cf. Figure 6). This is because both OPAUC and Top-K𝐾K metrics care more about the ranking of top-ranked items, as shown in Figure 3. Furthermore, we confirm the correlation through theoretical analysis that Recall@K𝐾K and Precision@K𝐾K metrics could be higher and lower bounded with a function of specific OPAUC(β𝛽\beta), respectively.

In short, our analyses reveal that equipping BPR with HNS is equivalent to optimizing the OPAUC, leading to better Top-K𝐾K recommendation performance (cf. Figure 4).Our analyses not only explain the impressive performance of HNS but also shed light on how to perform HNS in recommendation.Given the correspondence between Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics and OPAUC(β𝛽\beta), we offer two instructive guidelines to ensure the practical effectiveness of HNS.First, the sampling hardness should be controllable, e.g., via pre-defined hyper-parameters, to adapt to different Top-K𝐾K metrics and datasets.Second, the smaller the K𝐾K we emphasize in Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics, the harder the negative samples we should draw.

The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:•We are the first to establish the theoretical foundations for HNS: equipping BPR with DNS is an exact estimator of the OPAUC objective, and with softmax-based sampling is a soft estimator.•We conduct theoretical analyses, simulation studies, and real-world experiments, to justify the connection between OPAUC and Top-K𝐾K metrics and explain the performance gain of HNS.•We provide two crucial guidelines on how to perform HNS and adjust sampling hardness. The experiments on real-world datasets validate the rationality of the guidelines.

In this section, we investigate the connection between OPAUC(β𝛽\beta) and Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics, which is the second step to understanding the effectiveness of HNS. We propose two arguments to declare their relationship:

(1)Compared to AUC, OPAUC(β𝛽\beta) has a stronger correlation with Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics.(2)A smaller K𝐾K in Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics has a stronger correlation with a smaller β𝛽\beta in OPAUC(β𝛽\beta).We conduct theoretical analysis and simulation experiments to verify our proposals as follows.

In this subsection, we analyze the connection between OPAUC(β𝛽\beta) and Top-K𝐾K metrics from a theoretical perspective. To be concrete, we prove that given K𝐾K, Precision@K𝐾K and Recall@K𝐾K are higher bounded and lower bounded by the functions of specific OPAUC(β𝛽\beta).

Remark: From above, we get the following inspirations:(1)The Top-K𝐾K metrics like Precision@K𝐾K and Recall@K𝐾K have a strong connection with specific OPAUC(β𝛽\beta), where β=KN−𝛽𝐾subscript𝑁\beta=\frac{K}{N_{-}}. Early work sample items based on predefined distributions, e.g., uniform distribution (Ding et al., 2019; Rendle et al., 2009) and popularity-based distribution (Caselles-Dupré et al., 2018; Chen et al., 2017). These static samplers are independent of model status and unchanged for different users. Thus, the performance is limited. Later on, adaptive samplers are proposed, such as DNS (Zhang et al., 2013) and softmax-based sampling methods. Softmax-based sampling is widely used in adversarial learning (e.g. IRGAN (Wang et al., 2017) and ADVIR (Park and Chang, 2019)) and importance sampling (e.g. PRIS (Lian et al., 2020) and AdaSIR (Chen et al., 2022a)). They assign high sampling probability to top-ranked negative items, accounting for model status. There are also some fine-grained negative sampling methods (Zhu et al., 2022b; Wan et al., 2022; Mao et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2020). Empirical experiments verify the effectiveness and efficiency of HNS. The efficiency problem has been studied in AOBPR (Rendle and Freudenthaler, 2014). They argue that HNS samples more informative high-scored items, which can contribute more to the gradients and accelerate the convergence. Nevertheless, the reasons for the effectiveness of HNS are not revealed yet. To the best of our knowledge, only DNS (Zhang et al., 2013) provides clues of the connection between HNS and Top-K𝐾K metrics. But unfortunately, they fail to give a theoretical foundation and deep analyses.

Early work does not directly optimize the surrogate objective of Partial AUC, but instead, some other related objectives, e.g., p-norm push (Rudin, 2009), infinite-push (Li et al., 2014), and asymmetric SVM objective (Wu et al., 2008). Nevertheless, these algorithms are not scalable and applicable to deep learning. More recently, (Yang et al., 2021) considers two-way partial AUC maximization and simplifies the optimizing problem for large scale optimization. (Zhu et al., 2022a) proposes new formulations of Partial AUC surrogate objectives using distributionally robust optimization (DRO). This work motivates our proof of the connection between OPAUC and HNS. A more comprehensive study of AUC can refer to (Yang and Ying, 2022).

In this paper, we reveal the theories behind HNS for recommendation. We prove that the BPR loss equipped with HNS strategies optimizes OPAUC. Meanwhile, we conduct theoretical analysis and simulation experiments to show the strong connection between OPAUC and Top-K𝐾K evaluation metrics. On these bases, the effectiveness of HNS can be clearly explained. To take a step further, we propose two insightful guidelines for effective usage of HNS.In conclusion, the proposed theoretical understanding of HNS can not only explain effectiveness but also provide insightful guidelines for future study.

In the future, we will investigate the connection between Two-way Partial AUC and recommendation algorithms, which may bring more insights into recommendation systems. The effect of false negative items will also be an exciting research direction.
