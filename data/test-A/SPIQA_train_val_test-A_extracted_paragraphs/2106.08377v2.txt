We study the Stochastic Shortest Path (SSP) model, where an agent aims to reach a goal state with minimum cost in a stochastic environment.SSP is well-suited for modeling many real-world applications, such as robotic manipulation, car navigation, and others.Although it is widely studied empirically (e.g.,В (Andrychowicz etВ al., 2017; Nasiriany etВ al., 2019)) and in optimal control theory (e.g.,В (Bertsekas and Tsitsiklis, 1991; Bertsekas and Yu, 2013)), it has received less attention under the regret minimization setting where a learner needs to learn the environment and improve her policy on-the-fly through repeated interaction.Specifically, the problem proceeds in KрқҗҫK episodes.In each episode, the learner starts at a fixed initial state, sequentially takes action, suffers some cost, and transits to the next state, until reaching a predefined goal state.The performance of the learner is measured by her regret, which is the difference between her total costs and that of the best policy.

Our main technical contribution is a new analysis framework called implicit finite-horizon approximation (SectionВ 3), which is the key to analyze algorithms developed from our template.The high level idea is to approximate an SSP instance by a finite-horizon counterpart.However, the approximation only happens in the analysis, a key difference compared toВ (Chen etВ al., 2021; Chen and Luo, 2021; Cohen etВ al., 2021) that explicitly implement such an approximation in their algorithms.As a result, our method not only avoids blowing up the space complexity by a factor of the horizon, but also allows one to derive a horizon-free regret bound (more explanation to follow).

In order to achieve the minimax optimal regret, our model-free algorithm LCB-Advantage-SSP uses a key variance reduction idea via a reference-advantage decomposition byВ (Zhang etВ al., 2020b).However, crucial distinctions exist.For example, we update the reference value function more frequently instead of only one time, which helps reduce the sample complexity and improve the lower-order term in the regret bound.We also maintain an empirical upper bound on the value function in a doubling manner, which is the key to eventually make the algorithm parameter-free.On the other hand, for our model-based algorithm SVI-SSP, we adopt a special Bernstein-style bonus term and bound the learnerвҖҷs total variance via recursion, taking inspiration fromВ (Tarbouriech etВ al., 2021b; Zhang etВ al., 2020a).

We support our theoretical findings with experiments in AppendixВ H.Our model-free algorithm demonstrates a better convergence rate compared to vanilla Q learning with naive Пөitalic-Пө\epsilon-greedy exploration.Our model-based algorithm has competitive performance compared to other model-based algorithms, while spending the least amount of time in updates.
